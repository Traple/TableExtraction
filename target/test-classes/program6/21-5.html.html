3.3. Eﬂect of pH on the activity of immobilized SCAD

The pH dependence of the free and the immobilized SCAD activities at pH ranging from 5 to 8 at 25 0C was studied (Fig. 5). The free and immobilized enzymes showed much different behaviors of pH dependence. From Fig. 5, the maximum activ- ity for the free SCAD can be clearly seen at pH 6.8 and the optimum pH of the immobilized form is found at pH 7.5. From pH 6.8 to 8.0, the relative activity of immobilized enzyme kept high activity, While the native enzyme was more sensitive than immobilized enzyme in the range from pH 7.0 to 8. It was pre- sumed that the conﬁguration of SCAD was ﬁxed on the surface of carriers and led to increase the enzyme’s tolerability to the pH variance in surroundings, or the pH of the carriers inner was changed because of the surface charge of the carriers. It would be ideal if immobilized enzymes could be functional at a Wide range of pH values for many practical applications.

3.5. Activity and kinetic parameters

In enzyme catalysis it is important to quantify the efﬁ- ciency of enzyme (either supported or unsupported) in terms of a number of kinetic parameters. Typically, the ‘maximum velocity’, Vmax, represents the maximum rate of an enzymatic reaction with enzyme active sites saturated with substrate and Michaelis-Menten constant, Km represents substrate concentra- tion, [S], at Which the rate of reaction is one half its maximum are collected. Comparison of these parameters will reﬂect the efﬁciency and kinetics of enzyme at different environments. Thus, kinetic analysis of free and immobilized SCAD on mag- netic Fe3O4-chitosan nanoparticles at 25 0C Was carried out. The apparent Michaelis-Menten constant for free and immobi- lized SCAD enzyme were determined from double-reciprocal plots showing 1/v vs. 1/[S] (Fig. 7).
