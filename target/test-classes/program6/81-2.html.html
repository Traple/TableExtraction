Metal Exchange in Solution Using Anaerobic Column Chromatography

A system of two chromatographic columns sup- ported with Sephadex G-25 ﬁne was set up operating on-line in combination with a column eluate concen- trator (CEC 1, Amicon, Witten/Ruhr) and an optical monitor (Isco, Lincoln/Nebraska). The ﬁrst column was equilibrated with 0.1 M sodium acetate at pH 5.5, 0.1 M in cobaltous acetate and 0.1 M in sodium chloride. On passing the enzyme through the ﬁrst column the zinc was replaced by cobalt and simul- taneously separated from the protein. The protein band of the eluate was concentrated on-line and passed through the second column which was equilibrated with 25 mM Mes/Nal“ pH 6.1. In this step the excess of cobalt was separated from the protein solution, the pH raised and the ionic strength lowered. The eluate from the second column containing the protein was concentrated on-line and then stored in Schlenk tubes. Both columns were thermostatted. All solutions were kept oxygen-free and the column reservoirs were con- tinuously flushed with nitrogen (99.995 ‘X,).

Metal Exchange in Crystal Suspension

Crystallization. Horse liver alcohol dehydrogenase was crystallized by equilibrium dialysis either at pH 8.4 using 2-methyl-2,4-pentanediol as precipitant as de- scribed previously [32] or at pH 6.9 in 0.05 M Tes/Na+ with 25 ‘X, (v/v) tert-butanol as precipitant.

Removal of Catalytic Zinc from Crystals of Horse Liver Alcohol Dehydrogenase. The crystal suspension was dialyzed exhaustively against the mother liquor 5 mM in chelating agent until the speciﬁc activity of aliquots drawn from the batch had decreased to less than 0.5 ‘X,. Then the suspension was freed from the chelating agent by subsequent dialysis against the original buffer containing 25 ‘X, (v/v) tert-butanol. The buffers, the concentrations of the chelating agents and the time required for the operations are given under Results. The operations were performed under nitrogen in Schlenk tubes at 4 “C.

Insertion of Metal Ions in Crystalline Zinc-Depleted Enzyme. The suspension of crystals treated with chelating agents was dialyzed for 12 h against a buffer not more than 5 mM in cobaltous or zinc acetate. The total ionic strength of the buffer was maintained the same as during the crystallization by appropriate reduction of the concentration of the buffer substance. The concentration of the precipitant was kept at 25 ‘X,

(v/v) and excess of cobalt was removed by repeated dialysis.

Metal Insertion in Solution

To reactivate the zinc-depleted enzyme in solution, the crystals were dissolved in 0.05 M Tes/Na+ pH 6.9. The solution was then dialyzed for 20 h against 0.025 M Tes/Na* pH 6.9, 20 uM in zinc acetate or 1mM in cobaltous acetate. Finally, the reactivated enzyme was dialyzed against metal-free 0.05 M Tes/ Na+ of pH 6.9. All operations were performed under nitrogen at 4 “C.

Metal Exchange in Sepharose-4B-Bound Horse Liver Alcohol Dehydrogenase

The immobilised cobalt-zinc hybrid enzyme substi- tuted at the noncatalytic sites was prepared as de- scribed previously [33]. For the preparation of the active-site-substituted hybrid enzyme, a procedure similar to that used for the crystal was adopted. Immobilised enzyme (5 mg/ml packed gel) was ﬁlled into a column. The reagents used for the metal extraction and insertion (see above) were pumped through the column instead of using a dialysis proce- dure. For monitoring the decrease in activity, aliquots were taken from the column and analyzed as described [33]. The decrease in the zinc content was followed through analysis of the column eluate by atomic ab- sorption spectrophotometry. The whole preparation was done under nitrogen.

Preparation of the ‘Green Hybrid’ Horse Liver Alcohol Dehydrogenase: Zn(c)2Co(n)2-enzyme

Dialysis for short periods against buﬁers of pH 5.5 containing cobaltous ions yields a green enzyme con- taining about equal amounts of cobalt and zinc [1 - 3]. This species was obtained quickly in the column system described above. It was found that working at low temperature (4 OC) and providing a rapid passage through the ﬁrst column (max 4 h) yielded exclusively the ‘green hybrid’ which is characterized by essentially unchanged speciﬁc activity. This constancy of speciﬁc activity is taken as one proof that the noncatalytic zinc ions have been exchanged [1 — 3]. We agree with this interpretation and give below further evidence for it.
