Materials and methods Patients

The protocol was approved by the Human Care Committee of the Medical University in Bialystok, Poland (Approval No. R-I-002/47/2007). All patients gave an informed con- sent for the examination.

Biopsy specimens of liver healthy tissues and cancer tis- sues (80—100 mg) were taken during routine surgical operation for treatment of hepatocellular carcinomas from 44 patients (26 males and 18 females, range 34—75 years) (Table 1). Using the TNM data obtained from tumor and lymph node status, the patients were categorized according to American Joint Committee on Cancer Classiﬁcation (AJ CC). Seventeen patients had primary liver tumors and 27 had metastatic liver tumors. The histological study indicated that all patients were diagnosed as adenocarcinoma. Metastatic liver tumors originated from the pancreas (eleven), intestine (eight), stomach (ﬁve), kidneys (two) and in the lungs (one).

For the analysis patients were divided into two groups: moderate drinkers (12 men with mean alcohol intake of less than 20 g/day and 7 women with mean alcohol intake of less than 10 g/day) and heavy drinkers (14 men with mean alcohol intake of more than 20 g/day and 11 women with mean alcohol intake of more than 10 g/day). Before the examinations all patients had not consumed alcohol for almost 4 months.

Material

All specimens were frozen at —80°C until analysis within 2 weeks. The liver tissue (l:4 w/v) was homogenized in potassium phosphate buffer (0.1 mol/l; pH 7.4) for 20 s using an ultrasonicator (Sonoplus HD 70; Bandelin), centrifuged at 14,000 rpm for 30 n1in, and cooled to 4°C. The supernatant was used to determine ADH and ALDH activities.

Biochemical assays Determination 0f total ADH activity

Total ADH activity was estimated by the photometric method with p-nitrosodimethylaniline (NDMA) as sub- strate [6]. The reaction mixture (2 ml) contained supernatant (0.1 ml), 1.8 ml of a 26 umoUl solution of substrate in 0.1 mol/l sodium phosphate buffer, pH 8.5, and 0.1 ml of mixture containing 0.25 mol/l n-butanol and 5 mmoUl NAD. The reduction of NDMA was monitored at 440 nm on a Shimadzu UV/VIS 1202 spectrophotometer (Shimadzu Europa, Duisburg, Germany).
