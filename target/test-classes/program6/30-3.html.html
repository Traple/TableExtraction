Fig. 1. Stereoview of the active site residues and the bound water molecule. Non-hydrogen atoms are illustrated by red spheres (oxygen), blue spheres (nitrogen), and gray spheres (carbon). H-bonds are represented by green lines connecting the corresponding non-hydrogen atoms. Hydrogen atoms are illustrated by cyan spheres, the size of which is proportional to their population of state. A: The hydrogen atom positions whose population is calculated as a function of pH are illustrated by hollow spheres. The reference positions are connected to the corresponding non-hydrogen atoms. B: Low pH: “closed” state of the proton relay. The orientation of the NADT ribose O2’ hydroxyl group at which the hydrogen is not involved in hydrogen bonding with Tyr151 is mostly populated (0.7). Lys155 s-amino group is a proton donor rather to the NADT ribose O3’ hydroxyl group. C: High pH: “ready” state of the proton relay. The NADT ribose hydroxyl is proton donor to Tyr151 and Lys155 with equal probability. In the protonated form, Tyr151 is proton donor to the NADT ribose O2’ hydroxyl group. Both Tyr151 and Lys155 are half deprotonated. Possible protonation of Tyr151 by abstraction of a proton from the substrate hydroxyl group shifts the proton toward the NADT ribose O2’ and further to Lys155. The proton relay chain is completed.
