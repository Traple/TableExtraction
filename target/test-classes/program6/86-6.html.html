Reliability considerations

The measured enantiomeric-product composition of the 2-butanone reduction has not been used in the calculation of rate constants shown above. They have been exclusively used to relate the experimentally obtained rate constants to the production of (S)-2-butanol. The subsequent calculations, and thus the calculated rate constants themselves, are absolutely independent of the quantitative results (i.e. the analytically determined percentage product composition) of this exper- iment. Calculation of the enantiorneric composition from the kinetic parameters can be regarded as a test of the reliability of the calculation procedure.

Using k0,, and Km and either experimental data for (S)-2- butanol or calculated data for (R)-2-butanol production, it is possible to calculate the expected enantiomer composition according to Eqn (14).

A second way to test these data is to simulate the whole reaction with an appropriate simulation program according to Fig. 1, using the rate constants obtained from the calculations (Tables 3 and 4). In Table 5, the measured product compo- sitions are compared with the corresponding data obtained from (km/Km) and the data obtained from simulation with ChemSim for Zn(II)LADH and Co(II)LADH.

The experimental and calculated data are in good agree- ment. The identity of the simulated data and the data obtained with the apparent catalytic efficiencies, with respect to the different pathways of 2-butanone reduction, emphasises both the signiﬁcance of k,,,/Km for discriminating competing sub- strates or competing pathways of substrate metabolism as well as the reliability of the calculated rate constants of the ordered bi-bi system.

The program ChemSim was also used to simulate steady- state and single-turnover experiments. The simulated data were found to be in good agreement with those obtained exper- imentally. The speciﬁcations of the program ChemSim and a more detailed description of kinetic simulations will be published elsewhere. DISCUSSION

The reaction system (S)-2-butanol, (R)-2-butanol/2- butanone is the ﬁrst example of a LADH-catalyzed reaction for which the stereochemistry can be described in terms of the rate constants of the underlying mechanism. The independent tests of the data showed results which differ only in the range of experimental error.
