Structural zinc ion

The second zinc ion is positioned in a surface loop of the catalytic domain. This zinc ion, which is presumed to have a structural role, is present in the crystal structures of mammalian type I ADHs, and in the archaeal S. solfataricus ADH. Bacterial ADHs contain only one, catalytic, zinc ion per monomer. The structural zinc-binding site in the A. pernix ADH is homologous to those described previously, though with differences in the coordi- nating residues. Horse liver ADH and the human ADHs coordinate the zinc ion with four cysteine residues, while S. solfataricus ADH has three cysteine residues and one glutamic acid residue. The A. pernix ADH zinc ion is coordinated by three cysteine residues (Cys112, Cys115 and Cys123) and an aspartic acid residue (Asp109).

In the current A. pernix ADH structure, the struc- tural zinc ion is present at only partial occupancy in both monomers of the asymmetric unit. In its absence, Cys123 moves into a second position, distorting the tetrahedral geometry, and forms a disulfide bond with Cys115 (Figure 8). In the presence of the zinc ion, othe distance between Cys115 and Cys123 is 3.3 A, while in its absence this is reduced to 2 A.
