Some authors note that serum total ADH activity is an indicator of intrahepatic cholestasis (7,8). Moreover, the activity of ADH and its isoenzymes in the serum may be primarily elevated during liver cell injury, e. g., cirrhosis, viral hepatitis, and alcoholism (9,l0). In preliminary, still unpublished studies, we have shown that ADH and its isoenzymes and ALDH activities are present in the liver cancer cells. In addition, the activities of total ADH, ALDH, and class I ADH were signiÔ¨Åcantly higher in cancer cells than in healthy tissues. The aim of this study was to investigate the activity of ADH isoenzymes and ALDH in the sera of patients with liver cancer.
