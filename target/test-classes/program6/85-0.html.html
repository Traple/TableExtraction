Mammalian alcohol dehydrogenases usually contain two zinc atoms per subunit, one of which is essential for catalytic activity [1]. The amino acid ligands of both metal atoms are conserved. In human class I and horse liver alcohol dehydro- genase the active-site zinc atom is surrounded in the ﬁrst coordination shell by cysteine-46, cysteine-176, histidine-o? and one water or substrate molecule [2, 3]. The three amino acid ligands stem from rather distant loci along the polypep- tide chain. The structure of this metal center is predominantly shaped by the protein as shown by the crystal structure of the active-site metal-depleted enzyme species of horse liver alcohol dehydrogenase [4], which is essentially the same as that of the native enzyme. However, the preparation of the zinc-depleted enzyme and its reconstitution with Zn(ll) or other divalent metal ions depend on the preservation of the tertiary/quaternary structure of the protein which is stabilized in the crystal lattice as compared to solution [7]. This indicates that the metal ion also plays a structural role and that the performance of removing and replacing the catalytic metal ion may vary with variations in the protein structure as pro- vided by different alcohol dehydrogenase isozymes from horse or human liver. The modiﬁed horse liver alcohol dehydrogen- ases, which were obtained by site-speciﬁc introduction of dif- ferent metals, are useful tools for studying the structure of the metal center in solution as well as for obtaining information related to the catalytic mechanism. Nothing has been known until now about metal exchange in human liver alcohol de- hydrogenase.

Among class l (or, ﬂ 1, [32, y1, yz) human liver alcohol dehydrogenases, 1B 1 is the most abundant subunit in the ma- jority of caucasians; y1 is the one most closely related to the E subunit of horse liver alcohol dehydrogenase [8]. All possible combinations of homodimers and heterodimers are known. The investigations presented in this paper show that 311,81 and y1y1 isozymes are similar to horse liver EE isozyme if the metal extraction is carried out in solution, whereas in the case of B1181 isozyme speciﬁc extraction of active-site zinc in solution and reconstitution by coba1t(II) is possible.
