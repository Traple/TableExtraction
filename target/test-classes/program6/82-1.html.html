RESULTS AND DISCUSSION Steady-State Kinetics

Primary and secondary plots, shown in Fig.1 and 2 respectively, correspond to the initial rate values of the peroxidatic reaction carried out at pH 7. The values of the kinetic parameters obtained from the secondary plots agree within i 10% with those ob- tained from Lineweaver-Burk secondary plots, not reported here; they are: KQAD = 220 i 20 uM, KFEOZ = 100 i l0 mM, km = 290 i 10 min”. The appearance 0f the data obeys a rate law for sequential mechanisms for two substrate enzyme reactions, since the Eadie-Augustinsson-Hofstee primary plots con- verge below the horizontal axis.

It can be noted that both KQAD and kcat are of the same order of magnitude as those found for the reac- tion with ethanol at the same pH (160 uM and 162 min” respectively), which suggests a possible coincidence of the rate-limiting step in both types of reaction, namely dissociation of produced coenzyme, which in our case is not NADH but compound I. Although it is not possible from steady-state kinetics alone to state which substrate adds ﬁrst to the enzyme, it seems to us reasonable to postulate that NADT binds ﬁrst to the enzyme followed by H2O2, in view of the very large difference between their Km values which should reﬂect their very different afﬁnity of binding to the enzyme active site. This fact, however, does not mean that H2O2 cannot bind to the enzyme in the absence of NAD ‘L.
