Correlation between physical stability and enzyme activity

The thermal deactivation of enzymes is of strong interest in the food science industry, especially with regard to a better description of the time course of this phenomenon and understanding its relevant mechanism [54]. As discussed above, our results indicate that the reduction in ADH aggregation under thermal stress with the use of oz-CyD coincided with the thermal structural stability. lt is well known that the activity of an enzyme is strongly dependent on its conformational integrity [55]. To prove this, we analysed its effect on the residual enzyme activity at constant higher temperature (50°C), where aggregation took place after 3 min (see Figure 3). The enzyme activity assay is a very competent method for acquiring data with reference to the kinetic stability, since it can provide quantitative information.
