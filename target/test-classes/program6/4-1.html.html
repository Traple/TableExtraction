Fig.2. Schematic drawing showing the conformational change of the enzyme

The domains in each subunit rotate against each other. The catalytic domains are at each end of the dimeric molecule. The catalytic domain rotates 10° around an axis through the domain, while the coenzyme-binding domain rotates 1.5° through an axis approximately across the pleated sheet strands. The coenzyme parts covered by the domain are drawn with dashed lines.

The rotation of the catalytic domain is made possible by the rearrangement of a loop in the coenzyme domain at the opposite side of the cleft. This loop has to move away to allow the coenzyme-binding domain to rotate. Some atoms in this loop move as much as 9 A between the two conforma- tions. The conformational change of the loop brings in one Val into what is to become the substrate cleft. An important effect of the conformational change is that the water mole- cules present in the cleft between the domains are expelled. The active site in the presence of substrate or inhibitors is free from water molecules.
