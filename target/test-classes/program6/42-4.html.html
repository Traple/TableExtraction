Isotope Effects—A rapid equilibrium mechanism, either or- dered or random, predicted that the central complexes occur as the predominant enzyme species relative to those of free en- zyme and binary complexes. To assess whether conversion of the central complexes acts as a rate-determining step in reduc- tion of retinal, kinetic isotope effect experiments were per- formed with stereospeciﬂcally deuterated coenzyme (4R)[4- 2H]nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide in 0.1 M sodium phosphate, pH 7.5, containing 2% (v/v) acetonitrile and 0.02% (v/v) Tween 80 at 25 °C. For comparison, the isotope effects for acetaldehyde reduction were also measured in the same buffer but in the absence of acetonitrile and Tween 80. The isotope effects for V2 and VZ/Kp for reduction of retinal and acetalde- hyde were determined at 200 and 400 MM reduced coenzyme, respectively, and for VQ/Kq at 60 MM all-trans-retinal or 60 mM acetaldehyde. No significant deuterium isotope effect was ob- served for the maximum velocity with retinal (DV2 = 1.0) or acetaldehyde (DV2 = 1.4) as substrate. Thus hydride transfer appeared not to be rate-limiting in both the reactions. A more pronounced effect was observed for the DVQ/Kp relative to DVz/Kq with acetaldehyde reduction (DVQ/Kp = 2.4 versus DVg/Kq = 1.1) compared with that with retinal reduction (DVZ/Kp = 1.0 versus DVZ/Kq = 0.75).
