A low pKa value of Tyr151 enables and facilitates the abstraction of the hydroxylic proton from the alcohol at physiological pH in the manner of general base catalysis.

Binding of pyrazole, 2-propanol or ethanol to the E-NADJ“ complex is regulated by a group with an apparent pKa of about 7.6 (Winberg 8: McKinley- McKee, 1988b) that can also be assigned to Tyr151. Materials and Methods

Crystallization of DADH binary and ternary complexes

D. lebanonensis wild-type was grown in conventional medium, harvested from large population cages and purified from 100 g wet weight batches as previously described (]uan 8: Gonzalez-Duarte, 1980; Ribas de Pouplana et al., 1991). Two new chromatographic gels, Blue-Sepharose Fast Flow and HR-Sephacryl S-200 replaced the conventional matrices. The purity of the enzyme preparation was exhaustively assessed by SDS- PAGE and amino acid analysis. Contaminants were kept under 1 °/>.

The purified protein was concentrated to about 5 mg / ml and kept in 20 mM Tris-HCl (pH 7.5), 0.1 mM dithio- threitol, 0.01 % sodium azide. The concentration of the protein was determined by a calculated extinction coeffi- cient at 280 nm using the total number of tyrosine, tryptophan and cysteine residues (Gill 8: Hippel, 1989).

Binary (enzyme-coenzyme) and ternary (enzy- me-coenzyme-inhibitor) complexes were prepared by co-crystallization. They were crystallized by the sitting drop vapor-diffusion method as published previously (Ladenstein et al., 1995). The coenzyme (10 mM NAD+ for the binary complex (BIN), 1 mM NAD+ for the tem- ary complexes) was dissolved in the protein solution and 1 % of ketone (acetone (ADA), 3-pentanone (ADO) or cyclohexanone (ADC)) was added to the reservoir sol- ution before the droplets (5-10 ul) were set. The droplets were made by adding 5|.il protein solution (5 mg / ml) to 5ul reservoir solution. Crystallization conditions were similar to those of the apo form of DADH (Benach et al., 1998). Usually only changes in the pH and concentration of the precipitant (PEG) were required. Crystals suitable for X-ray analysis were obtained after several weeks from 28 % (w/v) PEG 2000 (BIN, ADA, ADO) / 20 % PEG 2000 (ADC), 0.2 M CaClz, 0.1 M Tris-HCl pH 7.0 (BIN)/ pH 7.5 (ADA, ADO)/PH 8.0 (ADC) at 40c.

X-ray data collection and processing

Four X-ray diffraction data sets were collected. Data sets to 2.4 A of the binary form of DADH and the tem- ary complex formed with acetone (2.2 A) were collected with a Mar Research imaging plate detector installed on a MAC Science / Siemens rotating anode generator (CuKu). Crystals of the ternary complex formed with 3- pentanone were measured at the X31 beam line at the DORIS storage ring, DESY, Hamburg (Germany) and diffracted beyond 1.4 A (A : 0.9511 A, 1° frames). The ternary complex formed with cyclohexanone diffracted beyond 1.6 A and was measured at the 711 beam line at the Max-lab, Lund (Sweden) (A : 0.9960 A, 1 O frames).
