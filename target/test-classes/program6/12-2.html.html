2. Materials and methods

Recombinant human liver ADH1C*2 was ex- pressed in Escherichia coli and purified by the procedure given in Ref. [20]. ADH1C*2 differs from ADHlC*l at two amino acid residues, which are not in the substrate binding pocket [44]. The turnover number, as determined in a standard enzyme assay [45] and by titration of the active sites with NAD+ in the presence of pyrazole [46], was 0.5 s-l at 25 0C and l.2 s_1 at 37 OC. Recombinant horse liver ADHlS was prepared as described in Ref. [23] and its turnover number was l s_1 at 30 0C. The purified enzymes appeared homogeneous upon electrophoresis in polyacryla- mide gels in the presence of sodium dodecyl sulfate.

Kinetics were studied in 83 mM potassium phosphate, 4O mM KCl buffer, pH 7.3, at 30 or 37 0C in order to mimic physiological conditions. Steroids were solubilized by first dissolving them in acetonitrile and then diluting with the buffer. Final acetonitrile concentrations were 2—4% in the assays, which has been shown to have minimal effects on the activity of the enzyme [22,25]. Initial velocities of steroid oxidation or reduction were determined by spectrophotometric assay using the change in absorbance at 340 nm or with the more sensitive ﬂuorometric method (lg, = 340 nm, 26m =460 nm). Steroid substrates were obtained from Steraloids Inc. or Sigma. Background rates in the presence of enzyme and NAD+ or NADH and in the absence of added substrate were very slow for the data reported, but prevented good measurements with some of the weakly-active

steroids. Kinetic data were fitted with computer programs [47] adapted to run on PCs.
