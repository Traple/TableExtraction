In this context, it was of interest to study the emission ﬂuores- cence properties following the removal of one out of two ﬂuorophores from the enzyme molecule.

A comparison of the emission spectra of wild-type and Trp95Leu mutant is presented in Fig. 3. Following the excitation at 280 nm, the maximum of the mutant emission spectrum is slightly blue-shifted (A/lmax ~ 2 nm) and the intensity is about 15% lower than that of the wild-type enzyme (Fig. 3, curve A and C). The addition of equimolar amounts of NADH to SsADH results in 66% quenching of intrinsic ﬂuorescence, with a 3-nm-blue shift of lmax and a concurrent appearance of a second peak at about 422 nm due to the radiationless energy transfer between the enzyme and reduced nicotinamide ring (Fig. 3, curve B). The indole rings of Trp95 and Trp117 in SsADH lie at distances of approximately 8 and 11 A, respectively, from the coenzyme nicotinamide moiety, hence the high efﬁ- ciency of energy transfer from excited protein to NADH (Giordano et al. 2004). The quenching effect occurs also for the single mutant, although at a lower extent. The addition of 17-fold molar excess of NADH results in 45% quenching of intrinsic ﬂuorescence with a 9-nm-blue shift of lmax and in the appearance of a less intense energy transfer band centered at 430 nm (Fig. 3, curve D). This quenching effect can be ascribed mainly to the energy transfer from the donor indole of Trp117 to the acceptor reduced nicotinamide which, in the mutant coenzyme binding site, are properly oriented as well as within Forster distance for favorable energy transfer (Lakowicz 1999). The spectra obtained by exci- tation at 297 nm display a greater difference in intrinsic ﬂuorescence (Fig. 3, inset). The ﬂuorescence intensity of the mutant enzyme is ~ 50% that of the wild-type enzyme while the lmax is red-shifted by 6 nm. On the basis of wild- type and mutant structures the emission red-shift can be explained by combined effects. Indeed, SsADH emission spectra is composed of two contributions due to Trp95 and Trp117, with the former embedded in a less-polar envi- ronment compared to the latter. Following the removal of Trp95 the emission maximum is shifted toward higher wavelengths. Moreover, Trp117 microenvironment in Trp95Leu mutant is changed as evidenced from the crystal structure obtained. Its indole moiety is sandwiched between Ile120 and Leu95 but many water molecules occupy the additional space left by Trp —> Leu substitu- tion, thus increasing the polarity of Trp117 surroundings (Fig. 4).
