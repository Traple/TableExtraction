Metal analyses revealed the presence of 1.225 and 0.995 mol of Zn“ per mol of ADH I subunit in the absence and presence of EDTA, respectively. The data suggest that each subunit of ADH I contains 1 mole of Zn“ , but ADH II seemed not to contain any speciﬁc metal, at least after dialysis. The effects of metal ions and EDTA on the activity of NAD-depen- dent ADHs were also studied. The activity of ADH II was inhibited around 67% by EDTA treatment, which was done by a dialysis against 5 mM EDTA fol- lowed by an additional dialysis against the buffer to remove EDTA, while ADH I activity was not inhibit- ed at all by the same treatment. Although data are not shown, however, the enzyme activity of ADH I was inhibited 45% by the direct addition of 1II1M EDTA in the reaction mixture. Discussion

In this study we present the puriﬁcation and characterization of two NAD-dependent ADHs from A. pasteurianus CN6-2 mutant strain. Two NAD- dependent ADHs, one with a preference for primary alcohols (ADH I), and the other for secondary alco- hols (ADH II), were detected in ethanol-grown A. pasteurianus CN6-2 mutant strain.
