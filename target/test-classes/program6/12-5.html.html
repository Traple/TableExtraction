Three dimensional structures of alcohol dehy- drogenases complexed with steroids in a produc- tive binding mode have not yet been determined. Thus, we can only speculate at this time that the inhibitory steroids bind with the 3-oxygen bound to the catalytic zinc and with the steroid ring positioned so that hydride transfer does not occur. A reverse binding mode for inhibitors is also possible, with an oxygen at the l7 position bound to the zinc. That 5ot-androstan-l7-ol-3-one lacks detectable activity (in contrast to the activity with the 5B isomer) but is a potent inhibitor suggests that trans A/B ring fusion may allow the steroid to bind in a non-productive mode, whereas steroids with the cis A/B ring structure bind preferentially in a productive mode.

3.3. Inhibition of ADH by an uncompetitive dead- end inhibitor in oioo

As shown in Fig. 2(A), the effects of an uncompetitive inhibitor cannot be completely counteracted by raising the concentration of the varied substrate to saturating levels, in contrast to the effects of a competitive inhibitor, as shown in Fig. 2(B). This characteristic makes uncompetitive inhibitors potentially more effective for therapeu- tic purposes. One reason for studying the speciﬁ- city of steroids as inhibitors of ADHlC is to determine the structural features that produce

good inhibition. Compounds that speciﬁcally in- hibit ADHlC may be useful for evaluating the physiological function of the enzyme and for preventing adverse effects of the activity of the enzyme, for instance, during metabolism of etha- nol where a coupled reaction could accelerate reduction of various carbonyl compounds.

Among the many inhibitors of ADHlC, we found that 3-butylthiolane l-oxide was the best [4l—43]. This compound has a sulfoxide that resembles the 3-keto group of steroids and also results in preferential binding to the enzyme- NADH complex. Thus, the sulfoxide gives un- competitive inhibition against varied concentra- tions of ethanol. 3-Butylthiolane l-oxide is also an excellent inhibitor of horse ADHlE and rat ADHl, class I enzymes that have serine at position 48 [49]. Therefore, evaluation of the inhibition of ethanol metabolism in rats by 3-butylthiolane l- oxide is an important ﬁrst step in the development of specific inhibitors.

The metabolic studies with rats were carried out previously, but new developments in kinetic simu- lation now permit a more detailed, comprehensive analysis of the data.
