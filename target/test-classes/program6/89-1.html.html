An aliquot 0f the library was processed for excising the pBluescript phagemids in the Escherichia coli strain XLl-BlueMRF according to the manufacturer’s instructions (Stratagene, La Jolla, CA). The pro- duced phagemids were used to transform E. coli SOL-R strain and at least 300 colonies were isolated and transferred to nitrocellu- lose ﬁlters. The cells on the ﬁlter papers were disrupted by three cycles of freezing with liquid nitrogen and thawing at room tem- perature. The cellular debris was washed out at 4 °C with a buffer of 5O mM Tris pH 8.0, 5O mM NaCl, 1mM EDTA, and 2O mM B-mercaptoethanol in the presence of 0.1% (v/v) Triton X-100. The ﬁlters treated this way were used to screen for colonies ex- pressing dehydrogenase activities using pyruvate and oxaloacetate with NADH as electron donor and isocitrate, malate, glutamate, propionaldehyde and ethanol with NAD+ as electron acceptor. For oxidoreductase activity detection, NBT+PMS were used as indicators. Using this strategy, a colony was identiﬁed containing a DNA insert with a partial sequence coding for an ADH (nt 510- 1,170 in Fig. 1).

Modeling of the Euglena gracilis alcohol dehydrogenase subunit. A three-dimensional model of the E. gracilis alcohol dehydrogenase (EgADH) subunit was obtained using the SWISS- MODEL software (available at http://swissmodel.expasy.org/) (Schwede et al. 2003). The template used to obtain the model was the structure of the NAD+—ADH of Pseudomonas aeruginosa (lLLU). Analysis of the model and drawing of the ﬁgures was carried out with PyMOL (http://pymol.sourceforge.net/).

The ampliﬁed product was cloned into the pGEM-T easy vector (Promega, Madison, WI) and sequenced. The EgADH gene ﬂanked by the NdeI and HindIII restriction sites was further subcloned into the E. coli expression vector pET28 (Novagen, Madison, WI). The resulting plasmid pET28-EgADH was re-sequenced to conﬁrm the gene insertion in the proper reading frame. The plasmid was used to transform E. coli BL21DE3pLysS cells (Novagen). Positive cells over- expressing the EgADH (39 kDa including a his-tail tag at its amino terminus) after induction with 0.4 mM IPTG were moni- tored through SDS-PAGE.
