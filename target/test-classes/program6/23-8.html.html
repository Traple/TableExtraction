4. Discussion

In the present study combined with a preceding investigation (Kollock et al., 2008) we have shown that four human ADH forms (ADHlC, ADH2, ADH3, ADH4) are able to efﬁciently oxidise benzylic alcohols of alkylpyrenes and thus compete with their SULT-mediated toxiﬁcation. The three members of the ADH1 subfamily found in humans only evolved after the humarﬂrodent phylogenetic branching and show high identity of the amino acid sequence (396%). They show functional similarity, as ethanol is a good substrate for all forms. There- fore, it is possible that ADH1A and ADH1B — like ADH1C — may metabolise some alkylpyrene derivatives, although such

an activity could not be shown with total cytosolic fractions from strains TA100-hADH1A and TA100-hADH1B (Kollock et al., 2008). However, since high concentrations of the ben- zylic alcohols (100 or 200 uM) were used in that study and background activities were produced by endogenous bacterial enzymes, only activities with high Vmax values would have been detected. In the present study we noticed that variation of the Km was larger than that of Vmax (20,000 versus 1400) among the various ADH-mediated biotransformation reactions studied with alkylpyrene derivatives. Thus, it cannot be ruled out that high-efﬁciency activities, resulting from a low Km value may also be found with ADH1A and ADHlB.
