The calculations also showed that the net charge of the active site changes from positive (+ 1) to zero with increas- ing pH. We speculate that the proton abstracted from the substrate is transported out of the active site via the observed chain of Water molecules, because the formation of a positive charge in the active site Would be energeti-

cally unfavorable at high pH. The fact that this Water chain is also observed in the ternary complex and that all residues lining it are conserved in 119 Drosophila species supports this hypothesis.

It is worth noting that Tyr151 remains half protonated for pH > 7, Which does not ﬁt the common understanding of a strong base as a negatively charged residue, ready to ab- stract a proton from the alcohol hydroxyl group.
