DISCUSSION

The total turnover number (TTN) linearly increases with the substrate concentration. Most NADH-depen- dent dehydrogenases show K M values for NADH below 0.1 mM. Therefore, a cofactor concentration of 0.2 mM should be sufﬁcient for an effective use of the enzyme even when the distribution between the oxidized and reduced form is considered. From this it is obvious that high substrate concentrations are one alternative to reach high TTN. From Table I it can be seen that by increasing the substrate concentration 1.8 times the TTN increases in the same way. Because the single EMR operates under outlet conditions, a two-stage cascade is advantageous to reduce the inﬂuence of product inhi- bition, especially when applying high substrate concen- tration. Therefore, the total LeuDH concentration for the cascade can be lower in comparison to the single EMR, also resulting in a lower product-speciﬁc cata- lyst consumption.
