ADP-ribose binding

The coenzyme fragment ADP-ribose and the ADP-ribose portion of NADH are bound to liver alcohol dehydrogenase through largely identical bonding interactions [14, 15]. The enzyme shows an about 100-fold higher afﬁnity for NADH than for ADP-ribose in the pH range 6— 10 [10, 16]; it was considered of interest to examine whether this afﬁnity difference reflects differences in the ligand on-velocities or off- velocities or both.

DISCUSSION Effects of pH on rate constants for coenzyme binding

Data in Figs 2 and 5 might seem to indicate that on- velocity constants for coenzyme binding to liver alcohol dehy- drogenase are controlled by a single ionization function (with pK, m 9.2) rather than being dependent on three ionization functions according to the relationship in Eqn (3). The conclusion that Eqn (3) applies is based primarily on analysis of the observed pH proﬁle for coenzyme off-velocity and equilibrium binding constants, however, and represents the only reasonable interpretation of the experimental results from a theoretical point of view. Data in Fig. 6, for example, establish that the NAD+ dissociation rate constant is controlled by two ionization functions, one corresponding to a true pKa of 7.6 and the other to an apparent pK, of 9.9 which does not affect the equilibrium binding of NAD J’ [11]. The kinetic theory in the preceding paper assigns the latter pK, to a coupling ionization function for the binding step [13], which means that the NAD+ association rate constant must be assumed to show a corresponding pH dependence with the same apparent pKa of 9.9. Similar considerations apply for NADH binding. The ionization function with apparent pK, 10.7 affecting the NADH dissociation rate constant (Fig. 3) must be assumed to control also the NADH association rate constant.

Furthermore, equilibrium binding studies have provided evidence that the afﬁnity of liver alcohol dehydrogenase for NADH and NAD+ decreases at high pH due to deprotona- tion of a group exhibiting a pK, of 10.4 in free enzyme and above 12 in the binary enzyme - coenzyme complexes [11]. This ionizing group must be assumed on theoretical grounds [13] to contribute a pKa-10.4 dependence to the NADH and NAD* association rate constants analogous to the pKa-9.2 dependence contributed by zinc-bound water [3, 12]. The available experimental data, therefore, provide the inference that pH proﬁles in Figs 2 and 5 include signiﬁcant contributions from three distinct ionization functions. The absence of an immediately obvious dependence of these pro- ﬁles on more than a single pK, is attributable to extensive cancellation of the overlapping effects caused by the pK,-10.4 group and the coupling ionization functions.

Effects attributable to ionization of Lys-228

There is strong reason to believe that the pK,-10.4 dependence of coenzyme binding to liver alcohol dehydroge- nase derives from ionization of Lys-228 and consequent effects on the electrostatic bonding of the coenzyme pyrophosphate group [3, 4].
