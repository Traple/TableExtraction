Other chemicals

All buffer salts and chemicals used in this study were of the highest quality commercially available. Alcohols and aldehydes were distilled twice or three time just before the kinetic experiments. Cinnamic alcohol was sublimated. Water, double-distilled from quartz apparatus, was used for the prep- aration of buffers.

Kinetics

All kinetic measurements were recorded at 20 °C on a Cary 118 spectrophotometer, using 50 mM Tes/NaOH buffer pH 8.0. With bz3PdAD l, the enzymatic reactions were followed by recording the absorbance at 375 nm. When NAD" was used, the variation of the absorbance at 340 nm was measured. The absorption band at 290 nm (a = 25000 M“ cm”) of cinnamaldehyde was used in kinetic measurements where cinnamic alcohol was the substrate.

Affinity and inhibition constants determinations were made from Lineweaver-Burk plots using two concentrations of inhibitors in the case of inhibition measurements.
