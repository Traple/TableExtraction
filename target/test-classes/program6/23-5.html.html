Otherwise, we did not recognise any simple patterns for asso- ciations of kinetic parameters with individual test compounds and enzyme forms. Kinetics even differed markedly between 1-HM-6-MP and 1-HM-8-MP and between l-F-6-MP and l-F- 8-MP (Fig. 3), pairs of compounds that only differ in the position of the methyl group-localised in the benzo ring most distant to the site of metabolism.

In general, the catalytic efﬁciencies for the alkylpyrene derivatives exceeded those for the reference substrates (ethanol and l-octanol or the corresponding aldehydes). Extreme examples are the oxidation of 2-HMP versus ethanol by

ADH2 (10,000-fold) and the reduction of l-F-S-MP ver- sus acetaldehyde by the same enzyme (5200-fold). In the latter case, the true difference is even larger due to the fact that DMSO (used for adding l-F-S-MP, but not for acetaldehyde) is a mixed-type inhibitor for this enzyme (Sec- tion 3.2), which negatively affects the catalytic efﬁciency. The higher catalytic efﬁciencies for the alkylpyrene deriva- tives compared to reference substrates were primarily due to much lower Km values. On the contrary, Vmax values were higher for the reference substrate (except 2-HMP with ADH2).
