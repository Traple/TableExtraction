We have cloned, characterised and crystallised a novel ADH from the hyperthermophile Aeropyrum pernixfo which was the first strictly aerobic hyperthermophilic archaeon to be identified. The organism, which is heterotrophic and grows opti- mally at 90-95 °C, pH 7.0, was isolated from a coastal solfataric vent at Kodakara-Iima Island, Japan.“ The complete genome sequence of A. pernix has been published.12 The A. pernix ADH enzyme is a tetrameric, zinc-containing, type I ADH with a monomer size of 39.5 kDa as calcu- lated from the amino acid sequence. The enzyme is highly specific for the cofactor NAD(H), and dis- plays activity towards a broad range of alcohols, aldehydes and ketones, while appearing to show a preference for cyclic substrates.

Crystal structures are available for a number of eukaryotic and prokaryotic type I ADHs. Horse liver ADH has been studied extensively by X-ray crystallography4 and more recently, an atomic- resolution structure has been obtained.” This NAD(H)-dependent enzyme is dimeric, with two zinc ions present in each monomer. To date, only two ADH structures from thermophiles are avail- able. The NADP(H)—dependent ADH from the thermophilic bacterium Thermoanaerobium brockii is tetrameric, with only a single zinc ion present in each monomer. It was crystallised in its holoo- enzyme form, with the structure solved to 2.5 A resolution.“ A tetrameric NAD(H)-dependent ADH with two zinc ions per subunit was crystal- lised from the hyperthermophilic archaeon Sulfolobus solfataricus. The structure has been solved recently to 1.85 A resolution in its apo form.“

Here, we present the high-resolution three- dimensional structure of the A. pernix ADH in complex with the cofactor NADH and the inhibitor octanoic acid. This represents the first holo form structure of an archaeal ADH.

Overall fold

The A. pernix ADH monomer consists of 359 amino acid residues. The sequence identity with horse liver ADH is 24%. The highest identity with a known structure is 39%, with a medium-chain ADH from the hyperthermophilic archaeon S. solfataricus. Structure-based sequence alignments of the A. pernix ADH to these ADH enzymes are shown in Figure 1.

The A. pernix ADH monomer (Figure 2) folds into two domains, one catalytic and one responsible for binding of the cofactor NADH.
