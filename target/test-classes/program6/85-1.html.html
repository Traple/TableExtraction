MATERIALS AND METHODS Enzyme

Human liver alcohol dehydrogenase was puriﬁed from autopsy livers of normal alcohol dehydrogenase phenotype as described in [9]. The separation of the isozymes was performed according to Biihler and von Wartburg [10].

Chemicals

Ion-exchange celluloses were purchased from Whatman (Maidstone), starch for electrophoresis from Electrostarch (Madison, W1). 4-(3-(N-6-Aminohexanoyl-aminopropyl)- pyrazol — Sepharose was synthesized as in [11]. B-NAD (grade I) and B-NADH (grade I) were obtained from Boehringer (Mannheim), trans-ll-(N,N-dimethylaminoycinnamaldehyde (DACA) from Aldrich (Steinheim, F RG). The concentrations were calculated from measurements of absorbance (DACA e398 = 31.5 mM” cm”). Dipicolinic acid was purchased from Sigma, Tes p.a. and reagents for polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis from Serva (Heidelberg); all other chemicals were the purest available grade from Merck (Darmstadt).

Pretreatments

Double-distilled water was used throughout. Glassware was soaked for several hours in concentrated HNO3, plasticware in 1M NaHCO3 containing 1mM EDTA to avoid metal contamination. Dialysis tubings (Visking 8/32, Serva, Heidelberg) were soaked consecutively in 1% acetic acid, water and twice in 1% Na2CO3 containing 1 mM EDTA at 70°C. After rinsing four times with water the tubings were stored at 4°C in H2O containing 0.02% NaN3 or one drop of chloroform. All metal-exchange experiments were done in Schlenk tubes, the buffers were kept oxygen-free by ﬂushing with nitrogen (99.99%). pH adjustments were made with suprapure NaOH.

Absorption and circular dichroic spectra

Absorption spectra were obtained with a Perkin-Elmer Lamda 9 UV/VIS/NIR spectrophotometer, circular dichroic (CD) spectra with a recording J asco J -20 spectropolarimeter. The sample compartments of both instruments were cooled to 5 — 10°C and purged with nitrogen. The sample cells were soaked for several hours in concentrated HNO3 and rinsed with double-distilled water. The molar absorption extinction coefficients were based on a relative molecular mass of 80 000.
