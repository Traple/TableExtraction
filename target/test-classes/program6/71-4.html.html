Enzyme properties: physical

A single active species of ADH was found in crude extracts or in pure enzyme preparations by polyacrylamide-gradient-gel electrophoresis. This had M, 290 000. With gel-ﬁltration procedures this M, was conﬁrmed when 1mM-dithiothreitol or 1.4mM-2-mercaptoethanol was present. Prelimi- nary experiments indicated there is a complex relationship between the quaternary structure of Aspergillus ADH and the type and concentration of the thiol reagents present, but it was decided that full investigation lay beyond the scope of this present work.

Only a single subunit of ADH was observed on SDS/polyacrylamide-gel electrophoresis with M, 37 500, indicating that the active enzyme as puriﬁed by the above procedure was an octamer.

Amino acid analysis was performed on pure ADH, and the results are given in Table 3. There are no unusual features in its composition, and the data are consistent with a protein of 353 amino acid residues with a unit M, of 37 900, very similar in size to yeast ADH I and ADH II. '

In view of the observation that ADH was produced by a variety of possibly unlikely induc- ers, we considered it desirable to check that it was the same protein. We showed that the ADH from cells grown with either threonine or alcohol as inducer had identical amino acid composition. Furthermore, ADH induced by ethanol, threonine, acetone, serine, propan-l-ol, propan-2-ol or butan-2-one or with no inducer puriﬁed in the identical manner had the same single bands in polyacrylamide-gel and SDS/polyacrylamide-gel electrophoresis.
