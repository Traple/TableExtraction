2.3. Synthesis of Fe 304 magnetic nanoparticles by hydrothermal method

F63 O4 nanoparticles were prepared by hydrothermal method with a ferrous complex using H2O2 as an oxidizer. Ferrous sul- phates heptahydrate (2.502 g) were dissolved in 30 ml water with a concentration of 50 g/l PEG-20000 10 ml. The mixture was achieved at 30 0C under vigorous stirring by adding 30 ml NH4 OH solution. During the reaction process, the pH was main- tained at about 10. Afterwards, 0.27 ml 30% H2O2 solution was added to the stirred mixture for 20 min. The mixture was put into the autoclaves and was heated at 160 0C for 5 h in a furnace. The F63 O4 nanoparticles were gained by centrifugation at 8025 >< g for 15 min and washed several times with water and ethanol, and Ô¨Ånally dried in a vacuum oven at 70 0C.

2.7. Activity and stability measurement

The activity of immobilized SCAD was determined by mea- suring the initial reduction rate of phenylglyoxylic acid by SCAD at the desired temperature following the decrease of NADH concentration at 340 nm on a Lab Tech UV 2100 spectrophotometer. Generally, 4 ml phosphate buffer solution (0.02 M, pH 7.0), 1ml 1mM NADH solution and 1ml 0.1 M phenylglyoxylic acid solution were mixed to the test tube, then 10mg SCAD-bound magnetic nanoparticles was added. After mixing for several minutes, the liquid solution separated from the magnetic nanoparticles via a permanent magnet was used for the analysis of NADH concentration. Unless otherwise stated, the amount of SCAD to 10 mg magnetic nanoparticles was 1 ml 430 U/ml SCAD solution. The activity of SCAD was determined in phosphate buffer (0.02 M, pH 7.0) at 25 0C, and the con- centrations of NADH, phenylglyoxylic acid and immobilized SCAD in the reaction mixture were 1 mM, 0.1 M, and 0.1 ml/mg, respectively.

For free SCAD, the activity measurement was done follow- ing the similar procedures and conditions for the immobilized SCAD except that free SCAD solution was used.
