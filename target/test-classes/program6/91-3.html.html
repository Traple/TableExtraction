No signiﬁcant difference in the expression of KZADH3 and KZADH4 was observed between wild-type and mutant cells grown on glycerol (Fig. 2). Because the amount of the KZADH3 transcript was more abundant in ethanol-grown than in glycerol-grown cells, we wanted to verify if the expression of the gene could also be induced by ethanol.

Total RNAs were prepared from glycerol-grown cells (Fig. 3, lanes 1 and 2) and 5, 10, 20 and 4O min after the addition of ethanol to cultures (Fig. 3, lanes 3—10). In the case of the wild type, the expression of KZADH3 progressively decreased after the addition of ethanol and, after 10 min, the transcript was almost undetectable (Fig. 3, lanes 1, 3, 5, 7 and 9). On the contrary, in the case of the CMK28-2A mutant, we could observe an induction of the KZADH3 gene that was very evident after 4O min (Fig. 3, lanes 2, 4, 6, 8 and 10).
