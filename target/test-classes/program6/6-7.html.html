Conclusions

ADH is irreversibly unfolded (eqn 4) under thermal stress owing to the concomitant aggregation. Hydrophobic interac- tions play an important role in the ADH aggregation upon thermal unfolding, which is mitigated by the oz-CyD-ADH interaction. The oz-CyD-ADH interaction is effectively increased by high ionic strength and culminated in efﬁcient diminishing of the aggregation. oe-CyD causes a decline in both the nuclei size and their population homogeneity. In the presence of oz-CyD/phenylalanine, the effect of oe-CyD on the mitigation of aggregation and the particle size was eliminated, probably because of its linking with phenylalanine instead of interacting with ADH. Hence, the inter-subunit interactions at the hydrophobic aromatic residues influence greatly the aggregation of ADH. The formation of the oe-CyD/ADH subunits masks the exposed hydrophobic aromatic regions with their own hydrophilic external surfaces, leading to the diminishing of the aggregation. In that way, the protein stabilization and the increase in the residual activity are achieved, making the protein storage feasible, even at high temperatures.
