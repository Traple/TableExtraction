It is noteworthy that SaADH proved to be very effective in reducing aliphatic and aryl oc-keto esters, halogenated aryl oc-keto esters, and halogenated ﬂ- keto esters (Table 2).

These data show that SaADH is a strictly NAD(H)- dependent oxidoreductase that has discrete substrate speciﬁcity. As such, SaADH is more similar to the ther- mophilic TtADH (Pennacchio et al. 2008), than to the three SDR superfamily mesophilic ADHs (i.e., LSADH, LB-RADH, and PED) which are active on a variety of

aliphatic as well as aromatic alcohols, ketones, diketones and keto esters.
