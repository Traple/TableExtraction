Coenzyme (NAD+) and inhibitor molecules were easily fitted into the elec- tron density maps. In the apo form of DADH the region from Thr186 to Thr191 has not been mod- eled because it was disordered and the loop region ranging from Gly15 to Leu17 had high B—factors. The binary form of DADH has a better-defined structure for the glycine-rich loop (Gly15 to Leu17) and adopts a slightly different conformation when compared to the apo form, due to the interaction with the pyrophosphate group of the coenzyme. In the binary complex the loop from Thr186 to Thr191 is disordered, allowing the substrate to enter the active site cavity.
