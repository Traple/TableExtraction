Inﬂuence of reagents and solvents 0n TADH activity

The inﬂuence of various reagents on TADH activity was investigated using the standard photometrical assay. TADH was incubated at 60°C and 500 rpm in a Thermomixer comfort (Eppendorf AG, Germany) in the presence of an indicated amount of metal ion, chelator, reducing agent (10 mM), detergent (10% w/v), denaturant (1-5 M), or solvent (10—50% v/v). After 1 h, samples were transferred to the photometer, and the remaining activity was deter- mined after addition of 1 mM NAD+ and 10 mM cyclo- hexanol. As a reference, TADH was treated in the same way without addition of any compound.

Determination of enantiomeric excess

In order to investigate the enantioselectivity of TADH, ketone conversion was quantiﬁed on a small scale. The assay mixture contained in a final volume of 1.2 mL, 10 mM substrate, approximately 0.5 U mL-l TADH, 1 mM NADH, and 10% (v/v) 2-propanol for cofactor regeneration. Samples were incubated at 60°C and 500 rpm on a Thermomixer comfort (Eppendorf AG, Germany). The conversion was followed by analyzing samples during the course of the reaction by gas chromatography (GC). Samples were extracted with 1:1 (v/v) diethylether contain- ing 1 mM dodecane as internal standard. The ether phase was dried over Na2SO4 prior to analysis (vide infra). The identity of the products was determined using enantiopure standards of the investigated alcohols.

Aldehyde dismutation reaction

To investigate the inﬂuence of pH on dismutase activity, assays were performed in 2-mL Eppendorf tubes, contain- ing 100 mM Bis-Tris, pH 6, 100 mM Bis-Tris, pH 7, 100 mM Na-phosphate, pH 8, or 100 mM bicine, pH 9. Furthermore, each assay mixture included 0.5 mM NAD+, 95 ug mL-l TADH, and 10 mM of butyraldehyde. For cofactor regeneration in a substrate-coupled approach 10% (v/v), acetone was added. The tubes were incubated at 60°C and 500 rpm on a Thermomixer comfort (Eppendorf AG, Germany). After 10 h, products were analyzed by saturating 100 uL sample with NaCl. Subsequently, half a volume of 50% H2SO4 was added to acidify the reaction mixture. The substances were extracted with 300 uL of diethylether containing 1 mM dodecane and analyzed by GC after drying over Na2SO4.

The inﬂuence of pH was also investigated spectropho- tometrically by following the formation of NADH at 340 nm and 60°C. An assay mixture contained in 1 mL of the appropriate buffer 53 ug mL-l TADH, 0.5 mM NADT, and 10 mM butyraldehyde.

Analytical methods

Protein analyses Sodium dodecyl sulfate (SDS) polyacryl- amide gel electrophoresis (PAGE) was performed according to Laemmli (1970), the gels were stained with Coomassie G 250 and were analyzed using ImageJ (ImageJ 1.3 8x, National Institute of Health, USA). Native gels were stained with Coomassie G 250 and a dehydrogenase-specific stain (Heeb and Gabriel 1984). Protein concentration was determined by the method of Bradford (1976) using the Quick Start Bradford dye (Biorad, Germany). Standard curves were prepared with bovine serum albumin (Aldrich).

Enzyme activity assay TADH activity was determined spectrophotometrically in a Cary 300 Bio UV-Vis spectro- photometer by following the consumption or formation of NADH at 340 nm and 60°C. A typical oxidation reaction was performed in 200 mM bicine, pH 9.0, containing 10 mM cyclohexanol as substrate and 1 ug of TADH in a reaction volume of 200 uL. The cuvette was closed with a teﬂon lid to prevent evaporation of the volatile substrates. The mixture was left for 2 min to equilibrate to 60°C, and the substrate was given time to solubilize homogenuously in the aqueous solution. Subsequently, the assay was started by addition of 1 mM NAD+. The reaction was monitored for 1.5 min. For reductions, 200 mM Bis-Tris, pH 6.0, was used, and the reaction mixture contained 20 mM cyclohex- anone and 1 ug of TADH in a total volume of 200 uL. The reaction was started by addition of 0.1 mM NADH. An activity of 1 U was deﬁned as 1 umol of produced or consumed NADH per minute. The extinction coefficient for NADH of 6220 cm_1 mM-l was used for the calculation.

Kinetics Kinetic parameters of TADH were calculated from independent measurements. The data were fitted by nonlinear regression to Michaelis—Menten’s equation using SigmaPlot (SigmaPlot 10.0, Systat Software). kcat was calculated based on homotetrameric TADH with a molecular weight of 149 kDa.

GC analytics GC analyses were performed on a Finnigan Focus GC (Thermo Electron Corporation, Dreieich, Germany) equipped with a Rt-BDex-sm column (30 m><0.25 mm>< 0.25 um, Restek GmbH, Bad Homburg, Germany) and a ﬂame ionization detector using N2 as carrier gas. The injector and detector temperature were 200°C and 230°C, respective- ly, and the split ratio was 1:10.
