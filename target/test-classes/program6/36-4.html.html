Effect of pH on Activity

For reduction, the optimal pH for purified sec-alcohol dehydrogenase “A” was found to be at pH 6.5, and at pH 9.0 for oxidation. The enzyme was active over a wide pH range starting from pH 6 (reduction) up to pH 11.0 (oxi- dation) (Fig. 3). The assay was performed in the absence of cosubstrates.

Long-Term Enzyme Stability

Pure enzyme in phosphate buffer (50 mM, 0.15 M NaCl, pH 7.0) can be stored as lyophilized powder or dissolved in buffer at —20°C after shock-freezing with liquid nitrogen. Both methods resulted in no significant loss of activity up to 10 months. Any other buffer solution used during enzyme purification is equally suitable for storage.

Kinetics

The enzymatic reduction of acetophenone in the presence of NADH was monitored by measuring the decrease of NADH absorption at different substrate- and NADH-concentrations. The obtained data were plotted according to Lineweaver- Burk and the determination of the kinetic constants was performed as described by Bisswanger (2000) giving a Km(NADH) = 2.8 mM and a Km(acetophenone) = 25 mM. The dissociation constant Kis = 0.15 mM describes the affinity of the enzyme-NADH complex for the second substrate acetophenone. The reaction rate constant for the forward reaction was determined as V1 = 66 umol-min_1 for the reduction of acetophenone in the presence of NADH. For the reduction of acetophenone, kCat/Km equals 270 s_1 uM-l calculated for the enzyme as a monomer (38 kDa).
