Measurement of enzyme activity

The total incubation volume was 3.0ml and the change in E340 at 24°C was measured with a Gilford spectrophotometer (model 2000) ﬁtted with a con- stant-temperature circulator (Lauda K2/R model, Brinkmann Instruments Inc., Toronto, Canada). Unless otherwise speciﬁed in the legends to the Tables and Figures, the alcohol dehydrogenase activity was measured in the direction of ethanol to acetaldehyde as follows: 0.1M-ethanol and LZmM-NAD were added to 0.075 M-sodium pyrophosphate—0.22M- glycine—0.075 M-semicarbazide, pH 8.2. One unit of enzyme activity was deﬁned as that amount of enzyme which reduced lumol of NAD/min. Speciﬁc activity was expressed as units/mg of protein.

Measurements of the activity with various substrates

The three fractions have different activity proﬁles for the alcohols that were used as substrates (Table 2). Although all fractions have the highest activity with cinnamyl alcohol, fraction ADH I-air is the most active with this compound. Both fraction ADH I-air and fraction ADH II are more active with allyl alcohol than with ethanol, but fraction ADH I-Oz is more active with ethanol than with any other alcohol, with the exception of cinnamyl alcohol.

Km for the substrates

The Km values of the three fractions for ethanol and NAD, and for acetaldehyde and NADH were meas- ured and the results are shown in Table 3. The results were calculated by the double-reciprocal method of Lineweaver and Burk and by plotting [S]/v against [S] (cf. Dixon & Webb, 1964).

Fraction ADH II has a higher Km than the other two fractions towards NAD and ethanol, and its Km for NADH is one-tenth and for acetaldehyde one- hundreth that for NAD and ethanol. Both ADHI fractions have similar Km values towards both sub- strate pairs.
