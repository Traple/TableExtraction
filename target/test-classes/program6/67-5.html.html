DISCUSSION

Structural Changes Resulting from the Substitution 0f Zinc with Cadmium. Replacement of zinc with other transition metals has an effect on the activity of the enzyme. It has been proposed that for most zinc-dependent enzymes, the metal acts as a Lewis acid by (de)protonating the substrate (32) and that the rate of activity should follow a trend in Lewis acidity of the metals. A correlation between the activity of cobalt-, nickel-, zinc-, and cadmium-substituted enzymes and the acidic properties of these metals was observed for aldehyde reduction in LADH (14). Cobalt is a

stronger Lewis acid than zinc, and the cobalt-substituted enzyme has 140% activity compared to the native enzyme. Cadmium is a much weaker Lewis acid than zinc, and the cadmium-substituted enzyme has only 2.5% of the activity of the native enzyme. Among the metals used in substitution, the electronic configuration of cadmium is most similar to that of zinc, and previous X-ray structures do not indicate substantial differences in metal coordination between the native and cadmium-substituted enzyme (33).

To validate the effect of the substitution of zinc with cadmium on the enzyme structure, a comparison between the ternary complexes of LADH with NADH and MPD with either zinc (lHET) or cadmium (lHEU) is made.
