MATERIALS AND METHODS

Activity Assay During Purification

rac-l-Phenylethanol (3.3 umol) for the oxidation mode or acetophenone (3.3 umol) for the reduction mode were added to a protein sample (0.99 ml). The reaction was started by the addition of 10 ul of a NAD+- or NADH-solution, respectively, in Tris-HCl buffer (10 mM, pH 7.5) to effect a final NAD+- or NADH-concentration of 1 mM. The samples were shaken at 1200 rpm in 1.5 ml Eppendorf vials at 30°C for 20 min employing an Eppendorf therrnomixer. The re- actions were quenched by addition of ethyl acetate (0.5 ml), the organic layer was separated and dried over Na2SO4. Conversion and enantiomeric excess of the alcohol were determined by GC analysis as previously described (Stamp- fer et al., 2003a).

Activity Assay in the Presence of Cosubstrate

The assay differed from the above-mentioned procedure in that rac-l-phenylethanol (3.3 umol) dissolved in acetone (0.1 ml) or acetophenone (3.3 umol) in 2-propanol (0.1 ml) were added to the protein sample (0.89 ml).

Protein concentrations were measured according to the method of Bradford (1976). Native gel electrophoresis (Grell et al., 1965; Barrett et al., 1980; Ornstein, 1964; Davis, 1964) and SDS-page electrophoresis (Laemmli, 1970) were performed as described in the literature.

Protein Purification

Wet cell paste (Kroutil et al., 1997) (50 g) was suspended in Tris-HCl buffer (10 mM, pH 7.5) to give a total volume of 120 ml. An equal volume of glass beads (O 0.25 mm) was added and the cells were disrupted in seven cycles (2-min disruption, 5-min cooling) using a Vibrogen cell mill. After removal of the glass beads by filtration, and centrifugation at 4°C (38,000g, 2 h), the supernatant was treated with DEAE-cellulose (30 g) suspended in Tris-HCl buffer (300 ml, 10 mM, pH 7.5) to remove carotenoids and mucous compounds. Alcohol dehydrogenase activity was released by addition of 0.5 M NaCl followed by centrifugation. After addition of ammonium sulfate to the supernatant (final concentration of 1 M), the protein solution was applied (4 times 35 ml each) to a phenyl sepharose high performance column (Pharmacia, Gaithersburg, MD, V = 20 ml) equili- brated with buffer A (10 mM Tris-HCl, 1 M (NH4)2SO4, pH 7.5). A stepwise gradient of buffer A (75 ml 100%, 50 ml 50%, and 50 ml 25%) followed by a linear gradient to 100% of buffer B within 50 ml (buffer B: 10 mM Tris-HCl, pH 7.5) at a ﬂow rate of 4 ml-min-l (fraction volume 8 ml) eluted the fraction of interest at 100% buffer B. All chro- matographic procedures were performed on a Pharmacia FPLC system (LCC-500) at room temperature. The active fractions (48 ml) were dialyzed against 1 L Tris-HCl buffer (10 mM, pH 7.5) overnight at 4°C and subjected to a UNO Q6 column (BioRad, Hercules, CA, V = 6 ml, 4 ml-min-l ﬂow, fraction volume 6 ml). The protein was released applying a stepwise gradient of 60 ml 0%, 70 ml 16%, 30 ml 50%, 20 ml 100% of elution buffer (10 mM Tris-HCl, 1.5 M NaCl, pH 7.5). The alcohol dehydrogenase activity of interest was eluted at 0.75 M NaCl after 135 ml.

Desalting and replacing the buffer system with Bis-Tris- HCl buffer (10 mM, pH 6.0) was carried out using desalting- chromatography-columns (BioRad, V = 10 ml). Samples (max. 50 mg protein) were applied onto a Blue Sepharose column (Pharmacia, V = 8 ml), equilibrated with 40 ml Bis- Tris-HCl (10 mM, pH 6.0). sec-ADH “A” was released right after the void volume of the column (ﬂow 4 ml-min-l, fraction volume 8 ml). Active fractions (24 ml) were con- centrated to a volume of 2—3 ml using Centriplus l0 tubes (Amicon. Beverly, MA). Samples (max. 200 ul each) were applied onto Superdex 200 (Pharmacia, V = 24 ml), equilibrated with phosphate buffer (50 mM, 0.15 M NaCl, pH 7.0), at a ﬂow rate of 0.5 ml-min-l. Under isocratic conditions, sec-ADH “A” was eluted at 15 ml.
