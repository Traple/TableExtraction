A more complete assessment of the model’s accu~ racy will require more exhaustive efforts to obtain improved fits. Nonetheless, interesting conclusions can be drawn from the present set of calculated spectra.

Speciﬁcally, for both free and immobilized enzyme the rotational reorientation of spin label attached to component 1 is slower and more anisotropic than that of label attached to component 2 (a larger value of r indicates slower rota~ tional reorientation, and the degree of anisotropy can be gauged by the extent to which the ratio of the rotational correlation times deviates from unity). In addition, for sol-

uble LADH, nitroxide motion perpendicular to the x axis is relatively rapid in both components; however, reorientation about the x axis is much slower in component 1. Upon im- mobilization of the enzyme, nitroxide motion is slowed in all directions, with the larger relative changes being seen for component 1. Moreover, the most pronounced decrease in the rate of nitroxide motion occurs about the y and z axes of component 1.
