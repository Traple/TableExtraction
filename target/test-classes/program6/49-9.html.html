Expression analysis revealed that KmADHI is a constitutive gene, as is ScADH1,46) and that KmADHZ is an inducible gene in the presence of glucose. Consistently with this, there are possible elements for Rap1 and Yap1 in the 5’-noncoding region of KmADHZ not yet discovered. There are several putative elements in KmADHI, but their physiological functions are not clear.

Little is known about the physiological functions of KmAdh isozymes. Although KmADH] and KmADHZ have been found to be preferentially expressed in the presence of glucose, as were KIADH], KZADHZQlM) and ScADHI,” KmADHI was expressed equally in glucose, glycerol, and ethanol-containing media, and KmADHZ was expressed only in glucose-containing medium. KmAdhl and KmAdhZ as cytoplasmic en- zymes can be synthesized in a constitutive fashion and an inducible fashion respectively. Therefore, KmAdhl and KmAdh2 might contribute to ethanol fermentation from various carbon sources and glucose, respectively.
