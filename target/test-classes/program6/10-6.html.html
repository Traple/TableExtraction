Sulfhydryl Group Analysis of YADH

We also monitored the concominant modiﬁcation of thiol groups of the yeast enzyme and the accompanying inactivation in the manner analogous to experiments with the horse liver enzyme. After 15 min of preincu- bation of 16 uM YADH with 10 mM tBOOH, at least three cysteine residues per enzyme subunit were mod- iﬁed (presumably oxidized) and YADH was inactivated by about 50%. A similar trend was obtained when HLADH was inactivated by H202 (3, 5) under condi- tions in which the active site metal bound Cys-46 or Cys-174 were oxidized. The rate of modiﬁcation of —SH groups of YADH increased over time with increasing concentration of tBOOH (Fig. 7a). In contrast to HLADH the time-dependent pattern of —SH group ox- idation of YADH strongly correlates with the time de- pendence of YADH inactivation by tBOOH.

Complete inactivation of YADH corresponded to ox- idation of about four thiol groups per ADH subunit; thus, two additional thiol groups were oxidized as com- pared to that seen with HLADH. The time course of thiol group oxidation of YADH showed two distinct phases (Fig. 7 a). Figure 7b shows the relationship be- tween the residual activity of YADH as a function of the number of —SH groups oxidized. In the ﬁrst faster phase of the time course about two thiol groups per subunit were oxidized within 5 min of the incubation with 5 mM tBOOH (Fig. 7a). This corresponded to a small (10—15%) decrease in YADH activity (Fig. 7b) and was reminiscent of the time dependence for thiol group oxidation seen with HLADH; i.e., two thiol groups were modiﬁed in about 5 min. In the second slower phase, which was not seen with HLADH, two additional thiol groups were oxidized in the yeast en- zyme (Fig. 7b). The oxidation of these thiol groups in the second phase was associated with the observed further loss in catalytic activity of the enzyme (Fig. 7b).
