If the substrate-metal ion interaction is the main determining factor, the structural vari- ation should result in opposite effects on alcohol oxidation and ketone reduction. This hypothesis is also supported by a comparison of the kinetic data for native Zn(H)LADH and Co(II)LADH. As can be seen from Tables 1 and 2, variation in the substrate structure results in similar changes in the kinetic parameters for both enzyme species, independent of the nature of the catalytic metal ion.

From these findings, it was concluded that the reduction of Z-butanone, producing (R)-2-butano1, should be faster than the rate found experimentally. Since (S)-2-butanol is the main product of this reaction, it was concluded that the observed rate of 2-butanone reduction in single-turnover experiments, which were found to be pseudo first order and monophasic, corresponds to the formation of (S)-2-butanol from the pro- ductive ternary complex with 2-butanone bound in a pro-S conﬁguration (see Fig. 2). This approximation has also to be extended to the steady-state results. The same reasoning holds for the reduction of 2-pentanone.
