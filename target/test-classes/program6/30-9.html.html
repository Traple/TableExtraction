Hence, this residue can take up and release a proton with equal probability. No conformational changes upon proton abstraction are needed or have to be assumed for this process. This is in accord with the observation that there are no structural differences in the active sites of the binary and ternary complexes.1 Our interpretation of this result is that with increasing pH the proton relay network creates an appropriate environment to transfer the proton out of the active site. The calculations do not reveal the driving force for proton abstraction; however, the tiny but still detectable presence of the deprotonated form of W144 indicates that the binary complex indeed facilitates a proton abstraction from the substrate.
