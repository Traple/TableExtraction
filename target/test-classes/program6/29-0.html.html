Puriﬁcation and Characterization of the Alcohol Dehydrogenase with a Broad Substrate Speciﬁcity Originated from 2-Phenylethanol-Assimilating Brevibacterium sp. KU 1309

Jun-ichiro Hiranof Kenji Miyamoto,1* and Hiromichi Ohtal

Department of Biosciences and Informatics, Center for Biosciences and Informatics, Keio University, 3-14-1 Hiyoshi, Yokohama 223-8522, Japanl

Received 12 April 2005/Accepted 6 June 2005

A novel 2-phenylethanol dehydrogenase has been puriﬁed from a soil bacterium Brevibacterium sp. KU 1309. The enzyme was puriﬁed about 1400-fold t0 homogeneity, and found to be a mono- meric enzyme of apparent 39 kDa. The enzyme had broad substrate speciﬁcity and catalyzes a re- versible oxidation of various primary alcohols to aldehydes. The enzyme required NAD+, but not NADP+ as a cofactor. Thus, the enzyme was classiﬁed into a group of NAD+-dependent primary alcohol dehydrogenase. The activity was inhibited by Cu“, Ni“, Ba“, Hg“ and p-chloromer- curibenzoate. The enzyme is expected to be applicable as an effective biocatalyst in the oxidation

of various alcohols.

[Key words: Brevibacterium, 2-phenylethanol, alcohol dehydrogenase]

Oxidation of alcohols is one of the fundamental and es- sential yet immature processes in organic chemistry. Al- though many methods and reagents for oxidation have been developed, traditional methods involve the use of stoichio- metric amount of toxic heavy metal reagents (e.g., Cr, Mn) or moisture-sensitive oxidants, and generate large quantities of waste. Thus the development of green, sustainable and safe alcohol oxidizing system is very important.

Recently we have shown that a soil bacterium Brevibac- terium sp. KU 1309 can be efficiently applied to the oxida- tion of alcohols. The strain could assimilate 2-phenyletha- nol, and exhibited high oxidative activity towards various alcohols (1). Endogenous 2-phenylethanol is found in the metabolic pathway of styrene in Xantobacter strain 124X (2), 2-phenylethylamine in Escherichia coli (3), and pheny- lalanine in Saccharomyces cerevisiae (4). However, there has not been sufficient information concerning the enzyme which participate in the metabolism of 2-phenylethanol, al- though 2-phenylethanol dehydrogenase (PEDH) is estimated to be the key enzyme of this metabolic pathway (Fig. 1). In this paper, we describe the puriﬁcation and characterization of the PEDH with broad substrate speciﬁcity.

Bacterial strain and culture conditions Brevibacterium sp. KU1309 was obtained via screening of a microorganism capable of degrading 2-phenylethanol and used as the source of the en- zyme. The strain was grown on medium I consisting of meat ex- tract (7 g), peptone (10 g), NaCl (3 g), yeast extract (5 g), and 2-phenylethanol (4 g) in 1000 ml of distilled water, pH 7.0. Brevi- bacterium sp. KU1309 was subcultured at 30°C for 24 h in a test tube containing l0 ml of medium I. The subculture broth (5 ml) was then inoculated into a 500-ml Sakaguchi ﬂask containing 100 ml medium I and cultivated for 3 d at 30°C. The cells were har- vested by centrifugation at 5000 >< g for 20 min at 4°C and washed with 0.1 M Tris—HCl buffer (pH 8.0).

Puriﬁcation of PEDH All puriﬁcation procedures were per- formed at a temperature lower than 4°C. Buffer used was Tris—HCl (pH 8.0) contained 5 mM B-mercaptoethanol and 0.5 mM EDTA. The washed cells (25 g wet weight) from 1 Z of culture broth were suspended in 0.1 M buffer (250 ml). The cell suspension was then homogenized by Dyno-mill (Willy A. Bachofen Co., Basel, Switzer- land) in the presence of glass beads for 30 min at 4°C. The suspen- sion was centrifuged at 4°C for 20 min at 12,000 >< g. The super- natant was fractionated with ammonium sulfate (50%—80%). The active precipitate was collected by centrifugation (12,000 >< g, 20 min), dissolved in l0 mM buffer containing 30% (NH4)2SO4. The resulting enzyme solution was applied to a Ether-Toyopearl 650 M column (2.8><20 cm) which had been equilibrated with l0 mM buffer containing 30% (NH4)2SO4.
