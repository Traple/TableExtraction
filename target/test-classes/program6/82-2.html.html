Ethanol Competition

Further experimental evidence, in addition to that already described [1], in favour of the essential role played by the well-known enzyme active site, has come from studies of inhibition of the peroxidatic reaction observed in the presence of ethanol. As one might expect, ethanol has appeared to be a competitive inhibitor towards H202 (see Fig.3) with an apparent inhibition constant K1 = 0.5 mM.

This result indicates that ethanol and hydrogen peroxide molecules compete for a very similar, if not identical, site within the same enzyme active site, hence near the catalytic zinc ion, which allows them to react with a molecule of NAD+ already bound. At present, no further inhibition studies have been performed with other alcohols nor with NADH, although they can in principle give other informative details on the mechanism of the peroxidatic pathway.
