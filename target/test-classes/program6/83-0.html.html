The kinetic mechanism and the substrate speciﬁcity of liver alcohol dehydrogenase are changed when 3-benzoylpyridine-adenine dinucleotide is used as coenzyme. Only primary alcohols are substrates of the enzyme and with ethanol the mechanism becomes rapid-equilibrium random bi-bi. According to model building experi- ments on a graphic display, the benzoyl group partially enters the substrate binding site, whereas the essential interactions between coenzyme and enzyme are preserved. This restraint on the substrate binding site provides a molecular explanation for the observed dependence between coenzyme and substrate chemical structures.

Several NAD+ analogues where the amide NH; group of the nicotinamide ring has been removed or replaced by an hydrophobic group have been prepared. All of them are cata- lytically active with horse liver alcohol dehydrogenase (re- ferred to hereafter as alcohol dehydrogenase), but show sig- nificant maximum rate value differences compared to NAD + [1 — 3]. It is only with 3-acetylpyridine—adenine dinucleotide [4] and thionicotinamide—adenine dinucleotide [5] that detailed kinetic studies have been made in order to explain these differences. The most bulky substituent introduced in that position is a phenyl ring. bz3PdAD+ was shown to be active as coenzyme with ethanol as substrate [1].

Examination of the holoenzyme active site and model building of coenzyme analogues within the enzyme structure lead to the conclusion that bz3PdAD+ binding should in- terfere with the substrate binding. We therefore investigated the catalytic properties of this coenzyme analogue with various alcohol substrates and determined the kinetic mecha- msm.

MATERIALS AND METHODS Enzymes

All experiments were done at 4°C. Crystalline suspen- sions of liver alcohol dehydrogenase was purchased from Boehringer. The material was centrifuged at 15000 rpm for 30 min. The pellet was dissolved in a minimum volume of 25 mM Tes/NaOH buffer pH 8.0 containing 100 mM potas- sium chloride. The enzyme concentration, measured from ultraviolet absorption at 280 nm (absorbance at 280 nm of a I-mg/ml enzyme solution is 0.455), was adjusted to 10 mg/ml.

After dialysis against 25 mM Tes/NaOH buffer pH 8.0, the enzyme was crystallised by slow addition of distilled t-butanol. Crystals appeared at about 5-7% (v/v) of t-butanol. Addi- tion of the precipitating agent was stopped at 25% (v/v) con- centration.

For each series of kinetic experiments, the enzyme crystals were separated from the mother liquor, dissolved in the same buffer, centrifuged to eliminate any insoluble material and the enzyme solution dialysed. The purity of this enzyme was checked by polyacrylamide/sodium dodecyl sulfate electro- phoresis and the active sites were titrated by mean of the NAD+/pyrazole assay [6]. The purity of the EE isoenzyme ranged from 95% to 100%.
