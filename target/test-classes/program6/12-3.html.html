Note that catalytic effi- ciency with the 3-keto compounds is generally lower than that with the 3-hydroxyl. However, we detected very low activity with 50c-pregnan-3-ol-20-one, which suggests that the specificity is not absolute. These results suggest that the formation of allo- pregnanolone does not result from the direct action of ADHlC.

The low activity of ADH1C*2 with the 50c steroids contrasts to that with the horse ADHlS and rat ADH, which have good activity on both 50c and 5B isomers, with a preference for the 5B isomers [29]. The data are limited, however, because human ADH1C*2 has low activity on steroids in general and it is difficult to determine the relative activity for a series of related 50c and 5B isomers. There is no obvious explanation for the differences in specificity, since these enzyme have similar structures and molecular modeling shows that steroids could ﬁt into the substrate binding sites, albeit with some close steric contacts [4,23,25]. Nevertheless, the different amino acid residues in the substrate binding sites and those distant from the active site probably account for the differences in activities [24,25,28]. Substitu-

tions of single amino acids in the active site of horse ADHlE or human ADH1B*1 that were designed to convert the enzyme to ones more like human ADHlC increased activity on steroids, but not to the same level as ADHlS or ADHlC [24,28].

3.2. Inhibition by steroids

Even if a steroid is not a substrate, it could bind to the enzyme and be an inhibitor of the catalytic reaction of a reactive substrate. Steroid substrates should bind with the hydrogen to be transferred from the steroid oriented toward the nicotinamide ring, but if the steroid is ﬂipped 180° in the active site so that the hydrogen points away from the nicotinamide ring, no reaction is possible. This effect was demonstrated with the stereoisomers of 3-methylcyclohexanol, where the 1S ,3S -isomer was a good substrate and the lR,3R-isomer was a good inhibitor of horse ADHlE [48]. Therefore, the inhibitory effects were determined with several of the inactive steroids.
