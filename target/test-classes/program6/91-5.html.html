Genetic analysis

We crossed the CMK28-2A strain carrying the aar900 mutation with CMK32-12B and, after sporulation of di- ploids, asci dissection and tetrad spore analysis were per- formed. Genetic analysis, reported in Table 2, clearly demonstrated that the aar900 mutation in the progeny followed a 2 :2 Mendelian segregation, showing a link with ade, arg and thr loci which are located on chromosome III of K. lactis. Distances between loci, calculated on the basis of genetic recombination, were too big to allow the mapping the aar900 mutation with sufﬁcient precision and we could only locate this mutation close to adel, towards arg5,6.

Discussion

In previous work, we demonstrated that, in K. lactis, ethanol induces the transcription of KZADH4 and simultaneously represses that of KZADH3, two genes encoding the mitochon- drial alcohol dehydrogenase activities. In this paper, we report the preliminary characterization of a new mutation, namely aar900, that affects this type of regulation. In fact, in contrast to wild type, mutant strains showed the presence of KlAdh3p and the simultaneous absence of KlAdh4p when grown on ethanol. As revealed by Northern blot analysis, aar900 seems to act at the transcriptional level in that KZADH3 escaped repression by ethanol and was even induced after the shift of mutant cells to this carbon source. On the contrary, aar900 seems to have no effect on KZADH4 because this gene was normally transcribed in the mutant but, surprisingly, the encoded activity could not be detected. At present, we do not have a conclusive explanation for this observation. One possibility is that the KZADH4 mRNA is not translated to protein, which suggests the existence of an additional regula- tory control for this activity operating at the posttranscrip- tional level. Another possibility is that KlAdh-lp is normally produced in the mutant and is quickly degraded or inacti- vated, indicating the existence of a post-translational control affecting the protein activity. Whatever the explanation, the absence of KlAdh4p was clearly related to the expression of KZADH3 in that the deletion of this gene in the CMK28-2A mutant restored the presence of a functional KlAdh-lp activity.

All these data seem to conﬁrm the existence of mechan- isms operating at different levels that avoid the simultaneous presence of the two activities within mitochondria. More- over, the aar900 mutation seems to affect speciﬁcally the transcription of KZADH3 and not a general negative regula- tion exerted by ethanol. In fact, the transcription in the CMK28-2A mutant of KZPDCI, another ethanol-repressible gene, did not escape from the repression exerted by this carbon source.
