The results given in Table 2 show that 5oz- androstan-l7B-ol-3-one is a good inhibitor of the enzyme, with a Ki of z 5 uM. Likewise, 4- androsten-3,17-dione is an inhibitor, competitive with cyclohexanone, but not a substrate with measurable activity. Our results differ from those of Mardh et al. [39] who found that 4-androsten- l7[3-ol-3-one (testosterone) was a noncompetitive inhibitor of human ADHlC*1 against varied concentrations of either NAD+ or ethanol, with K, values of z 10 uM. They also found that 4- androsten-l7B-ol-3-one and 5oc-androstan-17B-ol- 3-one (Soc-dihydrotestosterone) noncompetitively inhibited heterodimeric human ADHlB*l/lC*2 against varied concentrations of ethanol. We have no explanation for the difference between our results and theirs, but it may be noted that human ADH1B*1 substituted with Ser for Thr-48, which confers some activity with 5B-androstan-3B-ol-17- one, is also inhibited uncompetitively by testoster- one against varied concentrations of ethanol [28]. A possible explanation for simple noncompetitive inhibition (with a change in Vmax but not in Km) is that the enzyme is inactivated by the inhibitor, but we found no evidence for a time-dependent inactivation of human ADHlC*2 by 4-andros- ten-3,l7-dione. Noncompetitive inhibition would also be obtained if the inhibitor binds to both the enzyme-NAD+ and enzyme-NADH complexes with similar afﬁnity.
