Results and Discussion

The Growth. Previously, we had hypothesized that S. solfa- taricus P2 could metabolize ethanol, since preliminary results showed ethanol utilization in addition to glucose consumption by this organism.” Therefore, to further elucidate S. solfataricus’ mechanism of action for ethanol metabolism, cultures were grown on a variety of ethanol concentrations as the sole carbon source. Growth curves, as illustrated in Figure 1, show that S. solfataricus is indeed able to grow on ethanol in the absence of glucose. Here, growth rates for S. solfataricus’ cultures grown solely on ethanol were found to be at least 5 times lower than the glucose control cultures (Figure 1), with no growth observed at 3.97% w/v ethanol. These results suggest an inhibitory effect for growth in the presence of ethanol for S. solfataricus, with full inhibition occurring between 2.38 and 3.97%.
