CyDs are crystalline, water-soluble, cyclic, non-reducing oligosaccharides built up from six, seven or eight glucopyranose units and are used as artiﬁcial chaperones for the proper refolding of enzymes used in biotechnology, as well as in drug delivery for the encapsulation of poorly soluble drugs [I l—l3]. CyDs are effective functional excipients, which are being used more often to tackle adverse pharmaceutical characteristics, es- pecially low solubility in water [I4]. CyDs have hydrophobic cavities, which probably shield the hydrophobic surfaces of denatured or partially denatured proteins and prevent direct interactions between the surfaces known to cause protein aggregation [I5]. Nonetheless, the effect of CyD on the aggregation and the artiﬁcial chaperone activity is a challenge for researchers. lts mechanism is not completely clear. The enhanced protein re-activation by CyDs may be due to their ability to bind to hydrophobic sites in protein folding intermediate(s) [I6]. Modiﬁed CyDs are proposed for this purpose [I7]. Also, it is believed that CyDs show afﬁnity of the hydrophobic cavity for exposed aromatic amino acids residues such as phenylalanine [I]. Competitive studies with phenylalanine also showed the importance of the CyD cavity in the CyD-assisted refolding [I6], but there was no information on the competition-free phenylalanine on the CyD cavity as a CyD-anti-aggregating agent. In the present study, this was evaluated using horse liver ADH (alcohol dehydrogenase). ADH, a metalloenzyme, has been widely studied for its well-known biotechnological signiﬁc- ance. This enzyme is mesostable and dimeric, consisting of two identical subunits and used for the synthesis of chiral compounds [l8—20]. lts kinetics [2 I ,22] and its three- dimensional structure are well known [23,24]. ADH is easily aggregated when exposed to distorting pressures such as variations in pH and temperature, as well as organic solvents. Similarly to alcohol oxidase, ADH has been used in soluble form, trapped in ultraﬁltration reactors for biotechnological industrial process [25-27]. Nevertheless, its aggregation procedure limits its biotechnological applications. This enzyme has been used for many years as a model protein to study the chaperone-like activity of many proteins, such as oe-crystallin and heat-shock proteins [20,28—33].

In the present study, we investigated the aggregation modes of ADH, the aggregation mitigation of ADH with the help of CyDs, the competitive effect of the free phenyl- alanine on the CyD cavity and the ADH stabilization, both kinetically and structurally, using oz-CyD.
