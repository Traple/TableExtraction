The hydrogen bond between the imidazole of His43 and the 3'-hydroxyl group of NAD+ ribose is stable at 298 K, while the imidazole of His43 is hydrogen bound to the 2’- hydroxyl group of NAD+ ribose at 323 and 348 K. This is due to the enlargement of the interstice between the cofactor- binding domain and catalytic domain at low temperatures. As a consequence, the hydrogen bond between the hydroxyl group of Thr4O and the 2'-hydroxyl group of NAD+ is disrupted. At 348 K, the 3'-hydroxyl group of NAD+ ribose is closer to His43 and Thr4O is closer to the substrate. This subtle difference in the structure at different temperatures leads to a pivotal consequence. The placements of heavy atoms along the proton transfer pathway are more equidistant at 323 and 348 K than at 298 K. The abnormal larger distance (>4 A) between Thr4O (OGl) and NAD+ (NO2)' at 298 K must result in an increase in the energy barrier for proton transfer from EtOH.
