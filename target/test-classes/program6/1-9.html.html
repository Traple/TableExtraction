Due to the higher costs of polymer-bound cofactors such as 12, and the sometimes increased KM values, the TTN should be at least two to three times higher compared to the native cofactor 3. Extremely high values have been reached (up to 600,000) (Hummel et al., 1987). However, it is worth mentioning that the reduction of cofactor costs by TTN by more than 5000 is of more academic than economic interest.

CONCLUSIONS

High total turnover numbers can be easily achieved with polymer-enlarged cofactors if they are available. The feasibility of this approach has been shown for NAD" and ATP bound to polyethylene glycol. With native cofactors quite high cycle numbers of up to 4500 can be reached with charged ultraﬁltration membranes (Rothig et al., 1990). Alternatively, due to their higher retention of low-molecular-weight products nanoﬁltra- tion membranes can be used to increase the TTN (Seelbach and Kragl, unpublished results). The differ- ent solubility of substrates, products, and cofactors, respectively, can be used to increase TTN when the latter can be recirculated in a water phase of constant volume. When substrates are highly soluble in water there is no need to retain or recirculate the cofactors to reach reasonably high total turnover numbers. The

results given here clearly demonstrate that for cofactor- dependent processes—at least in the case of NAD+ or ATP—the cofactors costs are no longer economically limiting. Also, for more expensive cofactors (e.g., NADP+ ), enzyme reaction engineering will help make appropriate processes economically feasible.
