A 96-kDa E. histolytica membrane antigen (Torian et al., 1990) was later identiﬁed as ADH2 or its isoform by its reac- tivity with several monoclonal antibodies (Flores et al., 1996). Fourteen percent of this protein was found in the detergent phase of Triton X-114; it was located on ameba surfaces and in plasma and internal membrane preparations (Torian et al., 1990), raising the possibility that the NAD+-dependent ADH or its isoform could be a membrane enzyme (Flores et al., 1996). In the present work, the enzymatic activity is observed in in- ternal membrane fractions; however, the activity was more en- riched in the 160,000-g pellet, which has very few membrane vesicles. Negative staining of internal membrane fractions sug- gested that NAD+-dependent ADH is not a membrane enzyme, but a multimeric one. Moreover, in the preparations of. internal membranes, the gold-labeled antibody bound to a material pres- ent around membrane preparations, but in most cases, it did not bind to membrane bilayers; by negative staining, round struc- tures were observed, which possibly correspond to protomers of the rodlike enzyme, similar to that observed in E. coli (Ma- tayoshi et al., 1989). These results strongly suggest that the major NAD+-dependent ADH activity does not correspond to a real membrane enzyme; however, it could cosediment with membrane preparations. Otherwise, it is possible that a minor isoform of ADH2 could be a membrane enzyme.
