Neurospora is a representative multicellular ﬁlamentous fungus and a model microorganism to study the relationship between genes and proteins and the role of specific genes on cellular performance. Recent advancement on sequenc- ing of the Neurospora genome has increased the potential for use in the biotechnology area (Galagan et al. 2003). Its application in food processing revealed that Neurospora sp. isolated from bejiu, a traditional fermented food in Brazil,

NCBI Blastp searching of alcohol dehydro- genases from N. crassa with S. cerevisiae ADH] peptide sequence showed that N. crassa might have two alcohol dehydrogenases, ADHI and ADH3, with a homology to yeast ADHs.

In this study, N. crassa ADHI and ADH3 genes were cloned and expressed in recombinant Escherichia coli. The characterization of alcohol dehydrogenase activity and carboxylate ester formation was carried out with the crude ADH] and ADH3 solutions.

Materials and methods Strains, plasmids, and culture conditions

N crassa FGSC2489 grown in yeast extract-mannitol medium (0.5% peptone, 0.3% yeast extract, and 1% glucose) and E. coli TOP10 (Invitrogen, Carlsbad, CA) cultured in Luria Broth medium (Park et al. 2006) were used as a source for the ADH genes and as a host strain for ADH expression, respectively. Strains and plasmids used and constructed in this study are listed in Table 1. Cultures in a shaking incubator (Model G76, New Brunswick Scientific, New Jersey) were performed at 200 rpm and 30°C for N crassa and 37°C for E. coli.

Cloning and expression of ADH genes in E. coli

cDNA from mRNA collected with the SV total RNA isolation kit (Promega, Madison, WI) was synthesized with the Cloned AMV First-Strand cDNA synthesis kit (Invitrogen, USA). Two DNA oligomers to amplify the ADH] gene were designed as 5’-CGGGATCCATGCCT CAGTTCGAGATTCC-3' (NCA15B) and 5 '- CGGGA TCCCTATTTGCTGGTATCGACGAC-3' (NCA13B).
