1. On- and off-velocity constants for NADH and NAD+ binding to liver alcohol dehydrogenase in the pH range 10— 12 have been determined by stopped-ﬂow kinetic methods. The results are consistent with previously reported equilibrium binding data and proposals attributing the main effects of pH on coenzyme binding to

ionization of Lys-228 and zinc-bound water.

2. Deprotonation of the group identiﬁed as Lys-228 decreases the NADH and NAD+ association rates by a factor exceeding 20 and has no detectable effect on the coenzyme dissociation rates in the examined pH range.

3. Ionization of the group identiﬁed as zinc-bound water causes a 3-fold increase of the rate of NADH dissociation from the enzyme, and decreases the rate of NAD + dissociation by a factor of 200. The NADH and NAD+ association rates are decreased by a factor of 30 and 5, respectively.

4. The observed effects of pH can be rationalized in terms of electrostatic interactions of the ionizing groups with the charges present on the coenzyme molecules and lend support to the idea that binding of the coenzyme nicotinamide ring occurs subsequent to binding of the AMP portion of the coenzyme.

The affinity of liver alcohol dehydrogenase for its coenzymes NADH and NAD+ is strongly dependent on the ionization state of the water molecule which is bound at the active-site zinc ion of the enzyme subunit [1 — 3]. Deprotona- tion of this water molecule occurs with a pK, of 9.2 in free enzyme, shifted to 7.6 in the presence of bound NADJ’ and to 11.2 in the presence of bound NADH. These pK, shifts and consequent effects of pH on the stability of the enzyme- coenzyme complexes appear to be of electrostatic origin and to derive mainly from coulombic interactions of the ionizing group with the charges present on the coenzyme molecules [3, 4].

The detailed mechanism by which the protonation state of zinc-bound water affects the coenzyme binding process remains obscure, however. Kinetic pH-dependence studies have provided evidence that ionization of zinc-bound water decreases the NADT dissociation rate as well as the NADH and NAD “L association rates, but no information is available as to the absolute or relative strength of these effects. The present investigation was undertaken to obtain such informa- tion and reports kinetic data for coenzyme binding in the pH range 10 — 12 where enzyme species containing a zinc-bound hydroxide ion might be expected to show detectable reactivity.
