Substrate speciﬁcity of mutated ADH I

While the AADH] strain BY474l (EUROSCARF, Frankfurt am Main, Germany) was conveniently used to identify transformants carrying the mutated ADH] gene, a conventional laboratory strain was used for assessing the physiological effects of the mutated Adhl enzyme. Plasmid YEplacHXT-AD- H1-mut, carrying the mutated ADH] gene, was extracted from S. cerevisiae BY4741 transformants and used to transform S. cerevisiae CENPK 113- 5D, resulting in strain TMB3206. In parallel, the same strain was transformed with the control plas- mid YEplac-HXT lacking the structural mutated Adhl gene, resulting in strain TMB3280. NADH-dependent reductase activity was mea- sured with HMF, furfural and acetaldehyde as substrates in cell extracts from the control strain TMB3280 and the strain TMB3206 overexpress- ing mutated ADH] gene (henceforth referred to as the mut-Adhl strain), as well as from the origi- nal strain TMB3000 (Table 4). As expected, there was no NADH reductase activity with HMF in the control strain. An increase in speciﬁc activ- ity against HMF was observed for mut-Adhl strain when compared with the original strain.

Growth and product formation

The in vivo effect of the overexpressed mutated ADH] gene was evaluated by growing control and mut-Adhl strains in aerated shake-ﬂask cultures on glucose and in the presence or absence of 20 mM HMF. The results are presented in Figure 1.

Similar fermentative proﬁles were obtained for the two strains in the absence of HMF in the medium (Figure 1A, B). Both strains produced acetate to 0.3 g/l after glucose depletion but started consuming it after 22 h. Glycerol production levels differed slightly, mut-Adhl ending with 1.3 g/l, while the control strain produced 1.5 g/l. The semi-aerobic growth in shake-ﬂasks enabled the accumulation of ethanol, its ﬁnal concentration being 16.9 g/l for both strains without HMF.

Both strains were signiﬁcantly affected by the presence of HMF. However, the two strains adopted different growth and end product patterns. Substantial growth was detected for mut-Adhl, but only after the HMF level has decreased below 0.5 g/l, and HMF was totally reduced after 22 h. In contrast, little growth (OD620 : 3.5) was observed for the control strain. In this strain, 20% of the added HMF level was still left at the end of the experiment.

When comparing mut-Adhl in the presence and absence of HMF, acetate production (0.56 g/l) was higher in the presence of HMF and acetate consumption during stationary phase could not be detected. On the other hand, the glycerol level was signiﬁcantly lower (0.66 g/l) in the experiment with HMF added. Final ethanol concentrations in the growth medium were very similar.
