RESULTS

Alcohol dehydrogenase from horse liver has been shown to catalyze hydrolysis of p-nitrophenyl octanoate (NPO). Its kinetic studies can be performed only at low sub- strate to enzyme ratio under non-Michae- lian condition for following reasons: (a) low esterase activity necessitates the use of high enzyme concentrations, (b) low substrate solubility in aqueous solution precludes the use of high substrate con- centrations, and (c) substrate inhibition limits substrate concentrations within noninhibitory range, i.e., lower than 200 pM.
