Serum samples were taken before treatment from 45 women (49—71 years old) with breast cancer (carcinoma ductale invasivum) and 45 healthy women (51-67 years old). None of the patients had received chemo- or radiotherapy before the blood samples were collected.

Pretreatment staging procedures included physical and blood examinations, mammography, mammary ultrasound scanning, breast core biopsies, chest X-ray, and computed tomography (CT). Postoperative patho- logical staging was performed according to surgical and histological ﬁndings. Tumors were classiﬁed and staged in accordance with the International Union Against Cancer tumor-node metastasis (UICC-TNM) classiﬁca- tion. The patients were divided into four groups: stage I (n = 12), stage II (n = 10), stage III (n = 12), and stage IV (n = 11). Of the patients in stage IV, six had bone metastasis, three had liver metastasis, and two had lung metastasis). All of the patients drank alcohol occasionally.

Total ADH activity was measured by the photometric method with p-nitrosodimethylaniline (NDMA) as a substrate, and ALDH activity was measured by the ﬁuorometric method with 6-methoxy-2-naphtaldehyde (oxidation). To measure the activity of class I and II isoenzymes we employed ﬁuorometric methods with class-speciﬁc ﬁuorogenic substrates: 4-methoxy-l-naph- taldehyde for class I, and 6-methoxy-2-naphtaldehyde for class II (reduction) (8). The activity of classes III and IV of ADH was measured by the photometric method with n-octanol and m-nitrobenzaldehyde as a substrates,

respectively. The assays were performed as previously described (8,9). Statistical analysis was performed using Wilcoxon’s test and Spearman’s correlation coefﬁcients. Differences were considered signiﬁcant at P< 0.05.

The protocol was approved by the Human Care Committee of the Medical University of Bialystok (approval Nr R-I-003/85/2004). All of the patients gave informed consent for the examination.

RESULTS

The activities of total ADH, ALDH, and ADH isoenzymes in the sera are demonstrated in Table 1. The total ADH activity was not signiﬁcantly higher (about 6.3%) in patients with breast cancer than in healthy controls. The analysis of ALDH activity did not indicate a signiﬁcant difference between the patient group and healthy controls.

The comparison of ADH isoenzymes activities showed that the highest difference was exhibited by class I ADH. The mean activity of this class of isoenzymes in the total cancer group increased about 7.1% (1.28610.48 mU/L) in comparison to the control level (1.19510.56 mU/L). This increase was not statis- tically signiﬁcant. Other tested ADH isoenzymes also did not exhibit marked differences in activities between the patient and control groups.
