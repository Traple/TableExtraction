DISCUSSION

Breast cancer is one of the most frequently occurring aggressive neoplasms. Early detection of cancer in this organ is very important. Therefore, it is important to Ô¨Ånd markers that may be helpful for diagnosing breast cancer. Some authors have demonstrated ADH and ALDH activities in the cells of healthy parenchyma (10). In a previous study (unpublished results) we found that the activity of total ADH was lower (insigniÔ¨Åcantly) in breast cancer cells than in healthy tissues, but the activity of class I ADH was signiÔ¨Åcantly lower. There- fore, the activity of this isoenzyme in the case of breast cancer is contrary to that of other cancers. For example, the activities of total ADH and class I of ADH are signiÔ¨Åcantly higher in colorectal cancer than in healthy colon mucosa (11). Therefore, the activity of class I ADH isoenzymes is signiÔ¨Åcantly elevated in the sera of patients with colorectal cancer (unpublished results). The present study demonstrates that the activity of class I ADH was insigniÔ¨Åcantly higher in the sera of patients with breast cancer than in healthy controls. However, the majority of the patients with colorectal cancer were drinkers, and the patients with breast cancer drank alcohol occasionally. The serum activity of class I ADH isoenzymes appeared to have a tendency to increase in accordance with the advance of disease. The activity of ADH I was signiÔ¨Åcantly higher in the sera of patients with stage IV breast cancer compared to healthy controls. Tumors were classiÔ¨Åed and staged in accor- dance with the UICC-TNM classiÔ¨Åcation system. Of the patients in stage IV, six had bone metastasis, three had liver metastasis, and two had lung metastasis.

The pretreatment staging procedures included chest X-ray and CT. The activities of enzymes (ALT and AST) and bilirubin concentration, tested as markers of liver lesions were signiÔ¨Åcantly elevated. Class I ADH isoenzymes are expressed mainly in the liver, but they are also present in lungs (12). The elevated activity of class I isoenzymes in our study apparently was caused by the enzyme released from metastatic cells or organ

These suppositions are in accordance with those of Chrostek and Szmitkowski (14), who showed increased ADH I activity in the sera of patients with metastatic liver tumors. The other isoenzymes did not exhibit any characteristic changes of activity in patients in various advancing stages of tumor. The increase of serum total ADH in the course of breast cancer is probably due to an elevation of class I ADH iso- enzymes. In a previous study we showed that the differences of other tested classes of ADH isoenzymes activities (except ADH I) between cancer cells and healthy parenchyma were not statistically signiÔ¨Åcant. In conclusion, we can state that the activity of class I ADH isoenzymes was elevated in the sera of patients with stage IV breast cancer. We believe the observed changes resulted from isoenzymes being released from the organ damaged by metastatic disease, rather than from secondary tumors.
