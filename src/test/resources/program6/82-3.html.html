CONCLUSIONS

From the present kinetic study of the peroxidatic reaction, catalyzed by horse liver alcohol dehydro- genase, the emerging mechanism appears to be of sequential ordered. type. In the absence of a direct estimation of the reversible afﬁnity of the enzyme active site towards H202 and of product inhibition studies, one can only reasonably argue, in analogy with what happens with alcohols, that a plausible ordered pathway should pass through the enzyme- coenzyme binary complex. As already described [2], hydrogen peroxide reacts with NADl to give a co- enzyme derivative, called compound I; the process takes place in the same enzyme active site where ethanol also reacts, as directly deduced from compe- titive inhibition exhibited by ethanol towards hydrogen peroxide during the peroxidatic reaction. Hydrogen peroxide is, however, able to inactivate the enzyme by oxidation of its functional cysteine residues. This speciﬁcity of hydrogen peroxide has been demon- strated by the protective effect brought about by NADl on both activity and cysteine residues of en- zyme.
