Conclusions. Comparison of the gene and protein sequences from strictly related thermophilic microorganisms may consti- tute an effective approach to investigating the properties re- sponsible for stability under extreme conditions. In fact, only 17 amino acid replacements are responsible for the dilferent thermal activities and substrate speciﬁcities of the ADHs com- pared. Interestingly, 6 of these replacements are clustered in a very narrow stretch (14 to 26), and, more generally, 7 of 17 are located in the amino-terminal region (positions 14 to 77). The catalytic efficiency and substrate speciﬁcity of medium-chain alcohol dehydrogenases are highly inﬂuenced by amino-termi- nal residues, especially when charge introductions or shifts occur (1, l3-l3b), as in this case.

The prediction of segmental ﬂexibility With normalized B values (l4) and the comparative secondary structure potential of individual amino acids clearly indicated that all seven amino acids in RC3ADH contribute to decreased ﬂexibility and po- tential of coil-ﬂexible turn formation, with a greater tendency toward well-deﬁned secondary structure organization. It seems reasonable to argue that these local diﬂerences can be respon- sible for the diiferent thermoactivation levels and substrate aiﬁnities of the two Sulfolobus enzymes.

This study has shown that the signiﬁcant sequence similarity, the various substrate speciﬁcities, and the distinct thermal fea- tures of the ADHs from different thermophilic and, particu- larly, archaeal organisms, together can be considered helpful criteria in recognizing and distinguishing the archaeal sources themselves.
