Snapshots of states 1 and 3 extracted from the MD trajectories are presented in stereoview (Figure 5). The hydride equivalent transfer distance between HD and C4 is primarily determined by the relative position of the Zn” catalytic site and the NAD+ cofactor. The conformation of the NAD+ cofactor is largely determined by the cofactor domain. As shown in Figure 5, the three residues within van der Waals contact of the NAD+ cofactor are Leul76, Thr237, and Val260. They are included in fragments A—C, respec- tively, as described. The distances between CD1 of residue Leul76 and C5 of NAD+ are 3.55 and 4.06 A in states 1 and 3, respectively. Those for Thr237 (CG2) and NAD+ (O4') are 3.64 and 3.81 A, respectively. Larger distances in state 3 indicate weaker interactions between NAD+ and Leul76, as do NAD+ and Thr237.

The amide group of Val260 forms a stable hydrogen bond with the amide group of nicotinamide, and the hydrophobic side chain of Val260 is in contact with the C2 atom of the nicotinamide ring. A major difference between the two conformations of nicotinamide from states 1 and 3 is in the dihedral angles, X1 (N7—C7—C3—C2) and X2 (H7l—N7— C7—C3) (Chart 2). X1 is 17° and 28° and X2 3° and 16° for states 1 and 2, respectively. This difference is due to the nicotinamide ring being pushed toward the ligated EtOi The pushing motion is expressed through the hydrogen bond between the Val260 amide group and the nicotinamide amide group, as well as the Val260 side chain which is within van der Waals distance of the nicotinamide ring. State 3 is created by expanding the cofactor-binding pocket at lower temper- atures. This expansion changes the distance between the CG1 atom of Val260 and the C2 atom of the nicotinamide ring from 3.71 to 4.30 A. Simultaneously, the hydrogen bond between the 2'-ribose hydroxyl group of NAD+ and the hydroxyl group of Thr40 present in state 1 is disrupted in state 3. As the nicotinamide ring moves away, the interstice between nicotinamide and the Zn” catalytic site becomes larger. The hydride equivalent transfer distance between HD of the substrate and C4 of the nicotinamide is expanded from 2.55 to 5.18 A.

The substrate intermediate (EtO_) and the three side chain heteroatoms of the residues, Cys38—S_, Cys138—S_, and His61 (NE2), are coordinated with the Zn” ion in a distorted tetrahedral configuration (Figure 5). The partial charge of the cysteine thiolate (Cys-S‘) was set to —0.80 (33), and the partial charge of oxygen (O1) in EtO_ is optimized to be —0.91 by a procedure described in Methods. The experimental (1) and theoretical distances between the Zn” ion and side chain heteroatoms and hydroxyl oxygen are in good agreement (Table S1 of the Supporting Information).
