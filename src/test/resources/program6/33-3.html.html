Construction of strains TMB3206 and TMB3280

Strain CEN.PK 113-5D was transformed with plas- mid YEplac-HXT-ADH1-mut or with the control plasmid YEplac-HXT (Karhumaa er al., 2005) and transformants were selected on SD — ura plates. The resulting strains were named TMB3206 and TMB3280, respectively.

Growth experiments

Strains were grown overnight in 5 ml deﬁned medium (Verduyn er al., 1992) supplemented with 4O g/l glucose at 200 r.p.m. and 30°C. Deﬁned medium (100 ml) in buffered 1 l ﬂasks with and without 20 mM HMF was inoculated to OD620 = 0.1 and incubated at 200 r.p.m. and 30 °C for 31 h.

Analyses

Cell concentration was determined from absorbance measurements at 620 nm, calibrated against dry- weight measurements from duplicate samples. For dry-weight, 10 ml cell suspension was ﬁltered through a pre-weighed Gelman ﬁlter (diameter 47 mm, Supor-450, 0.45 um). After washing with three volumes of water, the ﬁlters were dried in a microwave oven (350 W, 8 min; ﬁlters pre-dried for 4 min) and weighed.

Samples for metabolite analysis were imme- diately centrifuged and ﬁltered (MFS Advan- tec 16 mm, 0.20 um). Glucose, ethanol, glyc- erol, acetic acid and HMF were determined by HPLC (Waters, Milford, MA, USA), using an Aminex HPX-87H ion-exchange column (Bio-Rad,

Results

Identiﬁcation of NADH-dependent HMF reductase

S. cerevisiae strain TMB3000 was grown in shake ﬂasks in the presence of 10% spruce hydrolysate. Cytosolic proteins were extracted and fraction- ated using, in the following order, ammonium sulphate precipitation, size exclusion and afﬁnity chromatography (Table 2). For each puriﬁcation step, the fractions showing the highest NADH- dependent HMF reductase activity were used for following puriﬁcation step. After the afﬁnity chro- matography step, a single band corresponding to a molecular mass of 37 kDa was obtained on SDS-PAGE. The puriﬁed protein was identiﬁed as S. cerevisiae Adhl, using electrospray ionization mass spectrometry (ESI-MS).

Cloning the ADHI gene from TMB3000

The ADHI gene from TMB3000 was ampli- ﬁed by PCR from genomic DNA and cloned into the YEplac-HXT vector (Karhumaa er al., 2005) carrying the strong constitutive truncated HXT promoter (Hauf er al., 2000). Plasmid DNA from several E. coli transformants was used to transform AADH] S. cerevisiae strain BY4741 (EUROSCARF, Frankfurt am Main, Germany).
