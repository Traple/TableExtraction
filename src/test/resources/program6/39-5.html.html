DISCUSSION

The group III ADHs include mainly Fe-containing and Fe- activated enzymes. Fe-containing ADHs still contain Fe after puriﬁcation, such as the ADHs from E. 001i ECL1 (Montella et 011., 2005), Thermotoga hypogea DSM 11164 (Ying et 011., 2007), Thermotoga m01ritim01 strain MSB8 (Schwarzenbacher et 011., 2004) and Thermococcus strain ES- 1 (Ma et 011., 1995), as well as the recombinant ADH from Thermococcus hydrothermalis strain AL662T (Antoine et 011., 1999). Fe-activated ADHs do not contain Fe after puriﬁcation, but the ion must be present in the assay mixture to obtain ADH activity, as seen for the ADH2 from Z. mobilis ZM4 (Bakshi et 011., 1989; Scopes, 1983). In a few cases, other metals instead of Fe have been detected, such as a Zn- and Mg-containing methanol dehydrogenase from Bacillus methanolicus C1 (Vonck et 011., 1991) and a Zn- dependent glycerol dehydrogenase from G. stearothermo- philus var. non-diastaticus (Ruzheinikov et 011., 2001). Based on observations that metals other than Fe may also bind to the group III proteins, some authors proposed to rename the Fe-dependent ADHs the group III metal-dependent polyol dehydrogenases (Ruzheinikov et 011., 2001). In our case, ADH1 and ADH2 contain neither Fe nor other metals after puriﬁcation, and their activities are not dependent on any metals, suggesting a new type of ADH in this group.
