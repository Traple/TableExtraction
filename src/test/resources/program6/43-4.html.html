Figure 4(a) indicates that Octyl-Sepharose-bound LADH and soluble LADH have the same deactivation kinetics whereas LADH immobilized to CNBr-Sepharose is more thermally stable. The results for the soluble and Octyl- Sepharose preparations were combined as shown and a two-component, first-order deactivation model was used to ﬁt the data:

A(t)/AO = a, exp(—k1t) + a2 exp(—k2t) (1)

where A0 is the total enzyme activity at time zero and a, and a2 are the initial fractions of enzyme components 1 and 2, respectively. Using the above formalism, component 1 was found to comprise 70% total enzyme and was character- ized by a much smaller deactivation rate constant than com- ponent 2 (Table I). On the other hand, the best fit for the CNBr-Sepharose sample was obtained by assuming a single component with a rate constant similar to that of compo- nent 1 of the soluble and Octyl-Sepharose samples.
