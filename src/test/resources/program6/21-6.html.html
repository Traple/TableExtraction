The apparent value of Km for the immobilized SCAD was 1.54 times higher than that for the free enzyme. This is a typical phenomenon generally observed for immobilized enzymes, mainly due to the mass transport resis- tance to the substrate into the porous carrier. The Vmax value of immobilized enzyme was 2.05 times lower than that of free enzyme. The immobilized SCAD retained 48.77% of enzymatic activity compared to the free enzyme. This might be due to the ﬁxation of SCAD molecules and/or the change in the property of active sites for the binding of NADH and phenylglyoxylic acid after enzyme immobilization. Compared to the result by Chen and Liao [l3,24], the immobilization process increased more SCAD afﬁnity to 2-butanone than to phenylglyoxylic acid as substrate because of phenylglyoxylic acid has big interspace block. At the same time, the immobilization process hindered more the substrate’s accessibility of the SCAD active site. There- fore the immobilized SCAD retained 48.77% of enzymatic activity compared to the free enzyme using phenylglyoxylic acid as substrate while the bound yeast dehydrogenase retained 62% of its original activity using 2-butanone as substrate.

3.6. Thermal stability measurement

The thermal stability of free and immobilized SCAD was determined by incubation of the enzymes in temperature range of 40-70 0C for 60 min and measurement of their residual activ- ity. The data in Fig. 8 showed that both free and immobilized enzyme shared similar thermal stability proﬁles below 60 0C. However, the residual activities of immobilized enzyme were consistently higher than that of the free enzyme when the incu- bation temperatures were above 60 0C. For free SCAD, almost no activity was retained when incubation temperature was above 70 0C. However, the activity of immobilized SCAD remained 18% activity above 70 0C. Compared the data for free and immo- bilized enzyme, the thermal stability of SCAD was improved after immobilization on the magnetic F63 O4-chitosan nanopar- ticles. 3. 7. Reasabilin/

The duration of a catalyst is an important feature for its potential application in industry. To investigate the reusability of immobilized SCAD, several repetitive use of immobilized SCAD were operated. After each cycle, the immobilized SCAD was recovered by magnetic separation and recycled for the reduction of phenylglyoxylic acid. The activity of the ﬁrst batch was taken as 100%. The assay condition remained the same for all batches. The activity of the immobilized SCAD in the repeated use did not decrease signiﬁcantly. Within 7 cycles of usage, the remaining activity was about l00, 89.15, 79.42, 69.50, 62.80, 56.48, and 48.26% of the ﬁrst use. The results indicated that the immobilized SCAD on the magnetic nanoparticles had a good durability and magnetic recovery.

4. Conclusions

Magnetic Fe3O4-chitosan nanoparticles with amino group were prepared by the covalent binding of chitosan onto the surface of magnetic Fe3O4 nanoparticles. SCAD was success- fully immobilized on the superparamagnetic F63 O4-chitosan nanoparticles via glutaraldehyde coupling reaction. The immo- bilized enzyme molecules retained their enzymatic activity after immobilization. The immobilized SCAD retained 48.77% activ- ity and exhibited a signiﬁcantly improved stability than the free enzyme. For the reduction of phenylglyoxylic acid by immo- bilized SCAD, the maximum speciﬁc activity, the Michaelis constants and the activation energy were 279.33 nmol min_1, 37.77 mM and 7.79 KJ mol_1, respectively.
