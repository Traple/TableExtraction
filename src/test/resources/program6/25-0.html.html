The aldehyde/alcohol dehydrogenase (AdhE) in relation to the ethanol formation in T hermoanaerobacter ethanolicus JW200

Hui Pengab, Guogan Wua, Weilan Shaoa’*

Abstract

A bifunctional aldehyde/alcohol dehydrogenase gene (adhE) from T herrnoanaerobacter ethanolicus JW200 was identiﬁed and cloned. To unambiguously characterize the activity of AdhE, the recombinant protein was puriﬁed. The puriﬁed AdhE exhibited high enzymatic activity attributed to aldehyde dehydrogenase (l1.0i0.3 U/mg) and low alcohol dehydrogenase activity (2.6i0.2 U/mg). Analysis of adhE homologous expression in T. ethanolicus showed that AdhE affected ethanol production.

1. Introduction

T hermoanaerobacter ethanolicus is a gram-positive anaerobic thermophile producing considerable amounts of ethanol from readily available substrates such as cellulose and starch [1]. The especially high rate of ethanol production makes T. ethanolicus an attractive candidate for use in bioconversion processes [2]. But this potential has not been demonstrated because of low product yields caused by ethanol inhibition of bacterial growth. The low tolerance to ethanol is related to a speciﬁc inhibition of enzymes involved in sugar metabo- lism. Two alcohol dehydrogenase (Adh) from T. ethanoli- cas 39E have been reported to be involved in ethanol metabolism, one is optimized for ethanol consumption (AdhA) and another is optimized for its formation (AdhB) [3].

AdhE has been demonstrated to be the key enzyme responsible for ethanol production in several alcohologenic strains, such as Lactococcus lactis, Clostri- diam acetobutylicum, and Escherichia coli [4—6]. If AdhE is present in T. ethanolicus, it could play an important role in controlling the metabolic ﬂow of ethanol.

In this study, we cloned and identiﬁed a gene of T. ethanolicus JW200 (ATCC 31550) and found it to encode AdhE. To our knowledge, this is the ﬁrst report of an adhE gene in an anaerobic gram-positive thermophilic bacterium. The AdhE was shown to be responsible for the ethanol production by homologous expression in T. ethanolicus JW200.

A 872-residue protein annotated as an iron-containing Adh with 48% amino acid sequence identity was revealed.
