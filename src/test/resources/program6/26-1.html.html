2. Materials and methods 2.1. Materials

T roseum (A.T.C.C. 27502) was obtained from Korean Culture Center of Microorganisms in freeze-dried form. Adenosine 5’-monophosphate, glycine, NAD+ and standard native molecular weight marker were purchased from Sigma (St Louis, MO, USA). Mono Q, Mono P, Polybuffer 74 and Superdex 200HR were obtained from Phar- macia Biotech (Uppsala, Sweden). Protein-assay reagent and SDS-PAGE molecular mass markers were purchased from Bio-Rad (Hercules, CA, USA). All reagents used were of the highest grade available commercially.

2.2. Enzyme purification

Cells of T roseum were grown with shaking in thermobacterium medium (0.1% yeast extract, 0.1% bactotrypton, and 1% Allen’s basal salt, pH 8.4) at 7O °C for 3 days as described previously (Kong et al., 2000). The cells were harvested by centrifugation at 8000>< g for l0 min and then were suspended in 20 mM potassium phosphate buffer contained 2 mM B-mercaptoethanol, pH 7.0 (buffer A). The cells were subjected to sonication using an ultrasonic processor (Sonics and Materi- als, Danbury, CT, USA) until lyzed. The disrupted cell suspension was centrifuged at 100 000>< g for 90 min, yielding the crude extracts. The superna- tant was dialyzed against buffer A and loaded on to an adenosine 5 ’-monophosphate affinity column (l.2>< 10 cm) previously equilibrated in buffer A. After washing the column with buffer A, bound proteins were eluted with 300 mM NaCl in buffer A at a ﬂow rate of 0.3 ml / min, followed with 0.1 mM NAD+, and subsequently eluted with 0.1 mM NAD+ and 1.1 mM pyrazole in buffer A at the same ﬂow rate. Fractions containing enzyme activ- ity were pooled, dialyzed against buffer A, and loaded on to a Mono-Q HR 5 / 5 column equili- brated in buffer A. The column was washed with buffer A and bound proteins were eluted with a linear gradient of 0—500 mM NaCl in buffer A at a ﬂow rate of 0.5 ml / min. Active fractions were pooled and dialyzed against buffer A. This purified

enzyme was used for the next experiment. Unless otherwise indicated, all purification procedures were performed either at 4 °C or on ice. The enzymes were stored at —70 °C until use.

2.3. Enzyme assay and kinetic studies

Enzyme activity was determined at 50 °C by spectrophotometrically monitoring, at 340 nm as described by Lumeng et al. (1979). Brieﬂy, the reaction mixtures contained 2.4 mM NAD, 33 mM ethanol, 100 mM glycine buffer (pH 8.8 or 10.5) and 5—50 ul of enzyme solution in a total volume of 1 ml. Non-enzymic reaction rates served as controls were subtracted from the reaction rates in the presence of enzyme. Molar absorbance coeffi- cient of NADH was 6220 M / cm and one unit of enzyme activity was defined as the amount of enzyme that catalyzed the generation of 1 umol NADH / min under the above experimental condi- tions. Specific activity was expressed as units per milligram of protein. Kinetic parameters were determined by the plot method of Lineweaver and Burk (1934). The enzyme concentration used for initial-rate studies was 5—50 ug / ml and the con- centration of ethanol was varied over the range 1- 50 mM. The protein concentration of the enzyme was determined by the method of Bradford (1976) using BSA as the standard.

2.4. Inhibition studies

The inhibitory effects on the activity of the enzyme were measured by preincubating the enzyme with each inhibitor at 50 °C for 2 min and initiating the reaction by addition of 33 mM ethanol. The remaining activity was determined by the assay procedure. The concentration of the inhibitor giving 50% inhibition (I50) was deter- mined from the plot of residual activity against inhibitor concentration.

2.5. Molecular-size determination

The purified enzyme was applied to a Superdex 200 HR FPLC column (1.0><30 cm) equilibrated with buffer A to estimate the molecular size. Molecular-size standards used were blue dextran, B-amylase, alcohol dehydrogenase, BSA, carbonic anhydrase and cytochrome c (Sigma).
