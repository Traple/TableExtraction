Materials and methods

The protocol was approved by the Human Care Committee of the Medical University in Bialystok, Poland (Approval Nr R-I-002 / 59/ 2008). All patients gave an informed consent for the examination.

Material

The study material consisted of abdominal aorta aneurysm wall fragments obtained from 45 patients undergoing elective surgery operations, including 28 men and 17 women aged 55-81 years. Comparative material consisted of unchanged aorta obtained from 45 patients (48-55 years old) donors of kidney transplantation, including 25 men and 2O women.

Preparation of tissue extracts

All specimens were frozen at -80 °C until analysis within two weeks. The examined tissues (1:4 w/ v) were homogenized in potassium phosphate buffer (0.1 M; pH 7.4) for 20 s using ultrasoniﬁcator (Sonoplus HD 70, Bandelin, Germany) and centrifuged at 14000 rpm for 30 min. with cooling to 4 °C. The supernatant was used to determine the ADH and ALDH activities.

Methods

Determination of total ADH activity

Total ADH activity was estimated by the photometric method with p-nitrosodimethylaniline (NDMA) as a substrate (Skursky et al. 1979). The reaction mixture (2 mL) contained supernatant (0.1 mL), 1.8 mL of a 26 uM solution of substrate in 0.1 M of sodium phosphate buffer, pH 8.5 and 0.1 mL of mixture containing 0.25 M n-butanol and 5 mM NAD. The reduction of NDMA was monitored at 440 nm on a Shimadzu UV/ VIS 1202 spectrophotometer (Shimadzu Europa GmbH, Duisburg, Germany).

Determination of total ALDH activity

Aldehyde dehydrogenase activity was measured using the ﬂuoro- genic method based on the oxidation of 6-methoxy-2-naphtaldehyde to the ﬂuorescent 6-methoxy-2-naphtoate (jelski et al. 2004). The reaction mixture contained 60 uL of supernatant, 60 uL of substrate, 20 uL of 11.4 mM NAD and 2.8 mL of 50 mM of sodium phosphate buffer, pH 8.5. The mixture contained also 50 uL of a 12 mM solution of 4-methylpyrazole as a speciﬁc inhibitor of ADH activity. The ﬂuorescence was read at an excitation wavelength of 310 and an emission wavelength of 360 nm on a Shimadzu RF-5301 spectro- ﬂuorophotometer (Shimadzu Europa GmbH, Duisburg, Germany).

Determination of class I and II ADH isoenzymes

Class I and II ADH isoenzyme activities were measured using ﬂuorogenic substrates (4-methoxy-1-naphthaldehyde for class I and 6-methoxy-2-naphthaldehyde for class II) in reduction reaction according to Wierzchowski et al. (1989). The assays were performed in a reaction mixture containing a supernatant (60 uL), substrate (150 uL of 300 ulVI), NADH (100 uL of1 mM) and 0.1 M of sodium phosphate buffer, pH 7.6 (2.69 mL) in conditions previously described (Chrostek et al. 2003). The measurements were performed on a Shimadzu RF-5301 spectroﬂuorophotometer at excitation wavelength of 316 nm for both substrates and emission of 370 nm for class I and 360 nm for class II isoenzymes.

Determination of class III ADH isoenzyme

The assay mixture for class III alcohol dehydrogenase contained a supernatant (100 uL), formaldehyde as a substrate (100 uL of 1 mM), glutathione (100 pL of 1 mM) and NAD (240 uL of 1.2 mM) in 0.1 mol NaOH-pyrophosphate buffer pH 8.0 (Koivusalo et al. 1989). The ﬁnal

volume was 2 mL. The reduction of NAD was monitored at 340 nm and 25 °C on a Shimadzu UV/VIS 1202 spectrophotometer. The ﬁnal results were expressed in mU/ mg protein (nM of NADH formed per min per mg of protein).

Determination of class IVADH isoenzyme

The assay mixture for class IV of ADH activity contained a supernatant (50 pL), m-nitrobenzaldehyde as a substrate (132 uL of 80 pM) and NADH (172 uL of 86 uM) in 0.1 M sodium phosphate buffer pH 7.5 (Dohmen et al. 1996). The oxidation of NADH was monitored at 340 nm and 25 °C on a Shimadzu UV/VIS 1202 spectrophotometer. The ﬁnal results were expressed in milliunits per milligram of protein (nanomoles of NADH formed per minute per milligram of protein).

Protein assays

Protein concentration was measured by the method of Lowry et al. (1951) using bovine serum albumin as the standard (Sigma Diagnostics, St. Louis, Mo). The assays were performed on a Shimadzu UV/VIS 1202 spectrophotometer.

Statistical analysis

A preliminary statistical analysis (Chi-square test) revealed that the distribution of ADH and ALDH activities did not follow a normal distri- bution. Consequently, the Wilcoxon test was used for statistical analysis. Data were presented as median, range and mean values. Statistically signiﬁcant differences were deﬁned as comparisons resulting in p< 0.05.

Results

The activities of total alcohol dehydrogenase, aldehyde dehydro- genase and ADH isoenzymes in wall of abdominal aortic aneurysm are listed in Table 1. We have shown that ADH and ALDH activities are present in the aneurysm tissues, although ALDH activity was much lower than ADH in all tested patients groups. The comparison of ADH isoenzymes activities showed that the highest activity was exhibited by class I ADH. The median activity of this class was 0.31 nmol/min/ mg protein in aneurysm and 0.46 nmol/ min/ mg protein in wall of normal aorta in total patients group. The activity of class III ADH was about 60% lower and the activity of class IV about 5 times lower than that of class I. The activity of class II ADH isoenzyme was barely detectable. The results for the men and women were similar.

We have found that the activity of class I ADH was signiﬁcantly lower in the wall of aneurysm than in unchanged aortic wall in the total patients group with no difference between men and women. The other tested classes of ADH isoenzymes had lower activities in aneurysms in comparison to healthy wall, but the differences were not statistically signiﬁcant in all patients groups (p>0.05). The total alcohol dehydrogenase and aldehyde dehydrogenase activities were lower in wall of aortic aneurysm than in wall of unchanged aorta, but not signiﬁcantly.

The activity of alcohol dehydrogenase and aldehyde dehydrogen- ase in wall of aneurysm of men did not differ from that in women's specimens (p<0.05). Not signiﬁcant differences in total ADH and ALDH activity between men and women specimen from healthy aorta were found. The comparison of ADH isoenzymes activity between men and women showed that men do not have signiﬁcantly higher activities of any tested isoenzyme compared with women.

Discussion

Our study demonstrates that the highest activity between all tested ADH isoenzymes was exhibited by the class I isoenzyme. This is in agreement with data reported by Allali-Hassani et al. (1997). They showed that this class is responsible for most of ADH activity and is present in all vessels studied.
