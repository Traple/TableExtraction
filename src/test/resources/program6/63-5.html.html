The rate constant for the dissociation of NADH was determined for the H51Q enzyme at pH 8 (Figure 2B). For the wild-type enzyme, the release of NADH is the rate- limiting step for ethanol oxidation with a rate constant of 5.5 s-l (36). The rate constant for dissociation of NADH from the H51Q enzyme is similar to the wild-type enzyme with a rate constant of 4.6 s”. This rate constant is close to the turnover number of about 2 s“ for ethanol oxidation, suggesting that the release of NADH is also a rate-limiting step for ethanol oxidation by the H51Q enzyme.

The transient kinetics of acetaldehyde reduction were determined at pH 8 with 10 ptN H51Q enzyme, 10 yM NADH, and varied concentrations of acetaldehyde. A maximum rate constant of 62 s-l was determined for saturating concentrations of acetaldehyde. This value is comparable to the turnover number of 54 s-l for VZ/Et, which was determined from steady-state kinetics (1%, and is decreased 6-fold as compared to wild-type ADH (8).
