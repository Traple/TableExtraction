Introduction

Kluyveromyces lactis is essentially an aerobic yeast and, despite its poor fermentative capability, four alcohol dehy- drogenase (ADH) genes are present in this organism. KZADH 1 and KZADHZ are constitutive genes encoding the cytoplasmic activities KlAdh1p and KlAdh2p (Saliola et aL, 1990; Shain et aL, 1992), whereas KZADH3 and KZADH4 encode the KlAdh3p and KlAdh4p activities, which are both located within mitochondria. The two proteins have different mitochondrial targeting presequences, but show more than 81% identity in the rest of the molecule. Despite the high sequence conservation, KZADH3 and KZADH4 differ in their promoter regions and are tightly regulated by carbon sources in a different manner. Abstract

KZADH3 and KZADH4 are Kluyveromyces lactis genes encoding the two mitochon- drial alcohol dehydrogenase activities located within mitochondria. In this yeast, ethanol induces the transcription of KZADH4 and, conversely, represses that of KZADH3. In this study, we describe the effects of the aar900 mutation on such regulation. This mutation, ﬁrstly isolated in a strain devoid of alcohol dehydro- genase genes except KZADH4, conferred to cells resistance to allyl alcohol because of the absence of the KlAdh4p activity. When the mutation was transferred by crosses to an isogenic strain containing all the alcohol dehydrogenase genes, we found that the KZADH3 gene was highly expressed even in the presence of ethanol. In addition, we observed that the absence of KlAdh4p resulted from a post- transcriptional control in that KZADH4 was transcriptionally induced by ethanol. We also found that KZPDC], another ethanol-repressible gene, was not transcribed in the mutant in the presence of this carbon source, indicating that the escape of KZADH3 from ethanol repression was a peculiar feature of this gene. Genetic analysis showed a Mendelian segregation of the mutation that was mapped in a region of chromosome III close to the adel locus. Interestingly, the aar900 mutants had a pleiotropic phenotype and showed increased resistance to monovalent cations and benomyl, suggesting that the mutation could also affect genes other than the alcohol dehydrogenase ones. Strains carrying the aar900 mutation could represent useful tools to unravel the peculiar regulation of KZADH3 and KZADH4 by ethanol.

Actually, during the growth on glucose, the etha- nol produced or added to the medium activates KZADH4 and represses KZADH3. At present, the physiological function of the two mitochondrial isozymes, as well as the mechanisms under- lying this peculiar regulation, is far from clear.

In this paper, we report the isolation of an interesting mutant, obtained after a screening for allyl alcohol resis- tance, that shows an altered expression of both KZADH3 and KZADH4. This strain, carrying an as yet unidentiﬁed muta- tion named aar900, could represent the ﬁrst tool for the identiﬁcation of factors involved in the regulatory pathways of these genes.
