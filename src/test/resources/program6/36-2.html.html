Determination of Molecular Mass

The molecular mass of ADH “A” Was determined by comparison of its mobility with those of standard marker proteins (Sigma, St. Louis, MO). Semilogarithmic plots of the logarithm of the Mr of the protein standards versus the Rf values (SDS-page) and partition coefficient Kav (Super- dex 200), respectively, were used for its determination using linear regression.

Effect of pH on Activity

To investigate the pH optimum, the phosphate buffer of the purified sec-ADH “A” (1 ml each) Was switched by using buffer exchanging tubes (BioRad) (25 mM Tris-maleate- NaOH at pH 6.0, 6.5, 7.0, 8.0, and 9.0; 50 mM phosphate buffer at pH 10.0 and 11.0). To each sample, rac-l- phenylethanol (3.3 umol) or acetophenone (3.3 umol) as substrate were added. The reaction was started by adding 10 ul of a stock solution of NAD+ or NADH, respectively, in Tris-HCl buffer (10 mM, pH 7.5) to give a final concen- tration of 1 mM NAD+ or NADH. After 8 min the reaction was stopped and analyzed as described above.

Effect of Temperature on Activity

An analogous assay as described for the pH was performed at different temperatures (10, 20, 30, 40, 50, 55, 65, and 70°C) using active fraction (0.49 ml, 0.064 Units) of puri- fied sec-ADH “A.” For the temperature stability experi- ments, samples were incubated at 30°C and 50°C for 10 min, 8 h, 24, and 36 h in an Eppendorf thermomixer before the reaction was started by addition of substrates, cosubstrates, and cofactors as described above.

Long-Term Enzyme Stability

Samples of purified sec-ADH “A” as Well as partially purified fractions after Phenyl Sepharose (HIC) and Blue Sepharose (affinity) chromatography were shock-frozen in liquid nitrogen, several probes of which were lyophilized. All samples were stored at —20°C and analyzed after 1, 3, 6, and 10 months as described above.

Stereospecificity

The stereoselectivity was determined by analyzing the transformed or obtained alcohol by chiral GC as described previously (Stampfer et al., 2003a).

Metal Ions

The determination of metal ions by ICP-MS was performed similarly as previously described (Hemmens et al., 2000) but using a Superdex 200 column and phosphate buffer (10 mM, 50 mM NaCl, pH 7.0) at a ﬂow rate of 0.3 ml-min_1.

Cofactor Recycling

The experiment to identify the formation of the (co)-product was performed as described above, using 4-methyl-2-penta- none (MIBK) and 4-methyl-2-pentanol, respectively, for co- factor recycling. The consumption (or formation) of MIBK and 4-methyl-2-pentanol, respectively, were monitored by GC on a HP 1301 GC column (60°C—4°C-min_1—80°C).
