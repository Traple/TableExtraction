3. Results and discussion 3.1. Purification and structural properties

The results of the purification of the enzyme from T roseum are summarized in Table 1. ADH activity toward ethanol in the crude extract was very low. Following affinity chromatography on adenosine 5’-monophosphate of the crude extract,

a single peak of ADH activity was eluted by the buffer A contained 0.1 mM NAD+ and 1.1 mM pyrazole with an increased specific activity of approximately 89 times. After passage through the Mono Q column, the specific activity was further increased to approximately 128 times, with an overall recovery of approximately 38% of the initial activity.

The purified enzyme gave a single band on electrophoresis in the presence of SDS (result not shown). Comparison of relative mobility of the purified enzyme with that of a standard protein indicated that a molecular mass of the T. roseum ADH was approximately 43 kDa by SDS-PAGE. On the other hand, runs of the purified enzyme in size-exclusion chromatography together with molecular mass markers indicated that the activity eluted at a point corresponding to a protein of approximately 88 kDa (results not shown). It is then concluded that the active form of the T. roseum ADH is a homo-dimer of two polypeptides of 43 kDa. The T. roseum ADH seemed to be similar to those of Clostridium beijerinckii and Mycobacterium tuberculosis enzymes, all of which were homo-dimers with a molecular mass of approximately 80 kDa (De Bruyn et al., 1981; Ismaiel et al., 1993). However, it was different from the enzymes of Alcaligenes eutrophus, Bacil- lus stearothermophilus and Thermoanaerobacter ethanolicus which were homo-tetramers with subunits of 39—41.5 kDa (Steinbuchel and Schle- gel, 1984; Burdette and Zeikus, 1994; Guagliardi et al., 1996). On the other hand, the enzymes of Amycolatopsis methanolica and Desulfovibrio gigas were homo-trimer with subunits of 39 kDa and homo-decamer with subunits of 43 kDa, respectively (Hensgens et al., 1993; Van Ophem et al., 1993).
