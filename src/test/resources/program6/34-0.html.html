Cloning and Overexpression in Escherichia coli of the Genes Encoding NAD-Dependent Alcohol Dehydrogenase from Two Salfolobas Species

The gene adh encoding a NAB-dependent alcohol dehydrogenase from the novel strain RC3 of Sulfolobus sp. was cloned and sequenced. Both the adh gene from Sulfolobus sp. strain RC3 and the alcohol dehydrogenase gene from Sulfolobus solfataricas (DSM 1617) were expressed at a high level in Escherichia coli, and the recombinant enzymes were puriﬁed, characterized, and compared. Only a few amino acid replacements were responsible for the dilferent kinetic and physicochemical features investigated.

Alcohol dehydrogenases (ADHs [EC 1.l.l.1]) are widely distributed in all living organisms, because they play an as yet not well deﬁned but essential role as universal factors in cel- lular defense mechanisms ranging from those of prokaryotes to those of humans (4, 13a). The considerable interest in their molecular biology, especially for those isolated from thermophilic archaea, depends both on their potential for bio- technological application (7, 8, 12) and on the possibility of elucidating the mechanism of action, the regulation, and the evolutionary relationships of the various enzymes from differ- ent sources. i

In this respect, the genus Salfolobas, comprising extreme thermoacidophilic, aerobic sulfur-metabolizing archaea, offers a variety of different species showing diiferent degrees of ther- mophilicity and therefore expressing different ADHs useful for comparative studies (3, 18).

In a search for new ADHs with diﬁerent thermal features, the adh gene from the novel and poorly characterized Salfolo- bas sp. strain RC3 (acihRC3), growing at the strict optimal temperature of 75°C, was isolated and overexpressed in Esch- erichia coli. Furthermore, expression to a similar extent in E. coli of the adh gene from the more thermophilic strain, Sal- folobas solfataricas DSM 1617 (adhss [growing optimally in the range of 78 to 87°C]), as well as the fast and high-yield puri- ﬁcation procedure used for both recombinant proteins, al- lowed a comparative kinetic and thermodynamic characteriza- tion of the two enzymes.

Saifolobas sp. strain RC3 was isolated by M. De Rosa from the solfataric ﬁeld at Pisciarelli near Naples, Italy, from which the S. soifataricas DSM 1617 type strain was isolated. Cells were grown under the conditions suggested for S. solfataricas by the Deutsche Sammlung von Mikroorganismen (Brann- schweig, Germany) catalog. Cloning and sequencing of the adh genes from Sulfolobus sp. strain RC3 and S. solfataricas. The isolation of the complete coding sequences of the adh genes from S. solfataricas and Saifolobas sp. strain RC3 was obtained directly from the genomic DNAs by PCR ampliﬁcation according to the method of Saiki (19). The 5’ and 3’ primers containing ATG initiation and TAA termination codons had the following sequences derived from adhss (3): 5’ primer, 5’-CGCGCATGCGAGCA GTTAGATTAGTAG-3’; 3’ primer, 5’-GGCCTGCAGTTAT GGTATGAGTACTTGT-3 ’.

The SphI and PstI restriction sites, underlined in the se- quences, were introduced to allow insertion into the plasmid vector pGEM5Zf(-) and to allow sequencing performed with the universal M13 forward and reverse primers as well as with the speciﬁc oligonucleotides Sp3 (F-GACTTAACGCTGTA GAA-3’), Sp4 (5 ’-CATCAATGCAAGACCCC-3’), and Sp10 (5’-ATTTCCTTCTCCCTGCC-3’), which were designed on the basis of the adhss gene sequence (3). The chromosomal adhRQ, sequence was also cloned with the heterogeneous am- pliﬁcation products used as the probe in the screening of the Salfolobas sp. strain’ RC3 gene bank in order to identify the correct PCR product and verify its authenticity.
