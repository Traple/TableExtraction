The Oxidation of Yeast Alcohol Dehydrogenase-1 by Hydrogen Peroxide in Vitro

Lijie Men and Yinsheng Wang*

Department 0f Chernistry-027, University 0f California, Riverside, California 92521-0403 Received July 28, 2006

Yeast alcohol dehydrogenase (YADH) plays an important role in the conversion of alcohols to aldehydes or ketones. YADH-1 is a zinc-containing protein, and it accounts for the major part of ADH activity in growing baker's yeast. To gain insight into how oxidative modification of the enzyme affects its function, we exposed YADH-1 to hydrogen peroxide in vitro and assessed the oxidized protein by LC-MS/MS analysis of proteolytic cleavage products of the protein and by measurements of enzymatic activity, zinc release, and thiol/thiolate loss. The results illustrated that Cys43 and Cysl53, which reside at the active site of the protein, could be selectively oxidized to cysteine sulfinic acid (Cys-S02H) and cysteine sulfonic acid (Cys-S03H). In addition, H202 induced the formation of three disulfide bonds: Cys43— Cysl53 in the catalytic domain, CysloLCysl“ in the noncatalytic zinc center, and Cyszm-Cysz”. Therefore, our results support the notion that the oxidation of cysteine residues in the zinc-binding domain of proteins can go beyond the formation of disulfide bond(s); the formation of Cys-S02H and Cys-S03H is also possible. Furthermore, most methionines could be oxidized to methionine sulfoxides. Quantitative measurement results revealed that, among all the cysteine residues, Cys43 was the most susceptible to H202 oxidation, and the major oxidation products of this cysteine were Cys-S02H and Cys-S03H. The oxidation of Cys43 might be responsible for the inactivation of the enzyme upon H202 treatment.
