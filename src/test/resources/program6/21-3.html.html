2.9. Reusability assay

The reusability of immobilized SCAD was performed by con- ducting the activity measurement of immobilized SCAD at 25 O C at time intervals of 30 min. After each activity measurement, the bound enzyme was separated by magnetization and washed 3 times with phosphate buffer (0.02 M, pH 7.0). Then the fresh NADH and phenylglyoxylic acid solutions were added to the bound SCAD in sequence and the next activity measurement was carried out and compared with the ﬁrst run (activity deﬁned as 100%).

In the spectrum of Fe3 O4-chitosan magnetic particles (Fig. 1(c)), compared with the spectrum of CTS, the 1589 cm_1 peak of N—H bending vibration shifted to 1562 cm_1, and a new weak peak 1627 cm_1 appeared, it indicated that chitosan react with glutaraldehyde to form Schiﬂ base [27,28], and a new sharp peak 560 cm_1 relating to Fe-O group appeared. The results indicated that CTS was coated to Fe3O4 magnetite nanoparti- cles successfully. Because the surface of iron oxide with negative charges has an afﬁnity toward CTS, protonated CTS could coat the magnetite nanoparticles by the electrostatic interaction and chemical reaction through glutaraldehyde crosslinking.

In the spectrum of Fe3O4-chitosan magnetic nanoparticles bound SCAD (Fig. 1(d)), compared with the spectrum of Fe3 O4-chitosan magnetic nanoparticles, after binding SCAD, the characteristic peak 1627 cm_1 relating to C=N group of Schiﬂ’ base shifts to 1649 cm_1, the peak 3401 cm_1 relating to the stretching vibration of O-H shifts to 3120 cm_1. Thus, it was suggested that the binding was accomplished via the reaction between the amine group on the Fe3 O4-chitosan nanoparti- cles and the amine group of SCAD through glutaraldehyde linkage.

To ascertain the time required to reach adsorption equilib- rium of the SCAD enzyme, immobilization experiments were performed at various initial protein concentrations. Fig. 2 shows an example. The result indicated that about 20% of S. cere- visiae protein had been absorbed onto the carrier after 40 min incubation at room temperature. The time necessary to achieve the adsorption equilibrium was around 120 min. Comparison of results from UV analysis at kmax =280 nm of the ﬁrst super- natant isolated after incubation and the initial enzyme solution, about 24% of the enzyme molecules were bound to the mag- netic Fe3 O4-chitosan nanoparticles.
