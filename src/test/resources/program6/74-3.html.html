Puriﬁcation of the alcohol dehydrogenases

(a) Protamine sulphate precipitation. Protamine sulphate (1 mg) was added for each ml of 48000g supernatant fraction. The mixture was stirred for 10min, and then centrifuged at 12000g for 10min. The E28o/E260 ratio changed from 0.6 to 0.7 in the supernatant, and further addition of protamine sulphate did not cause further precipitation.

(b) (NH4)2S04 precipitation. Solid (NH4)2SO4 was added to the supernatant obtained from the previous step to obtain 55 ‘X, saturation at 4°C (DiJeso, 1968). The mixture was stirred for 45min at 4°C, left for 15min and then centrifuged at 12000g for 10min. The supernatant fraction resulting from this ﬁrst precipitation was adjusted to 75 ‘X, saturation with (NH4)2SO4. It was again stirred for 45min at 5°C, left for 15 min and centrifuged at 12000g for 10min. The precipitate from the 55 %-75 %-satd.-(NH4)2SO4 was resuspended in 2.5ml of 0.1 M-sodium phosphate buffer, pH 7.6, or 0.1M-Tris-HCI, pH 8. The fraction that precipitated between 55 %- and 75 %-satd. (NH4)2SO4 (fraction F 55-75 %) contained all the cytosol alcohol dehydrogenases.

The initial puriﬁcation of the alcohol dehydro- genase activities from air- and O2-grown cells are described in Table 1. The initial speciﬁc activity of the supernatant obtained from O2-grown cells was about threefold that of the air-grown cells. Both fractions lost more than 50 ‘X, of their total activity after centri- fugation at 48000g owing, at least partially, to the removal of the mitochondria. The total puriﬁcation obtained by these procedures was tenfold for the air- grown cells and 20-fold for the O2-grown cells.
