Speciﬁcally, ethanol was oxidized by ADH enzyme activity to produce acetaldehyde by reducing nicoti- namide adenine dinucleotide (NADH to NADH (see Figure 4).35*36 This hypothesis was supported by the ADHs’ increased expression at the protein level, obtained via the iTRAQ ap- proach, in cultures grown on ethanol. As tabulated in Table 1, ADH-10 (8802536) was found to be increased in relative abundance, approximately 10-fold and 4-fold, in cultures grown on 0.08 and 0.79% w/v ethanol, respectively compared to the glucose control. Putative ADH-13 was found to be 1.9-fold more abundant in 0.40% w/v ethanol cultures and remained un- changed in 0.08 and 0.79% w/v ethanol. More than 2-fold increased expression in ADH-2 was also observed in cultures grown at these three ethanol concentrations (i.e., 0.08, 0.40, and 0.79% w/v), whereas no change in expression was observed for both ADH-5 and ADH-6.

The increased expression of ADHs at the protein level were also found to be correlated to quantitative RT-PCR results, with transcriptional data confirming adh-10 as being highly ex- pressed, with approximately 9 and 4-fold up-regulations in 0.40 and 0.79% w/v ethanol cultures, respectively (see Figure 5). Similarly, adh-Z was found to be 2-fold increased in relative abundance in 0.79% w/v ethanol. Meanwhile, adh-13 was found to be 1.9-fold increased in expression in 0.40% ethanol cultures and no significant change in 0.79% w/v ethanol.

Conversely, no change in adh-Z transcriptional levels was observed for growth on 0.40% ethanol cultures, despite a 2-fold increased expression obtained at the protein level. Further- more, the ethanol cultures’ expression ratios at the transcript level for both adh-5 and adh-6 were found to be at least 1.5- fold decreased in expression, whereas translational levels showed no significant changes. Nonetheless, the increased expression obtained in both transcript and protein levels for ADH-2 (8800764) and ADH-10 (8802536) suggested that these genes might be essential in ethanol metabolism.

The transcript data also shows a >2-fold increase in expres- sion of adh-l (8800472) in 0.40 and 0.79% w/v ethanol cultures, grown in the absence of glucose (Table D, Supporting Informa- tion), whereas in the 0.40% w/v ethanol cultures, adh-9 (8802501) and the maleylacetate reductase, clcE (8802490), genes were also shown to be highly expressed (>2-fold). Yet, both genes were found to be 1.7-fold down in relative abun- dance in the 0.79% w/v ethanol cultures. Furthermore, the presence of ethanol (at 0.40 and 0.79% w/v) in cultures also diminished the expression level of adh-3 (8801220), adh-4 (SS01300), and adh-7 (8802441) (Table D, Supporting Infor- mation), yet these ADHs were not identified at the protein level.
