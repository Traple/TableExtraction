Cloning and Nucleotide Sequence Analysis. DNA from S. solfataricus P2 was obtained using the UltraClean Microbial DNA Isolation Kit (Mo Bio Laboratories, USA), according to the manufacturer’s protocol. The putative adh-2 gene was isolated by PCR (PCR System 9700, Applied Biosystems GeneAmp) using the forward primer: 5’-CACCATGAAAGCTGTAGTAGTTCCG-3’ and reverse primer: 5’-GGAGTCTAAATCTGAATGTGG-3 for protein overexpression in E. coli, having a His tag at the N terminus. An additional 4 nucleotides (in italics above), were added at the 5’ end of the forward sequence as they are complementary to the overhang sequence of the pET200 vector (Invitrogen, Paisley, UK) used for directional cloning.” The targeted DNA fragment (approximately 1.1 kb in size), was amplified using Taq DNA polymerase (Sigma) at an annealing temperature of 53 °C. The resulting PCR product was visualized on a 1% agarose gel to ensure correct product amplification. The PCR product was then cleaned using the SureClean reagent (Bioline, London, UK) according to the manufacturer’s proto- col, prior to transformation into E. coli.

The cleaned PCR product was cloned into pET200 and then transformed into E. coli TOP10 cells (Invitrogen). The trans- formation product was spread onto Luria—Bertani (LB) agar plates containing 50 ng/mL of kanamycin and incubated overnight at 37 °C. PCR screening, using a T7 primer pair, was carried out on randomly selected colonies, to ensure positive transformation. Colonies with the plasmid insert were then grown overnight in 10 mL of LB broth containing 50 ng/mL of kanamycin at 37 °C with shaking at 300 rpm.“ Cultures were then harvested by centrifugation (5000>< g, 4 °C for 10 min) for plasmid DNA extraction using an UltraClean Plasmid Prep Kit (Mo Bio Laboratories). Plasmid DNA sequences were confirmed using a ABI 3730 capillary sequencer (Applied Biosystems) located at the Genetic Core Facility, The University of Sheffield, to verify nucleotide sequence and insert orienta- tion.

Overexpression and Purification of Recombinant ADH-2. A plasmid with an insert was then transformed into BL21 Star (DE3) E. coli (Invitrogen) for protein expression. E. coli trans- formants harboring the gene of interest were cultured in 1 L of LB broth with 50 ng/mL of kanamycin at 37 °C on a orbital shaker at 300 rpm.
