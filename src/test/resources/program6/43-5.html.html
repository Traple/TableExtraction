Spectral Simulation Results

Differences in the EPR spectra of spin label attached to each component of LADH and to free and immobilized en- zyme can be partially quantified by computer simulations of the experimental data. Spectral simulations are poten- tially much more informative than alternative analyses, such as measuring maximum peak-to-peak distances,” or calculating an average rotational correlation time from line- shape parametersfs” because the analysis is more rigor- ous and is based on the entire spectrum rather than isolated features.

In this work, it was assumed that the motion of SL-1 in the enzyme’s active site can be described by three rota- tional correlation times, rpry, and 2",, which are charac- teristic times of Brownian rotational diffusion about the three axes of a molecular coordinate system defined with respect to the nitroxidelsjo By convention, the x axis is taken as being along the N-O bond, the z axis along the 2p 7r orbital of the nitrogen, and the y axis perpendicular to the other two.” The calculated spectra were generated with computer programs based on algorithms developed by Freed and co-workersfo” assuming axially symmetric ro- tation, i.e., T, = 1-,.
