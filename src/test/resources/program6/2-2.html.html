Determination of Class IV ADH Isoenzyme

The assay mixture for class IV of ADH activity contained a supernatant (50 uL) m-nitrobenzaldehyde as a substrate (132 uL of 80 uM), NADH (172 uL of 86 uM) in 0.1 M sodium phosphate buffer, pH 7.5.12 The oxidation of NADH was monitored at 340 nm and 25°C on a Shimadzu UV/VIS 1202 spectrophotometer. The ﬁnal results were expressed in milliunits per milligram of protein (nanomoles of NAD formed per minute per milligram of protein).

Protein Assays

Protein concentration was measured by the method of Lowry et all?’ using bovine serum albumin as the standard (Sigma Diagnostics, St Louis, Mo). The assays were performed on a Shimadzu UV/VIS 1202 spectrophotometer.

Statistical Analysis

A preliminary statistical analysis (X2 test) revealed that the distribution of ADH and ALDH activities did not follow a normal distribution. Consequently, Wilcoxon test was used for statistical analysis. Data were presented as median, range, and mean values. Statistically signiﬁcant differences were deﬁned as comparisons resulting in P < 0.05.

RESULTS The activities of total ADH, ALDH, and ADH iso- enzymes in pancreatic cancer are demonstrated in Table 1. We have shown that ADH and ALDH activities are present in

the cancer cells, although the activity of ALDH was much lower than ADH activity in all tested groups. The comparison of the ADH isoenzyme activities showed that class III exhibited the highest activity. The median activity of this class was 1.23 nmol/(min mg“ of protein) in cancer tissue and 0.87 nmol/(min mg“ of protein) in healthy tissue in the total patients group. The activity of class I ADH was about 8 to 10 times lower, and the activity of class IV was about 40 to 50 times lower than class III ADH. The activity of class II ADH isoenzyme was barely detectable. We found that the activity of class III of ADH was signiﬁcantly higher (P < 0.001) in pancreatic cancer than in healthy tissues in total patient group and in both drinker and nondrinker speci- mens. The other tested classes of isoenzymes had higher activities in cancer cells than in healthy mucosa, but dif- ferences were not statistically signiﬁcant in all patients groups (P > 0.05).

The total activity of ADH was higher, but the total activity of ALDH was lower in cancer tissues than in healthy tissues, but these differences were not statistically signiﬁcant.

The activity of ADH and ALDH in cancer tissues of drinkers did not differ from that of nondrinkers specimens (P < 0.05; Table 2). No signiﬁcant differences in the ADH and ALDH activity between drinkers and nondrinkers specimens from healthy tissues were also found. The comparison of the activity of ADH isoenzymes between drinkers and nondrinkers showed nonsigniﬁcant differences.
