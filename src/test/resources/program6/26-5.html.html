T roseum ADH was shown to be active over a Wide range of temperatures (from 30 to 85 °C) (Fig. 3a). The optimum temperature of the enzyme Was 7O °C and up to 60% of activity was observed even at 8O °C. This value was higher than those reported for mesophilic microorganism ADHs (25-40 °C) (Niehaus et al., 1978; Neale et al., 1986; Hensgens et al., 1993; Tsigos et al., 1998). The enzyme Was therefore quite thermophilic. Similar results have also been found in ADHs from Bacillus stearothermophilus (65 °C) (Guag- liardi et al., 1996), Suifolobus solfataricus (95 °C) (Rella et al., 1987), Thermoanaerobacter ethan-

olicus (70 °C) (Pham and Phillips, 1990) and Thermococcus hydrothermalis (80 OC) (Antoine et al., 1999). The thermostability of the T roseum ADH was investigated by incubation of the enzyme at various temperatures for 1 h. The midpoint of the temperature-stability curve was approx. 77 °C for the enzyme (Fig. 3b). The enzyme was fairly stable at temperature up to 7O °C. Above 75 °C, its activities declined rapidly as the temperature increased, but the enzyme was not completely inactivated even at 85 °C.
