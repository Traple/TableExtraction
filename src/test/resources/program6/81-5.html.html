Therefore we conclude that generally the ab- soprtion maxima and coefﬁcients both depend on the kind and the amount of metal present in the protein as well as on the mode of binding and the state of the protein. It is not yet established how the variations in absorbance correlate with the metal content, the active-site content or other parameters. Since the knowledge of protein concentration is required for the calculation of the degree of metal substitution, problems may arise especially when working with commercial enzyme of varying degrees of purity (e.g. in this work we used commercial enzyme with active- site contents often as low as 85 ‘X, and metal contents less than 4 mol/mol protein). Spectra of metallo- alcohol dehydrogenases, especially in the visible region, will therefore suffer from considerable errors if the molar absorption coefﬁcients are calculated on the basis of protein content. In the past however, most spectra of metallo-alcohol dehydrogenases had to be based on protein concentration, since the assign- ments and the properties of the metal binding sites were not deﬁnitely settled. For the sake of compar- ability, our spectra in Fig.1 are therefore based on protein concentration, too. However, in Table 2 the molar absorption coefﬁcients of the d-d transitions of the ‘blue hybrid’ and the ‘green hybrid’ enzymes are calculated on the basis of total metal content, which facilitates spectrochemical evaluation.

Active-Site-Speciﬁc Substituted Cobalt(II) Alcohol Dehydrogenase

The Fully Substituted C0(c)2C0(n)2-enzyme. Our preparation which most closely resembles the fully substituted enzyme of Sytkowski and Vallee [1 —3] contains too little metal (see Table 1). However, the absorption spectrum shows all bands given in the literature and, in addition, a weak band at 520 nm (s= 250 M” cm”) not reported elsewhere. This band also occurs in the ‘blue hybrid’ enzyme substi- tuted speciﬁcally at the catalytic sites. The 740-nm band (s = 750 M” cm”) is identical in position and strength in both the ‘green hybrid’ and the fully sub- stituted enzyme. However, the maximum at 656 nm (s = 1000 M” cm”) of the ‘green hybrid’ is shifted to 650 nm (s == 2000 M” cm”) in fully substituted enzyme. The spectral data are summarized in Table 2. An extensive comparison of the ‘green hybrid’ and the fully substituted enzyme obtained by dialysis and the column technique has been performed previously [40]. The data show that the spectrum of the fully substi- tuted species is a superposition of the spectra of both binding sites.

The ‘Blue Hybrid’ C0(c);Zn(n)2-enzyme. When the spectrum of the ‘green hybrid’ was subtracted from that of the fully substituted enzyme, a visible difference spectrum was obtained with maxima at 650 nm (s = 1200 M” cm") and 520 nm (s = 250 M” cm”). The same difference spectrum resulted from the spectrum of the fully substituted enzyme minus that of the fully substituted enzyme after treatment with 1,10-phenanthroline [3]. These difference spectra are given elsewhere [40]. The spectrum of our ‘blue hybrid’ (Fig. 1, broken line) is identical to these differ- ence spectra calculated previously. Since 1,10-phenan- throline removes the catalytic metal ions in fully sub- stituted cobalt liver alcohol dehydrogenase [3], the difference spectra provide additional evidence that our ‘blue hybrid’ is substituted at the catalytic sites. As expected, 1,10-phenanthroline eliminates totally the visible absorption spectrum of the ‘blue hybrid’.
