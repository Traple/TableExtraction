Enzyme Membrane Reactors

Production of L-tert-Leucine

For production of L-tert-leucine 4 a continuously oper- ated enzyme membrane reactor (EMR) was used. It consists of a loop containing a circulation pump (501U, Watson Marlow, Rotterdam, The Netherlands), a heat exchanger, and an ultraﬁltration unit (Filtron Mini- sette with a membrane stack, cutoff 10,000 Da; Filtron, Karlstein, Germany). Substrates were dosed by using a Pharmacia P-500 pump (Pharmacia LKB GmbH, Frei- burg, Germany) through a sterile ﬁlter (Sartorius ﬁlter holder with microﬁltration membrane 0.1 um, cellulose nitrate; Type 11358-47; Sartorius AG, Gottingen, Ger- many) into the reactor.

The substrate (feed) solution was cooled in an ice bath and replaced every morning. Prior to use, the reac- tor was sterilized by pumping 1 L of 0.05% peracetic acid, followed by distilled water for ﬂushing. Total back- mixing in the reactor was guaranteed by a high ﬂow of the recirculation pump. The total reactor volume was 70 mL. The reactor was operated for 71 days (see Fig. 3). For a two-stage cascade, two identical reactors were operated in series, each with a volume of 46 mL. The pressure drop over each reactor was about 2 bar. Production of (S)-1-Phenyl-2-Propanoi

The setup of the reactor is shown in Figure 2. For EMR the same basic setup was used as for the production of L-tert-leucine. Instead of the ﬂat membrane stack (Filtron Minisette) a hollow ﬁber module was used (Type H1p10-43, Amicon, Witten, Germany). Steriliza- tion of the reactor was accomplished using VelcorinTM (Bayer AG, Leverkusen, Germany). The effuent of the EMR was passed through an extraction module (Liqui- Cel 5PCM-106, Hoechst Celanese Corp., Wiesbaden, Germany). To prevent the hexane from entering the water phase through the hydrophobic microporous membrane a slight overpressure was applied. This type of extraction module has been characterized recently by Daiminger et al. (1995).
