In conclusion, the aerobic bacteria representing normal human colonic ﬂora possess NAD-linked ADHs with low apparent K,,,’s for ethanol oxidation. Therefore, they may participate in the bacteriocolonic pathway for ethanol oxi- dation, resulting in the production of intracolonic acetal- dehyde even after moderate drinking with nearly maximal velocity. Different kinetic characteristics of the ADHs of these colonic bacteria may enable them to produce acetal- dehyde from the endogenous ethanol formed in the colon by bacterial fermentation.
