Approximately 21 and 31% of the 284 proteins (with 22 MS/ M8 spectra) identified and quantified via iTRAQ were up or down in expression, respectively, in ethanol cultures compared to the glucose control. The well characterized ADH-10 (8802536) was found to be 10-fold increased in expression in 0.08% w/v ethanol, and 4-fold increased in expression in 0.79% w/v ethanol compared to the glucose control. Furthermore, there was at least a 2-fold increased expression of ADH-2 (8800764) when S. solfataricus was grown solely on ethanol, at various concentrations. The transcription level obtained from quantita- tive RT-PCR was correlated with translational (protein) expres- sions, where both adh-IO (8802536) and adh-Z (8800764) genes

were found to be highly expressed in ethanol cultures com- pared to the control. The increased expression by >2-fold at protein level of acetyl-CoA synthetases (8802863 and 8803203) further revealed that ethanol was catabolised into the central metabolism of S. solfataricus, via acetyl-CoA and pyruvate intermediates, whereas the 2-fold decrease in protein expres- sion of isocitrate dehydrogenase suggested the ethanol grown cultures had shifted toward the glyoxylate cycle.

Both the transcriptional and translational results suggest that the putative adh-Z may be an alcohol dehydrogenase enzyme, being integral in ethanol catabolism. Preliminary analysis of the purified recombinant ADH-2 enzyme has shown ethanol oxidation activity, with KM estimated at approximately 1.33 :I: 0.25 mM at 65 °C. The activity of this recombinant enzyme was also found to be higher as the temperature increased to 95 °C. Nevertheless, greater detailed characterization of this enzyme will be the main focus of future studies.
