Thermal stability

Crude extracts. The 48 000g supernatants obtained from either air-grown or Oz-grown cells were heated for the time speciﬁed in a constant-temperature bath at 50°C. They were then cooled for 5min in ice and centrifuged at l2000g in a refrigerated Sorvall centri- fuge The supernatant fractions were used for electro- phoresis and for the spectrophotometric determina- tions of enzyme activity as described below. In the air- grown cells the uppermost band of activity was de- natured very rapidly at 50°C, and after 8min activity could no longer be detected on the gels. However, in the supernatant obtained from the OZ-treated cells, there was still considerable fraction ADH I activity after 10min at 50°C (Plate 1). After spectrophoto- metric assay of the enzyme activity (as described below) it appeared that although the alcohol dehydro- genase activity of the air-grown cells was little affected by heat treatment at 50°C for 2min, the supernatant from the Oz-grown cells lost one-third of its activity under the same conditions. It also appeared from the electrophoretic behaviour of the crude extract that fraction ADI-I II was little aﬁ°ected by heat treatment, although it was not possible to evaluate its behaviour by a spectrophotometric assay under these conditions.
