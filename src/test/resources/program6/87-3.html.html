RESULTS Purification of the major ethanol-active liver enzyme

The major form of ethanol-active alcohol dehydrogenase was purified from an alligator liver homogenate by affinity chromatography on CapGapp—Sepharose as described in Materials and methods. One affinity chromatographic step resulted in a 183-fold purification and a yield of 72% from the crude extract. The specific activity obtained was 0.42 U/ mg protein (Table 1). the breakthrough and washing fractions from the CapGapp column contained a further 20% of the total ethanol dehydrogenase activity. Subsequent chromatog- raphy on AMP*Sepharose resulted in two eluates with dif- ferent responses toward increased ethanol concentrations (Fig. 1A). The early eluate revealed a significantly increased activity with an increasing ethanol concentration from 0.04 M to 1.8 M; this was not observed for the later eluate. The ethanol-eluted material in the CapGapp—Sepharose chromatography was analyzed with starch gel electrophoresis (Fig. 1B) and SDS/PAGE (Fig. 1C) prior to kinetic and structural characterization.

Enzymatic properties

Starch gel electrophoresis and activity staining of the alli- gator enzyme with ethanol yields a major cathodic band with characteristics of class I alcohol dehydrogenase and a ladd.er of more anodic, regularly spaced and successively weaker subsidiary bands (Fig. 1B). The kinetic constants of the ma- jor band with respect to several substrates are given in Table 2 and show that it is a class l alcohol dehydrogenase with a Km value for ethanol of 1.2 mM.

Line and class relationships

The homology of the alligator enzyme to other alcohol dehydrogenases is unequivocal but differs in regard to dis- tinct vertebrate lines, on the one hand, and enzyme classes, on the other.
