Sequence Comparisons

Retrieved from previous studies34 and from GeneBank and EMBL databases were 128 Drosophila ADH se- quences available as full or partial DNA or protein entries. The ADHs analyzed corresponded to different Drosophila species and to the ADH isoforms and alleloforms already described for some species. The ClustalW package35 was used to construct the amino acid sequence alignment. The sequences of the BD and OLE secondary structure elements, which include the positions relevant to this study (90, 101, 107, 110, 113, and 114), were analyzed in 119 of the total 128 sequences, because those comprised the compared segment. For residue 62, only 113 sequences were avail- able. Residue position in all cases is referred to the D. lebanonensis sequence (reference describing the D. lebanon- ensis ADH structure). A ﬁle containing the ADH multiple- sequence alignment is available as supplementary mate- rial upon request to the authors.

RESULTS

The protonation/deprotonation equilibria were calcu- lated for all titratable groups of the Drosophila lebanonen- sis ADH binary complex with NAD+. However, only the results for the active site groups are presented and dis- cussed below.

As was already discussed in the Methods section, one of the major limitations of the models for the calculation of

293

electrostatic interactions in proteins is that they are based on a ﬁxed three-dimensional structure. The introduction of the elementary conformational ﬂexibility involving only polar hydrogen atoms is a step toward improving the predictive power of the electrostatic calculations.12’21

As a dimer, the DADH molecule provides a good model to estimate the inﬁuence of small conformational differences on the ionization properties of the protein. There are small differences in the side-chain conformations of the two subunits. These differences modify some factors determin- ing the ionization properties of the titratable groups, such as the solvent accessibility of the titratable groups or the charge-charge interactions. It is important to note that the ionization of the titratable sites, as well as the population of the polar hydrogen atoms with multiple positions, show the same pattern in both subunits. This suggests that the differences between the values calculated for the different subunits can be considered as a measure of the minimum interval of reliability of the results but do not have any impact on the ﬁnal conclusions.

Ionization Properties of the Active Site Residues

The groups that are of particular interest are Ser138, Tyr151, and Lys155.

Ser138: The calculations showed that Ser138 does not undergo deprotonation within the interval 2 < pH < 12. The most populated rotamer of the hydroxyl group of this residue is the reference state. This corresponds to a hydrogen bond between W144 (the active site bound water) and the Ser138 hydroxyl group, in which the latter is the proton donor (Fig. 1). The population of this rotamer is about 0.9 in subunit A and changes from 0.8 (pH < 8) to 0.7 (pH > 10) in subunit B.

Tyr151: The titration curve and the populations of the protonated states of Tyr151 are shown in Figure 2. The population of the reference state, corresponding to the hydrogen bond with W144 where the Tyr15 1 hydroxyl acts as a proton donor, is negligible in the whole pH interval. The preferred protonated state of Tyr151 is to act as a proton donor to the O2’ hydroxyl group of the NAD+ ribose moiety. The titration of Tyr151 exhibits two peculiarities. First, the ionization behavior of this residue in the two subunits differs at pH > 6, and at pH > 10 the difference in the population of the deprotonated state is about 0.2. The second peculiarity is that Tyr151 is partially deprotonated in the whole pH range.

Lys155: The ionization behavior of Lys155 displays similar properties to that of Tyr151. As seen in Figure 3, the degree of deprotonation differs in the two subunits for pH values > 6, reaching again a value of about 0.2 at pH 10. This value is in the range of the accuracy limit of the calculations; thus, differences in the calculated popula- tions < 0.2 are considered irrelevant. Finally, the ioniza- tion of these residues in both subunits is irregular.
