The (NH4)2SO4 precipitate is centrifuged at 12,000 g for 40 min, and the precipitate is dissolved in a minimum volume of 0.05 p sodium phosphate buffer, pH 8.0. Pro- longed dialysis against the glycine buffer causes excessive destruction of the enzyme. The dialyzed enzyme is applied to a 3 X 30 cm diethylam- inoethylcellulose (Calbiochem) column (ﬂow rate 300 ml/hr) equili- brated with 0.01 p glycine-NaOH buffer, pH 9.5. The enzyme is not adsorbed by the column and is recovered as a colorless solution? The enzyme is precipitated by the addition of 56 g of solid (NHi) 2504 per 100 ml of solution, and adjusted to pH 8.0 with concentrated ammonium hydroxide. The precipitated enzyme is stable at 4° for several months.
