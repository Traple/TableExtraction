3.4. Testing of chaperone-like activity of native yADH

Bhattacharyya et al. [21] have demonstrated that the isolated peptide —YSGVCHTDLHAWHGDWPLPVK- [40-60] of the yADH mole- cule reveals an ability to suppress aggregation of a number 0f model protein substrates. It was of special interest t0 check whether the whole yADH molecule possesses a chaperone-like activity. It is evident that we should select a test system where yADH itself does not undergo aggregation and remains in the native form. As such a test system we used aggregation of UV-irradiated BL-crystallin. Aggrega- tion of UV-irradiated BL-crystallin (0.2 mg/ml) proceeded with a rather high rate already at 37 °C (Fig. 10, curve 1). yADH (1 mg/ml) did not aggregate at this temperature (Fig. 10, curve 2). Curve 3 in Fig. 1O corresponds to aggregation of UV-irradiated BL-crystallin (0.2 mg/ml) in the presence of yADH (1 mg/ml). yADH preparation used in these experiments was preheated for 13 min at 52 °C. As it is evident from Fig. 10, yADH greatly suppresses aggregation of UV-irradiated BL- crystallin. Thus, native yADH possesses a chaperone-like activity.

4. Discussion

judging from the DSC data, denaturation of yADH proceeds as an irreversible monomolecular reaction. It is worthy of note that the yADH preparation preheated for 13 min at 52 °C was used in the denaturation and aggregation experiments. Quantitative analysis of temperature proﬁles of CE‘ allowed us to estimate the parameters of the Arrhenius equation (15361 and ﬁe“) and calculate the dependence of the denaturation rate constant (kden) on temperature. The values of activation energy for protein denaturation following a one-step irreversible model have been summarized in the reviews [39,43,44]. The DSC data were used in these calculations. The minimum values of the energy of activation are about 160-230 kj/mol (for example, 156 kj/mol for horseradish peroxidase [45], 165 kj/mol for DNA ligase from E. coli [46], 172 kj/mol for lactate dehydrogenase from pig skeletal muscle [47], 203 kj/mol for elongation factor 2 from Metha- nococcoides burtonii [48], and 226 kj/mol for pineapple stem bromelain [49]). The value of ESE“ for thermal denaturation of yADH was found to be 234:2 kj/mol. Consequently, yADH belongs to a group of proteins characterized by the minimum values of activation energy for the denaturation process.

When studying the kinetics of thermal aggregation of proteins using DLS, we have shown that aggregation proceeds under the kinetic regime wherein the sticking probability for the colliding particles is equal to unity (the so-called regime of diffusion-limited cluster-cluster aggregation; DLCA) [26,36,50]. Such a mechanism of aggregation is conﬁrmed by the character of the dependence of the hydrodynamic radius of protein aggregates on time. We have em- phasized that distribution of the protein aggregates by size remains unimodal over the entire period of the registration of the aggrega- tion process (until precipitation of the large-sized aggregates). The initial part of the dependences of Rh on time is linear. At rather high values of time the dependences of R1, on time follow the power law: Rh~t1/df, where df is the fractal dimension of aggregates. For the DLCA regime of aggregation parameter df has a universal magnitude: df= 1.8 [30,51-53].

One can expect that thermal aggregation of protein chaperones will proceed under the regime of reaction-limited cluster-cluster aggregation, because chaperone should suppress its own aggregation. Actually, the dependence of the hydrodynamic radius on time for the protein aggregates formed in the course of oi-crystallin aggregation at 55 °C in the presence of 16 mM CaCl2 follows the exponential law [54].

The main result of the present work is that the initial parts of the dependences of the hydrodynamic radius of the protein aggregates on time (or temperature) for thermal aggregation of yADH follow the exponential law. In conclusion, it is to be said that analysis of the dependence of the hydrodynamic radius of the protein aggregates on time permits to reveal proteins containing intramolecular chaperones. When analyz- ing the literature data on protein aggregation registered by DLS, we discovered that the dependence of the hydrodynamic radius on time for aggregation of B-lactoglobulin (1 mg/ml; pH 5.2 and 5.4; ionic strength 0.0045 M) is characterized by the transition of the distribution of particles by size from the unimodal function to the bimodal function at rather high values of time [56]. Besides, as our calculations show, the initial part of the dependence of the hydro- dynamic radius on time follows the exponential law. Thus, one may assume that the molecule of B-lactoglobulin contains an intramole- cular chaperone. Additional experiments need to be carried out to verify this assumption.
