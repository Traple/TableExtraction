The speciï¬c activity of AdhE in the strain JW200- adhE was approximately three-fold higher than the activity in the parent strain JW200 (Table 1). The elevated enzyme activity was due to increased adhE gene copy number in the strain JW200-adhE. There was no difference in growth between strain JW200-adhE and JW200. When the concentration of the substrate is less than 1%, the major metabolic products are ethanol and acetate [1]. A larger amount of ethanol was produced by strain JW200-adhE than by JW200, while there was no difference in acetate production (Table 1). These results suggest that ethanol production was affected by overexpression of AdhE. Six Adhs were published in T. ethanolicus ATCC 33223 genome, and two of them were reported previously to be responsible for the ethanol fermentation. Knowledge about AdhE of T. ethanolicus will be beneï¬cial for further understanding of its ethanol fermentation pathway.

In conclusion, we identiï¬ed a putative Adh of T. ethanolicus to be a bifunctional AdhE. Analysis of adhE homologous expression in T ethanolicus indicated that AdhE affected ethanol production. Our work provides a partial elucidation of the ethanol metabolism pathway in T ethanolicus and facilitates construction of the ethanol production strain by genetical engineering.
