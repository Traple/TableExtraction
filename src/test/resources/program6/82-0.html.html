As described earlier [2], the primary product of the enzymatic reaction is rather unstable and has been called ‘compound I’ (e300 = 15000 M“ cm”). It spontaneously converts to NADX, the ﬁnal stable product of the whole process (e300 = 23 000 M" 1 cm“), which has been investigated for its structure [3]. Further support in favour of a mechanism of the per- oxidatic reaction involving the same enzyme active site where alcohols are oxidized by NAD+ has been found, in addition to that already described [1]. Actu- ally ethanol behaves as a competitive inhibitor to- wards hydrogen peroxide during the peroxidatic reaction.

MATERIALS AND METHODS

Horse liver alcohol dehydrogenase, catalase from beef liver and B-NADJ’ (grade I) were obtained from Boehringer (Mannheim) and further puriﬁed as de- scribed in the preceding paper [2]. Hydrogen peroxide (Peridrol) and 1,4-dithiothreitol (Cleland’s reagent) were supplied by Merck (Darmstadt). The titer of stock solutions of hydrogen peroxide was determined according to Eisenberg [4]. The sulfhydryl content of enzyme samples was measured according to Ellman [5]. All other reagents were analytical grade substances from Carlo Erba (Milan). Quartz-bidistilled Water was used throughout.
