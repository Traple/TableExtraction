DISCUSSION

Most of the alcohol dehydrogenases are soluble enzymes; in addition, mitochondrial ADHs have been reported in some fun- gi, such as Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Kluyveromyces lactis (Young and Pilgrim, 1985; Saliola and Falcone, 1995). In the present study, most NAD+-dependent ADH activity was pellet- ed at 160,000 g. This result and the negative staining analysis of this fraction demonstrate that ameba ADH2 is organized as multimers, in a structure similar to that of the ADHE from Escherichia coli (Kessler et a1., 1991) and puriÔ¨Åed E. histolytica ADH2, which however, was puriÔ¨Åed from a 150,000-g soluble fraction (Bruchhaus and Tannich, 1994). The present study clearly demonstrates that the main ADH activity sedimented in a pattern similar to ADHE from E. coli and does not behave as a membrane enzyme. Moreover, in fresh preparations, ameba ADH2 has a structure similar to that of the E. coli.
