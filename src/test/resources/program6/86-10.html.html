Thus, it is not surprising that the stereoselectivity of 2- butanone reduction is affected very little by the catalytic metal-ion substitution.

It can be concluded that the substrate speciﬁcity and stereoselectivity of LADH for secondary alcohols and ketones are dominated by substrate/protein interactions. A compari- son of the substrate-speciﬁc data shows how substrate speci- ﬁcity and stereoselectivity are determined by a combination of free energies of binding and activation. In the case of the substrates studied here, the differences in free energies of binding are the dominating factors.
