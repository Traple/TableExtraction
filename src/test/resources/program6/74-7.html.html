There can be little doubt that various forms of alco- hol dehydrogenase exist in intact Astasia. We ob- served previously with baker’s yeast preparations that new electrophoretic bands of alcohol dehydro- genase activity appeared after storing or freeze-drying the extracts (Heick et al. , 1969). In contrast the alcohol dehydrogenase of Astasia was found to be very labile, the activity being lost after storing or freeze-drying of cell-free extracts so that the various isolation steps had to be performed very quickly and on fresh ex- tracts. Further, the electrophoretic bands of enzymic activity that were recovered after puriﬁcation were similar to those observed in freshly broken cells.

The puriﬁcation steps with hydroxyapatite and heat treatment do not lead to a complete separation of the alcohol dehydrogenase activity from other proteins, but these steps do lead to the separation of three forms of alcohol dehydrogenase from each other. Thus by judicious manipulations of the culture conditions and of the carbon source, it might be pos- sible eventually to ascribe a more deﬁnite role to the various forms of this enzyme in Astasia.
