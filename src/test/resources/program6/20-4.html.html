Even if Raman results cannot lead to certain conclu- sions about copper co-ordination in Cu-ADH, they are consistent with EPR results and can be a conﬁrmation of the hypothesis that copper is bound by the same ligands as zinc in native ADH. As a consequence, the hypothesis of different catalytic properties due to differ- ences in the metal features seems to be more acceptable.

4. Conclusions

The in vitro substitution procedure, previously car- ried out to prepare a cobalt-modiﬁed ADH, allows us to obtain the substitution of a zinc atom in alcohol dehydrogenase, with copper(II). This substitution could not be obtained by growing Saccaromyces cerevisiae (as was the case of Co-ADH) in a culture medium contain- ing high concentrated copper(II), because of its toxicity for cells. On the contrary, the described technique allows a copper-substituted ADH to be prepared and characterised. The spectroscopic results suggest that copper is bound to the same residues and has the same co-ordination geometry as zinc in the active site of the enzyme. Catalytic properties of Cu-ADH, on the other hand, are different from those of the native enzyme (lower SA, higher resistance towards pH) but they are similar to those showed by the in vitro substituted Co-ADH, suggesting that the nature of the metal plays a fundamental role in the catalytic mechanism.
