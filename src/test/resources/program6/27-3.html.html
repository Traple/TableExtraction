It is noteworthy that the thermostability of both YADH and TBADH increase with increas- ing hydrophobicity (log P) of the organic sol- vents. Thus n-dodecane with the highest logP (6.6) afforded the highest degree of protection (Fig. 1), followed by n-octane (logP=4.5) and toluene (log P = 2.5). Other investigators (e. g. 1.5) have made similar observations. The impressive protection against thermoinactivation in organic solvents observed here may be due to conforma- tional rigidity of the proteins in the dehydrated state and their resistance to deleterious processes causing irreversible thermoinactivation [5]. It has been demonstrated that all the processes causing irreversible thermal inactivation of enzymes re- quire water [4,7,2l]. The occurrence of such unfa- vourable events in aqueous media related to apo- and holo-yeast alcohol dehydrogenase has been recently described [22]. Results presented here are in line with the conclusion that it is possible to provide a mesophilic protein with thermophilic characteristics by changes in its microenvironment [22,23]. Accordingly, the mesophilic protein which showed zero activity when incubated in the aqueous medium at 70 °C, showed thermostabili- ties even higher than the thermophilic protein (in

the aqueous medium) when placed in the hydro- phobic environment (Fig. l).

3.2. Effect of miscible organic solvents

Fig. 2 presents the effect of incubation of YADH in 50% dimethylformamide and dioxane at two different temperatures. As indicated, loss of activity was observed in both cases with diox- ane being more effective. Due to the fact that these solvents have very similar log P (— l.l for dioxane and — 1.0 for DMF, see reference [24]), it is possible that the higher effectiveness of dioxane in causing loss of activity is, at least partly, due to the higher denaturation capacity for this solvent (92.1) as compared to DMF (63.3), using reported values [25].

For the thermophilic enzyme, an activity of about 20% was obtained in the presence of dimethylformamide which remained constant for 2 h (Fig. 3). It was subsequently found that the same percentage of activity was obtained when the solvent was added directly to the assay mix at the same ﬁnal concentration (0.5%), without any pretreatment with the solvent. The reciprocal plots related to the reversible inhibitory effect of DMF showed noncompetetive inhibition patterns (Fig. 4), from which K, values were determined. The effect of dioxane was very minimal and re- maining activities were approximately 95 and 90% corresponding to 0 and 25 °C.
