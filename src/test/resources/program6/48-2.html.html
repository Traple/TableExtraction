Results

Localization of alcohol dehydrogenase

OPEOH biodegradation by P. putida S-5 proceeds mainly via the terminal oxidative mechanism.10'12) This is equivalent to the proposed PEG biodegradation pathway in S. terrae.5) Therefore, OPEOn biodegrada- tion is also initiated by the oxidation of its terminal alcohol group by alcohol dehydrogenase. To identify the subcellular localization of alcohol dehydrogenase, cell extract and culture supernatant were prepared from 300 ml of P. putida S-5 cultures grown for 3 d on a TX liquid medium. The alcohol dehydrogenase activity toward Triton X-100 was determined for both fractions. Enzyme activity was detected in the cell extract, but not in the culture supernatant, even after concentration. Subcellular fractionation analysis was performed with glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase as the cytoplasmic marker, NADH oxidase as the membrane marker, and alkaline phosphatase as the periplasmic marker. Slight cross-contamination of the fractions was detected (Table 1). Considering the activities of the control markers in each fraction, most of the alcohol dehydro- genase was found to be in the cytoplasmic fraction and only a little in the membrane fraction.
