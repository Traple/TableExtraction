Wojciech Jelski, MD, Lech Chrostek, MD, PhD, and Maciej Szmitkowski, MD, PhD

Objectives: The pancreas can metabolize ethanol via oxidative pathway involving the enzymes alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH) and aldehyde dehydrogenase (ALDH) as well as the nonoxidative pathway. In this study, we compared the activity of ADH isoenzymes and ALDH in the pancreatic cancer with the activity in normal tissue. In addition, the differences between enzyme activities of drinkers and nondrinkers were compared.

Methods: For the measurement of the activity of class I and II ADH isoenzymes and ALDH activity, we used the ﬁuorometric methods. The total ADH activity and activity of class III and IV isoenzymes were measured by the photometric method. The samples were taken from 56 pancreatic cancer patients (22 drinkers and 34 nondrinkers) and 56 healthy subjects.

Results: The activity of class III ADH was signiﬁcantly higher in cancer than in healthy tissues. Total activities of ADH and ALDH were not signiﬁcantly different in cancer and normal cells. The differences between enzymes of drinkers and nondrinkers in both cancer and healthy tissue were not signiﬁcant.

Conclusion: Pancreatic cancer tissue exhibits higher activity of class III ADH isoenzyme than healthy tissue, and we consider that oxidative pathway of ethanol metabolism via ADH and ALDH does not play a role in pancreatic carcinogenesis.

Key Words: alcohol dehydrogenase isoenzymes, aldehyde dehydrogenase, pancreatic cancer

MATERIALS AND METHODS

Patients

The protocol was approved by the Human Care Committee of the Medical University in Bialystok, Poland (approval R-I-003/ 199/2006). All patients gave an informed consent for the examination.

Biopsy specimens of pancreatic healthy tissues and cancer tissues (80-100 mg) were taken during routine surgical operation for treatment of pancreatic carcinomas from 56 patients (33 men and 23 women; mean age, 62 years; range, 46-74 years; mean age of men, 63 years; range, 46-74; mean age of women, 62 years; range, 5 3-7 1 years). Using the TNM data obtained from tumor and lymph mode status, the patients were categorized according to American Joint Committee on Cancer Classiﬁcation staging. All patients were diagnosed with pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (G2, pT3). The patients group included 17 men and 9 women with mean alcohol intake of more than 50 g/d. The group also included patients (16 men and 14 women) with moderate alcohol intake (1-3 drinks a day, 5-6 days a week). The patients were not treated with any antibiotics (no antibiotics were used 2 weeks before and during the operations).

Material

All specimens were frozen at —80°C until analysis within 2 weeks. The pancreatic tissue (l:4 wt/vol) was homogenized in potassium phosphate buffer (0.1 M; pH 7.4) for 20 seconds using ultrasoniﬁcator (Sonoplus HD 70; Bandelin), centrifuged at 14000 rpm for 30 minutes, and cooled to 4°C. The supernatant was used to determine the ADH and ALDH activities.
