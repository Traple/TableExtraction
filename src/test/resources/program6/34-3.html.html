Gene expression and puriﬁcation of the recombinant ADHs. The vectors for the isopropyl-B-D-thiogalactopyranoside (IPTG)- inducible expression of SsADH and Sulfolobus sp. strain RC3 ADH (RC3ADH) were constructed by insertion of the correct ADH coding sequences into the plasmid pTrc99A (2) between the Ncol and PstI sites. Optimal conditions for the growth, IPTG induction, and lysis of E. colt Rb791 transformants were determined according to the method of Guagliardi et al. (10). For the highest yield of active enzymes, l-liter cultures were grown to a cell density measured as an optical density at 600 nm of 2 and then exposed to the inducer for 18 h. The crude extracts were incubated at 70°C for 10 min, centrifuged, ﬁve- fold concentrated, and again heat treated.

The enzymes were puriﬁed by gel ﬁltration chromatography. The sample was dialyzed against 50 mM sodium phosphate buifer (pH 7.0)—O.2 M NaCl and loaded onto a Hi-Load Su- perdex 200 column (2.6 by 60 cm; Pharmacia) connected to a fast protein liquid chromatography (FPLC) system (Pharma- cia) and chromatographed in the same buffer at a ﬁow rate of 2 ml/min.

ADH activity was assayed as described by Rella et al. (18) with 50 mM glycine-NaOH (pH 10.0) as the reaction buffer and 10 mM benzyl alcohol or 20 mM ethanol as the substrate.

Gel exclusion chromatography revealed a molecular mass (140 kDa) which can be ascribed only to tetrameric enzymes, with the recombinant SsADH showing a speciﬁc activity of about 10 U/mg. Despite previous reports (3, 18), a more de- tailed characterization of the wild-type SsADH has recently ascertained the presence of both dimeric and tetrameric forms, distinguishable by their diiferent levels of retention according to several kinds of gel exclusion chromatography procedures (15), with the dimeric conformation showing the lower level of catalytic eﬂiciency (speciﬁc activity of 2.5 U/mg versus 10 U/mg for the tetramer).

Chemical, physical, and kinetic characterization of the Sul- folobus ADHs. The comparative kinetic analysis carried out with the RC3ADH and SsADH proteins (recombinant and wild type) was restricted to the most representative substrates such as ethanol and benzyl alcohol. SsADH showed higher afﬁnity towards the aromatic alcohol; in fact, the Vma, was found to be 3.5-fold higher than that calculated for ethanol, and the Km was lower to the same extent. In contrast to this, no distinct or measurable diiference in the activity displayed by RC3ADH on both substrates could be detected, with the Vmaxs being identical and with the Michaelis constants being very similar (Kms of 14.5 i 0.7 uM for benzyl alcohol and 17.2 i 0.7 uM for ethanol). Furthermore, the enzymes (0.1 mg/ml in 50 mM sodium phosphate buffer [pH 7.0] or in 50 mM Tris HCl [pH 8.5]) exhibited identical resistance to heating, with a half- life of 3 h at 85°C (or 1 h at 90°C); these values were also conﬁrmed for the wild-type enzymes.
