Exposure of Enzyme Samples to Elevated Temperatures

For soluble LADH, vials containing 0.5 mL enzyme so- lution (0.12 mg/mL in 0.01M phosphate buffer, pH 7.5) were incubated in a constant-temperature bath at 60°C for time periods to 100 min- Samples were then removed from the bath and 0.1 mL enzyme solution was added to 3.0 mL substrate solution to measure the remaining activity versus ethanol at 25°C as described in ref. l5.

In the case of immobilized LADH, immobilized-enzyme conjugate containing 0.1-0.2 mg immobilized enzyme was placed in a sealed Bio-Rad disposable chromatography column containing 2.0 mL phosphate buffer and incubated in the water bath for various lengths of time. At the indi- cated exposure times, the buffer was removed and tested for protein content. The immobilized enzyme remaining in the column was then assayed at 25°C.

Resolution of EPR Spectra with Potassium Ferricyanide

To resolve each composite EPR spectrum into its individ- ual constituents, each spin-labeled sample was treated with potassium ferricyanide. Potassium ferricyanide is a para- magnetic reagent which, if added in the appropriate amount (referred to as the end point), can selectively broaden- i.e. eliminate -- the signal of the more accessible nitroxides in a heterogeneous population of spin label.1°'” Thus, each spin-labeled sample was exposed to varying concentrations of potassium ferricyanide until the spectrum obtained by sub- tracting the residual spectrum from the original spectrum (i.e., the portion of the spectrum eliminated by exposure to ferricyanide) contained features indicative of two over- lapping components (Fig. 1). At this point, the broadening agent had begun to affect the less-accessible spin-label population and the end point had been surpassed- Once the end point was determined, double integration of the origi- nal and residual spectra revealed the relative amounts of each spectral component present initially.

Isoelectric Focusing

Isoelectric focusing (IEF) was performed using the Bio- Rad lll mini IEF eel with a Bio-Rad 500/200 power supply. The procedure was that outlined by Bio-Rad for acrylamide gels attached to polyacrylamide support film. Gels were pre-

pared either without urea (native) or with 8.5M urea added to the acrylamide solution prior to polymerization (denatur- ing). Gels were stained with Bio-Rad silver staining reagent, as described by Bio-Rad for gels less than 0.5 mm thick with a polyester backing. Bands were quantiﬁed by a GS 300 transmittance- reflectance scanning densitometer from Hoefer Scientific (San Francisco, CA).
