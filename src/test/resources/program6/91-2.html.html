Results

Isolation of KIADH4 expression mutants

A total of 2 >< 107 cells of the haploid Kluyveromyces lactis strain CMK5, which contains KZADH4 as the single Adh gene (Saliola et 011., 1999), were plated on YPDEAA medium containing allyl alcohol (see Materials and methods), a compound that is converted by alcohol dehydrogenases into acroleic aldehyde, a very toxic compound for the cell. The 166 resistant clones grown on these plates could represent different classes of mutations: (a) mutations in the structur- al KZADH4 gene; (b) mutations in the KZADH4 promoter; (c) mutations in an extragenic factor that regulates the expression of KZADH4; and (d) mutations that modify membrane permeability to allyl alcohol. To eliminate mu- tants of types (a) and (b), the isolated clones were crossed with a KZadhO strain lacking all the four alcohol dehydro- genase activities; 22 diploids able to grow in ethanol were then conﬁrmed for the presence of a functional KlAdh4p by nondenaturing polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis. analysis, performed both in the haploid mutants and in the corresponding diploids, allowed us to distinguish between mutations in membrane permeability to allyl alcohol from those affecting putative regulatory factors of KZADH4. In fact, in the case of permeability, the KlAdh4p activity was expected to be present in the mutants grown in ethanol; conversely, in the second case, KlAdh4p should be missing in the haploid mutant and present in the diploid background. On this basis, we selected 13 regulatory mutants missing KlAdh4p. One of these mutants showing a clear phenotype, namely CMK9, was used in further experiments. To study the effect of the mutation, named aar900, on the ADH system, we constructed by repetitive crosses the strain CMK28-2A, isogenic to MW179-ID, carrying all the four KlAdh activities and the aar900 mutation (see Materials and methods).

The occurrence of this mutation in spores was detected on the basis of KlAdh4p-negative electrophoretic patterns, because the original allyl alcohol resistance phenotype could not be used here for the presence of other ADHs.

In Fig. 1 the KlAdh pattern of the wild type (wt) and of the mutant strain grown in the presence of glycerol (YGly) and ethanol (YPE) is shown. As can be seen, the mutant never presented the KlAdh4p band of activity but, unex- pectedly, the aar900 mutation also affected the regulation of KlAdh3p, the second mitochondrial K. lactis alcohol dehy- drogenase activity. In fact, in cells grown on ethanol, a repressive condition for the expression of KZADH3, CMK28- 2A showed an intense band of KlAdh3p activity.

The aar900mutation is partially dominant on the expression of KlAdh3p

To verify if the aar900 mutation was dominant or recessive, we crossed the CMK28-2A mutant with CF41, a strain that naturally does not express KZADH3 (Mazzoni et aL, 1992). The latter strain, which is deleted in KZADH4 and presents only KZADHI and KZADHZ as alcohol dehydrogenase en- zymes, was chosen to avoid possible effects of KZADH3 and KZADH4 gene dosage.
