Discussion

Liver ADH is a homodimeric enzyme containing different sets of zinc atoms localized in different regions of each subunit: one at the active site bound to Cys, His and water, the other at a â€œstructuralâ€ site formed by four Cys residues in a separate loop. The existence and structure of this loop with four Cys ligands to zinc was considered to be an opportunity for investigation of the chemistry of a zinc atom at a protein site thought to be enzymatically inactive.
