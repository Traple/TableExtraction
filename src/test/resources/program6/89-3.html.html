Modeling 0f EgADH subunit. A three-dimensional model of the subunit of the EgADH was generated using the structure of the ADH of P. aeruginosa, as this was structurally close to EgADH (51% identity and 67% similarity). The E. gracilis amino acid sequence used as input in the SWISS-MODEL program started at the ATG codon positioned at nt 31 of the EgADH gene (Fig. 1); however, after the alignment analysis, the software considered as the starting N-terminus ﬁve amino acid residues before the ATG codon positioned at nt 76 (data not shown). The EgADH subunit model was obtained with NAD+ and 1,2-ethanediol (an analogue of the substrate) at the active site, as well as with the catalytic and structural Zn2+ atoms (Fig. 3A). Analysis of the EgADH model showed that residues Cys38, His6l, and Cys148, whose function is the stabilization of the catalytic Zn2+, and the Cys residues 92, 95, 98, and 1Q6, which stabilize the structural Zn”, were located at 2.11-2.39 A; this distance interval is similar to that found in the P. aeruginosa crystal (Fig. 3C). On the other hand, residue Asp196 responsible for the speciﬁcity for NAD+ in other ADHs was located at 2.64A from the 2’-hydroxyl of the ribose of the

adenosine of NAD+ (Fig. 3B). Furthero, Thr40, which interacts with the substrate, was located at 2.62A from the substrate ana- logue 1,2-ethanediol (Fig. 3C).

Protein over-expression and puriﬁcation. The 1,038-bp nu- cleotide sequence corresponding to the coding region for EgADH (from ATG codon at nt position 76) was ampliﬁed by PCR with NdeI and HindIII restriction sites at the 5 ’ and 3’ ends followed by further cloning into the pET28 plasmid. This vector allowed the expression in E. coli BL21DE3pLysS cells of a 37-kDa protein fused to a His-tag at its amino terminus (39 kDa in total). The EgADH was abundantly over-expressed under different protocols of induction (16 0C, 25 0C, or 37 OC; 1, 3, or 18 h of induction; data not shown). Although in most conditions the protein was mainly present in the cellular insoluble fraction, soluble enzyme was higher in cells cultured overnight at 16 °C after induction. A positive clone over-expressing the EgADH was selected and the protein was pu- riﬁed through metal-afﬁnity chromatography. An induced bacterial culture of 500 ml yielded 0.743 :|: 0.25 mg of active protein (n = 5) with a high degree (> 98%) of purity (data not shown).
