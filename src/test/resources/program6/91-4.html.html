To verify whether the general ethanol-repression mechan- ism was switched off, we analyzed the transcription of the KZPDCI gene, which codes for the unique pyruvate decar- boXylase activity in K. lactis and is known to be induced by glucose and repressed by ethanol (Destruelle et al., 1999). As shown in Fig. 3, KZPDCI was repressed by ethanol with the same kinetics in both wild type and mutant, indicating that the observed induction of KZADH3 by ethanol was limited to this gene and did not affect other ethanol-repressible genes.

Additional phenotypes in the aar900mutant

We wanted to verify whether the aar900 mutation could have additional effects in K. lactis. Firstly, we measured the kinetics of growth and the viability of cells cultivated in media containing ethanol as the sole carbon source. In this condition, the mutant showed a prolonged generation time of about 2h 4O min compared with 2h for the wild-type strain and, interestingly, it also showed a drop in cell viability during the stationary phase. In fact, as can be seen in Fig. 5(a), after 7 days of growth in YPE, cell viability was about 65% in the wild type and 15% in the mutant. In the latter strain, the loss of viability was not a result of the presence of KlAdh3p, in that the double mutant aar900/ Kladh3A showed similar kinetics of cell death. The high mortality rate in the CMK28-2A mutant was also conﬁrmed by growing cells on YPE plates supplemented with the vital dye phloXine B. In fact, dead or dying cells are more permeable to this dye than healthy ones and show a more intense stain (Cannon et al., 1986). As can be seen in Fig. 5(b), CMK28-2A cells showed an intense pink staining compared with the light pink colour of the wild type.
