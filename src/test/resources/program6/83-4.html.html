DISCUSSION

Among the numerous coenzyme analogues which were prepared and tested with alcohol dehydrogenase, bzilPdADl bears one of the largest substituents introduced at C-3 of the pyridinium ring. In spite of this, bzllPdADl still acts as an active coenzyme [1]. The discrimination observed between primary and secondary alcohols, prompted the idea that, with bzlPdADl, the interactions between the coenzyme and alcohols determined in part the substrate speciﬁcity. If the substrate selectivity depends upon the cofactor, the kinetic mechanism could also be affected.

The kinetic mechanism with NADl and ethanol corre- sponds to an ordered bi-bi mechanism with coenzyme binding ﬁrst [11]. The possibility that, prior to NAD l binding, ethanol occupies the substrate binding site, is not kinetically signiﬁ- cant. If an aromatic substrate is used, the ordered bi-bi mecha- nism becomes partly random [14 —16].

The model building experiments on the Graphic Display showed that the phenyl ring of bzllPdAD l bound to alcohol dehydrogenase enters the substrate binding site and makes accessibility to the active site more difﬁcult for the substrate than is the case with NADl. A rather narrow space is avail- able for the alcohol molecule and the measured distances between coenzyme analogue and protein atoms excludes the possibility for the enzyme-bzllPdADl complex to bind any secondary or aromatic alcohols.

The initial velocitystudy agreed with a sequential mecha- nism, a common feature of pyridinium-adenine-dinucleo- tide-dependent dehydrogenase. The product inhibition study showed that in the reagent concentration range, the kinetic mechanism agreed with a rapid-equilibrium random bi-bi mechanism.

The constants for coenzyme and substrate binding rep- resent then dissociation constants for the different complexes. The dissociation constants of bzsPdAD l and bzlPdADH are in the range of those of NADl and NADH [17]; thus the hydrophobic phenyl group of these analogues docs not greatly perturb the binding of the coenzyme to the enzyme. The detection of the binding of ethanol to the apoenzyme agrees with the detection of formation of binary complexes of the apoenzyme with various alcohols [18]. The cooperativity of bzilPdADH on the acetaldehyde binding is small compared to the effect of NADH on acetaldehyde binding and bzllPdAD l disfavors ethanol binding. The partial occupation of the sub- strate binding site by the large phenyl group of the coenzmye destroys the synergism between the coenzyme and substrate binding [18]. A random order for the binding of NADH and aldehyde above a certain concentration of aldehyde has been demonstrated for liver alcohol dehydrogenase [18].
