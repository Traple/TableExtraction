Kinetic data

In Table 1, the experimentally determined kinetic data for the different substrates are listed for the native Zn(II)LADH. Table 2 shows the corresponding data for the Co(II)-substi- tuted enzyme.

To allow comparison of all kinetic data, the relevant exper- iments were carried out under identical conditions. All single- turnover experiments produced pseudo-first-order rate con- stants. The rate constants evaluated from substrate concen- tration-dependent measurements of NADH consumption and production could be directly interpreted as the rate constants for the hydride-transfer step. For the ketones, this was shown by determining the primary kinetic isotope effects (Tables 1 and 2). For all ketones and alcohols investigated, all other rate constants are of such a magnitude that, in a ﬁrst approxi- mation, the rate of ternary-complex interconversion under substrate-saturating conditions is the only limiting rate [24]. The km, values obtained from the direct linear plots of sub- strate-concentration-dependent determinations, are taken as rate constants for the hydride-transfer step.

The steady-state measurements of ketone reductions (2- butanone, Z-pentanone, 3-pentanone) exhibited a biphasic be- haviour resulting in higher rates for the steady state than found for the hydride-transfer step. The ketones contain impurities which are also substrates of the enzyme and which are reduced at a much higher rate (more than one order of magnitude) than the ketones themselves. Repeated fractionated distillation reduced this faster reaction to such an extent that the following steady-state phase could be evaluated. In the corresponding single-turnover experiments, no biphasic behaviour was ob- served. Due to the time scale of the experiments and the small amplitude of the side reactions, the much faster reduction of the impurities did not interfere with the ketone reductions studied.

The solubility of secondary alcohols and ketones decreases with increasing chain length, thus 2-octanone could not be studied in the aqueous system used.

Stereoselectivity ofketone reductions

Since both the (S)-2-enantiomers and (R)-2-enantiomers of secondary alcohols are substrates of LADH, the question arose as to which selectivity criteria would govern the re- duction of the corresponding ketones.

In order to allow a kinetic interpretation of the results derived from an experimental determination of stereo- selectivity, the corresponding reaction has to be run under kinetic control. A feasible way to attain such conditions is to carry out the reactions under single-turnover conditions, similar to the corresponding stopped-flow experiments. The problem arising from this method is the very low amount of product obtained. For an accurate determination of the enantiomer composition of the alcohols produced, the analyti- cal method described in Experimental Procedures provided reliable results with less than 1 umol alcohol.
