Sterically demanding substituents such as aromatic rings were converted at decreased rates compared to their unsubsti- tuted analogs. The degree of inhibition correlated With the distance of the substituent from the reactive group.

Cyclohexane derivates were excellent substrates for TADH. The inﬂuence of the ring substitution pattern was investigated more closely (Table 2). Generally, ring substitution decreased TADH activity With the exception of the 3-position. Also, bicyclic structures such as decahydro- 2-napthol were converted at signiﬁcant rates.

In the reductive direction, cyclohexanone was chosen as the standard substrate. TADH exhibited the highest con- version rate toward aldehydes, with butyraldehyde being the best substrate (Table 3). Activity decreased toward higher chain length and bulkier substituents, especially if the bulky group was close to the ketone function (see acetophenone). Aliphatic ketones Were converted at com- parable rates.
