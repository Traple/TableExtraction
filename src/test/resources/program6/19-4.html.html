Inhibitors. The enzyme forms a ﬂuorescent ternary complex with NADH and isobutyramide, from which the active site concentration can be determined.“ The titration of the active sites apparently produces agreement with the report that the enzyme contains 2 active sites per molecule.‘ Pyrazole and NAD also form a ternary complex with the en- zyme, from which the active site concentration can be determined by spectrophotometry.“ The enzyme is inhibited by pyrazole and certain substituted pyrazoles,“ and by 8-amino-ﬁ-methoxyquinoline and a num- ber of its S-amino-substituted derivatives.“ Substrate inhibition by alco- hol is observed when the alcohol concentration is greater than 0.3 M .12
