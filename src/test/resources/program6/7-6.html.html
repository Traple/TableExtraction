These two classes can be readily differentiated by the effect of changes in solvent polarity and ionic strength on the ionization constants (27). For cationic groups, a change in solvent polarity or ionic strength has small effect. However, a decrease in solvent polarity or ionic strength lowers the ionization con- stants (and increases pK ) of neutral groups. The pK ’s of two ionizable groups involved in the esterolysis have been eval- uated from the pH-rate proﬁle to be 8.35 and higher than 10 for free ADH. Since the N ter- minus of ADH is acetylated (28), three amino acid residues, cysteine, tyrosine, and lysine, are possible candidates in this pK region (29).
