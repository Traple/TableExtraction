Enzyme assay Enzyme activity was assayed by spectropho- tometrically at 25°C by measuring the increase in the absorbance of NADH at 340 nm. The reaction was carried out with 5 umol 2-phenylethanol, 0.5 umol NAD+, and appropriate amount of the enzyme in 1 ml of 85 umol NaOH—glycine buffer (pH 10.4). One unit of enzyme activity was deﬁned as the amount that converts 1 umol of NAD+ to NADH in 1 min. The amount of protein was estimated by Bradford method (5) with BSA as the standard, using a kit from Bio-Rad Laboratories (Tokyo). The protein concentra- tion in the chromatographic procedure was monitored by the opti- cal absorbance at 280 nm.

Analytical measurements SDS—PAGE was performed by the method of Laemmli (6). The molecular mass of the subunit of the enzyme was calculated from the relative mobility of perfect protein maker 15—150 kDa (N ovagen). Proteins were stained with CBB. To estimate the molecular mass of the enzyme, the sample was subjected to SMART system (Amersham Biosciences) equip- ped with the Superose 12 gel ﬁltration column at a ﬂow rate of 40 ul/min with 10 mM buffer containing 100 mM NaCl. A calibration

curve was constructed using gel ﬁltration standard (Bio-Rad).

Measurement of optimal pH and kinetic constants To de- termine the optimal pH, the enzyme activity was examined with 10 mM of 2-phenylethanol, 0.25 mM of NAD+ (oxidation) or 10 mM of phenylacetaldehyde, 0.25 mM of NADH (reduction) in 77.5 mM buffer at various pH values. The apparent Km values were de- termined at various concentrations of alcohols or carbonyl com- pounds, and kw and Km was determined by Lineweaver—Burk plot.

Substrate speciﬁcity The relative activity was determined by comparing the initial velocity per mg of protein. The reaction mixture for oxidation reaction was composed of lmM of sub- strate, 1 mM of NAD+, 50 ul of enzyme solution, in total volume of 1 ml of NaOH—glycine buffer (pH 10.4). The reaction system for reduction reaction was composed of 0.5 mM of substrate, 0.1 mM of NADH, 50 ul of enzyme solution, in total volume of lml of K2HPO4—KH2PO4 buffer (pH 6.0).

Inhibition study The enzyme solution was pre-incubated with 1 mM concentration of metal ions and various inhibitors for 10 min. The enzyme activity of the incubation mixture was deter- mined as describe above.

Determination of optimal temperature, and thermostability The optimal temperature was determined in a range of 20—60°C. The thermostability was determined after heat treatment of the en- zyme solution for 30 min at various temperatures between 20°C and 60°C.

RESULTS

Cultivation of Brevibacterium sp. KU1309 A bac- terial strain KU1309 was isolated from soil and identiﬁed as Brevibacterium sp. The strain was grown on 2-phenyletha- nol as a sole source of carbon, and catalyzed the oxidation of 2-arylpropanols to 2-arylpropanoates. The dehydrogenase was constitutively produced intracellulary in the absence of 2-phenylethanol, although the addition of the alcohol in- creased the activity. The optimum concentration of 2-phe- nylethanol in the medium and cultivation period were 0.4% and 3 d, respectively.
