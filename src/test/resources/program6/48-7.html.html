To clarify these matters, further investigations, such as localization of ADH1 and isolation and characterization of other proteins, includ- ing the alcohol dehydrogenase involved in the degrada- tion of OPEOn, are required.

Sequence analysis of P. patida S-5 chromosomal DNA upstream of aclhl revealed two ORFs, encoding a putative acyl-CoA synthetase and a putative esterase, which can potentially form part of an operon. RT-PCR analysis indicated that aclhl is co-transcribed with the two ORFs during growth on OPEOn (Fig. 2B). Intrigu- ingly, an operon comprising an alcohol dehydrogenase and an esterase gene from Rhodococcas ruber SC134) or an acyl-CoA synthetase gene from P. patida P132) have been reported. They contribute to the biodegradation of cyclododecanone and alkane respectively, but the structure of these operons differed from that of P. putida S-5. Furthermore, the biodegradation pathway for cyclo- dodecanone and alkane is quite different from the pathway proposed by Sato et al. 1142) for OPEOn. We are currently analyzing the aclhl operon in order to under- stand the role of the putative acyl-CoA synthetase and esterase genes in the biodegradation of OPEOH.
