Results Preparation of TADH and catalytic properties

Production of TADH was performed in shake ﬂasks by cultivating E. coli BL21 (DE3) pLysS pASZ2 in TB medium. TADH was purified by direct heat treatment of cells resulting in a technical pure TADH preparation (65%). Using anion exchange chromatography, TADH was further purified to 95% apparent purity (Fig. 1 and Table 1). On a native PAGE subjected to a dehydrogenase-speciﬁc activity stain, only one single band appeared (Fig. 1), indicating that no competing activities were present in this enzyme preparation after heat treatment. Optimal conditions for TADH were found at 60°C as a compromise between activity and stability (Hollmann et al. 2005). Like most ADHs, TADH exhibits different pH optima for oxidation and reduction reactions. Using several buffer systems, the optimal pH for reduction was found at a pH of 6, whereas the activity in the oxidative direction was highest at a pH of 9 (Fig. 2). Substrate spectrum of TADH

In the oxidative direction, cyclohexanol was chosen as standard substrate and reference. TADH exhibits a clear preference for primary alcohols and corresponding alde- hydes for aliphatic substrates. In the oxidative direction, activity steeply increased with chain length until l-propanol and then decreased slightly again with growing chain length (Table 2).
