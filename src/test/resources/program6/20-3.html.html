The experimental re- sults agree with the hypothesis of a four co-ordinated pseudo-tetrahedral site for copper, i.e. copper may be complexed in the same co-ordination site as zinc in native ADH. The g-values and anisotropy (presented in Table 3) of Cu-ADH are not very different from those of typical blue copper proteins such as Plastocyanin and Stellacyanin, in which copper is four co-ordinated to histidines and cysteines [22,23]. As a consequence, it may be reasonable to suggest that copper substitutes zinc in the very same co-ordination site and that it is bound to the same ligands (two cysteines and one histidine).

Experimental results obtained from Raman spec- troscopy conﬁrm this last hypothesis founded upon EPR. Fig. 3 shows two differential Raman spectra obtained, respectively, by subtracting the native ADH spectrum from that of Cu-ADH (upper dotted curve) and by subtracting the histidine spectrum from that of the complex between histidine and copper (lower solid curve) in the range between 200 and 500 cm‘ 1. In the hypothesis that copper is bound by the same ligands than zinc in native ADH, we expected histidine and cysteine complexes to be formed. Since the cysteine has the tendency to oxidise itself to cystine, it is very difﬁcult to obtain reliable data in proper conditions to compare the spectrum of the cysteine—copper complex to that of the Cu-ADH. As can be seen in Fig. 3, the differential spectra allow us to decrease the complexity of the spectra and to highlight the interesting signals due to the presence of copper. Despite this, the spectrum of the enzyme shows a complex proﬁle, due to the very high number of signals, compared with the well-deﬁned peaks of the copper— histidine spectrum. Comparing the main bands ob- served for Cu-ADH with those reported in the literature for Stellacyanin and Plastocyanin [24] and summarised in Table 4, some assignment can be sug- gested. The signals corresponding to 392, 408 (409) and 434 cm-l seems to be correlated with those of Stella- cyanin and Plastocyanin which are assigned to the stretching of the Cu-N. This assignment may be conﬁ- rmed by the fact that the same bands are also present in Cu-His Raman spectrum even though they have a different intensity. On the contrary, the strong band at about 270 cm-l that is observed in the Cu-ADH spectrum but is absent in the Cu-His spectrum, may be associated with the Cu-S vibrational mode [25,26], as well as the band at about 344 cm-l (medium intensity), which can be related to the 350 cm-l band of Stella- cyanin Cu-S stretching mode according to literature [24].
