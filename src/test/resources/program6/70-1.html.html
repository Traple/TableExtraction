To determine whether NAD* and acetone are bound to Adh-l or not, the Adhs alleloenzyme from Drosophila melanogaster was converted into the Adh-l form (Fig. 1). The converted enzyme was then dialysed against three changes of 0.1 M-Tris/HCI buffer, pH 8.6, resulting in a l0”-fold dilution of unbound NAD* and acetone. The Adh-l form was not reverted back into Adh-5 by dialysis. This indicates that an irreversible change has occurred with Adh-l. The absorption spectrum in Fig. 2 shows that the 280 nm absorption peak of the native Adhs alleloenzyme has changed to 273 nm in the converted enzyme. This is as expected for an enzyme- coenzyme complex. Fig. 2 also shows that the converted enzyme has an additional absorption peak at 328 nm. This indicates the formation of a reduced nicotinamide compound, in this case most probably an NAD(H)— acetone complex irreversibly bound to the enzyme.
