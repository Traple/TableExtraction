tBOOH Inhibition 0f Yeast ADH

We wondered if the stimulation of ADH by tBOOH was speciﬁc for the horse liver enzyme. The enzymatic activities of HLADH and YADH are absolutely depen- dent on the integrity of the zinc coordination sphere of the catalytic site. In contrast to the stimulation of HLADH by tBOOH, incubation of YADH with 0.5—50 mM tBOOH caused progressive inactivation of the en- zyme (Fig. 5). A plot of log of the percentage of residual YADH activity vs time of incubation with tBOOH (Fig. 6a) is linear to about 10% of the initial activity. This indicates that the inactivation reaction approximates ﬁrst-order kinetics with respect to enzyme at any ﬁxed concentration of tBOOH, which is reminiscent of the decay in ADH activity caused by hydrogen peroxide (5).
