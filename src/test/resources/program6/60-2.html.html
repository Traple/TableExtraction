Protein concentration was measured by the method of Lowry et al. [12] using bovine serum albumin as the standard (Sigma Diagnostics, St Louis, MO, USA). The assays were performed on a Shimadzu UV/VIS 1202 spectrophotometer.

Statistical analysis

Preliminary statistical analysis (chi-squared test) revealed that the distribution of ADH and ALDH activities did not follow a normal distribution. Consequently, the Wilcoxon test was used for statistical analysis. Data were presented as median, range, and mean values. Statistically signiﬁcant differences were deﬁned as comparisons resulting in P<O%.

Results

The activities of total ADH, ALDH, and ADH isoenzymes in liver cancer cells are listed in Table 2. We have shown that ADH and ALDH activities are present in cancer cells, although ALDH activity is much lower than ADH in all patients groups. Comparison of the ADH isoenzymes activities showed that class III has the highest activity. The median activity of this class was 6.26 nmol/min/mg protein in cancer tissue and 5.64 nmol/min/mg protein in healthy tissue in the total patients group.
