Unfortunately, CMK9, the original mutant that we iso- lated, also carried the ragZ mutation that inactivated the phosphoglucose isomerase gene (PGI1). As a consequence, this strain was unable to grow on ethanol and could not be used for the isolation of the wild-type gene on this carbon source by direct complementation of the aar900 mutation. Moreover, because aar900 has a semi-dominant effect, the attempts to isolate the cognate gene by negative selection following the recovery of the other phenotypes have had no success up to now.

On the other side, the chromosomal region where the mutation was mapped is large and contains many genes that could be related to the observed phenotypes. One should keep in mind that in this region are also present ORFs with no obvious homology to genes of other organisms and which represent peculiar K. lactis genes.

We cannot exclude the possibility that small chromoso- mal rearrangements occurred in the original mutant during the selection on allyl alcohol and then were transferred by crosses to the CMK28-2A. This possibility could explain the pleiotropic phenotype observed in this mutant.

A more detailed physical map of this region of chromo- some III in the wild type and in the mutant, as well as a tight genetic correlation between the aar900 with all the observed phenotypes, could help us to understand the nature of this mutation.
