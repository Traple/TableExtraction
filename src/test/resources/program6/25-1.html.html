The assay of AdhE activities includes the Aldh activity and the Adh activity. The Aldh activity was assayed spectrophotometrically by measuring the decrease in absorbance at 340 nm following the oxidation of NADH to NAD in a solution of Tris—Cl buffer (pH 7.6), 5mM DTT, 0.2 mM NADH, and 0.1 mM acetyl-CoA at 65 OC. The Adh activity was measured identically with the substitution of 10 mM acetaldehyde for acetyl-CoA in the buffer solution. One unit of enzyme activity was deﬁned as that which consumes 1 umol of NADH per minute. Values are expressed as means of at least three independent experiments.

3. Results and conclusions

The putative adh gene of T. ethanolicus JW200 encodes a 874-aa protein (GenBank accession no. DQ836061). Homology comparisons show 97% identity to the T ethanolicus ATCC 33223 putative Adh, and 53% identity to the Entamoeba histolytica AdhE [8] (BLAST search). Enzyme puriﬁcation was effectively achieved through two steps as described in Section 2. The puriﬁed putative Adh protein exhibited a molecular mass of 100 kDa based on SDS—PAGE migration (Fig. 1, lane 4). Analysis of the puriﬁed Adh protein demonstrated that the enzyme exhibited Aldh activity (1 1.0103 U/mg) and Adh activity (2.6 i 0.2 U/mg). The result conﬁrmed that the Adh is a bifunctional AdhE. These data suggest that the AdhE functions predominantly in acetyl-CoA cleavage to acet- aldehyde during sugar catabolism. AdhE has been reported to have pyruvate formate-lyase (PF L)-deactivase activity in E. coli [6]. In contrast to the results, Nnyepi et al. recently declared that the AdhE did not have an effect on the activated PF L [9]. AdhE activities of T ethanolicus JW200 are similar to that of anaerobic C. acetobutylicum. Both AdhEs exhibit high Aldh activity and low Adh activity [5]. The fusion of an acetyl-CoA reduction and an acetalde- hyde reduction probably accelerated the successive reduc- tion of acetyl-CoA to ethanol by bringing the two active sites into close proximity, as a result, the steady-state level of acetaldehyde, a toxic intermediate, could probably be lowered [10].
