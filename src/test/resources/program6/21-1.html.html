The immobilization of alcohol dehydrogenase on vari- ous supports has been investigated by several researchers [13,20—24]. Its immobilization on magnetite nanoparticles was ﬁrst reported by Shinkai et al. [23], but the residual activity was relatively low (12%). Then binding of alcohol dehydroge- nase to magnetite nanoparticles via carbodiimide activation was reported by Chen and Liao [13,24], the kinetic measurements indicated the bound yeast dehydrogenase retained 62% of its original activity and exhibited a 10-fold improved stability than did the free enzyme using 2-butanone as substrate.

2. Experimental 2.1. Chemicals

Strains (S. cerevisiae, strain No. 3) used in this Work were obtained from our culture collection and alcohol dehydroge- nase (EC1.1.1.1) from S. cerevisiae was prepared as described Section 2.2. Phenylglyoxylic acid was purchased from Sigma Chemical Co. Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide, reduced form (NADH) was procured from Roche Ltd. Chitosan (CTS, MW 4.9 >< 105, degree of deacetylation 95%) Was procured from Dalian Xindie Chitin Co. (Dalian, China). 25% Glutaraldehyde solution was purchased from Hunan Normal University Chem- ical Co. (Hunan, China). Ferrous sulphates heptahydrate and aqueous ammonia solution were purchased from Tianjin No. 3 Chemical Plant (Tianjin, China). Other chemicals were the ana- lytic grade reagents commercially available and used Without further puriﬁcation. All solutions were prepared with distilled, deionized Water.

2.2. Cultivation and puriﬁcation of S CAD from Saccharomyces cerevisiae

S. cerevisiae was cultivated in Rough bottles in culture medium (g/l): glucose 50, peptone 3.0, yeast extract 2.5, K2HPO4 1.0, MgSO4-7H2O 0.5, NaCl 0.5, Fe2(SO4)3 0.01, ZnSO4 0.01, pH 6.5. The cells were incubated at 30 0C and harvested after 24 h. Then the cells were collected from cul- ture by centrifugation at 6687.5 >< g at 4°C for 15min.
