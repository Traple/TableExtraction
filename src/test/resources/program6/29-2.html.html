Molecular mass and N-terminal amino acid sequence Molecular mass of PEDH was estimated by analytical HPLC on gel ﬁltration column chromatography to be 29 kDa. The molecular weight under denaturing condition was decided to be 39 kDa by SDS-PAGE (Fig. 2). These results suggested that the puriﬁed enzyme is a monomeric protein. The N-ter- minal amino acid sequence of PEDH was determined to be MKASLATAIGGEFTVHD. It was revealed that there are no similar proteins by search of the sequence in database. Thus, the family of this enzyme was very interesting.

Effect of pH and temperature on the activity The activity of PEDH was measured at various pH values. The pH-activity proﬁle is shown in Fig. 3. The maximum activity of 2-phenylethanol oxidation was shown at pH 10.4. In con- trast, the enzyme exhibited the maximum activity of phenyl- acetaldehyde reduction at pH 6.0.

As is clear from the date in Fig. 4, no signiﬁcant loss of the enzyme activity was observed after heat treatment for 30 min at 35°C, while about 50% of the initial activity was lost after heat treatment at 50°C. The optimal reaction tem- perature for the enzyme was estimated to be around 60°C. Further heat treatment above 60°C caused a rapid decrease in the enzyme activity.

Kinetic properties and substrate speciﬁcity The Michaelis-Menten kinetic parameters were shown in Table 2-4. Compared to the kcat/Km value of NAD+, the value of NADH was about 10-fold larger (Table 2). As a result, the reduction of aldehyde proceeded faster than oxidation of alcohol.
