In similar studies on the effect of or- ganic solvents on enzymatic catalysis [l4,28], threshold concentrations were introduced, and deﬁned as values at which half inactivation (C50) is attained [14]. Based on this deﬁnition, the C50 observed here was approximately 25% (Fig. 6). Concomitant with lowering of catalytic activity, intrinsic ﬂuorescence intensity of the enzyme was found to increase with a slight blue shift of 2-3 nm. Furthermore, decrease of catalytic activity and ﬂuorescence enhancement were found to have similar dependencies on solvent concentration

(Fig. 6). These results may be taken to suggest that conformational changes may occur in the protein molecule when placed in environments of relatively higher dioxane concentrations resulting in ﬂuorescence enhancement and lower catalytic activities. Similar results were obtained on the effect of organic solvents on chymotrypsin [28].

In conclusion, results presented here clearly in- dicate that it is possible to provide a mesophilic protein with thermostabilities higher than that encountered for its thermophilic counterpart by substituting water with a hydrophobic environ- ment. Also, in selecting an appropriate solvent for an enzyme—catalyzed reaction, some speciﬁc de- tails should be considered in addition to the gen- eral rules outlined in the literature.
